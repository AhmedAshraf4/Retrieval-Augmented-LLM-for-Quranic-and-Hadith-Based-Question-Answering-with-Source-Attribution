Allah gave Moses the Torah, but the Children of Israel altered its meanings and defied its commands, so Allah sent prophets after Moses who followed his law. He then sent Jesus, son of Mary, with some rulings that differed from parts of the Torah, and supported him with clear signs: reviving the dead, forming living birds from clay by Allah’s permission, healing the sick, and informing of unseen matters. Allah strengthened Jesus with the Holy Spirit, identified as Gabriel, and these signs confirmed the truth of his message. The Children of Israel rejected him out of envy and refusal to accept any change to the Torah, even though he came to make lawful some of what had been forbidden. This pattern continued whenever a messenger brought commands opposing their desires, leading them to deny some prophets and kill others because the prophets upheld divine law against their altered practices.
