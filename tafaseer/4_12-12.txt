A husband inherits half of his deceased wife’s estate if she has no children, and one-fourth if she has children, after paying off any debts and legacies. The wives share one-fourth or one-eighth of the inheritance if there is more than one wife. Kalalah refers to a person who has neither descendants nor ascendants, meaning no children or parents. If a deceased person’s estate is inherited under Kalalah, and there are half-siblings from the mother’s side, each receives a sixth, but if there are more than two, they share a third. The estate must be divided fairly, ensuring no harm to the heirs, and no will can be made that alters the fixed portions for rightful heirs, as Allah has already determined the due rights for each.
