The divorced woman, whose marriage was consummated and who still menstruates, must wait for three menstrual periods (Quru') before she can remarry. The Quru' refers to the menstruation period, a ruling established by the companions of the Prophet Muhammad, such as Umar ibn al-Khattab and Ibn Mas'ud. It is not lawful for women to conceal their pregnancy or menstruation status. If they do, it is considered deceitful. During the `Iddah, the husband has the right to take back his wife if his intention is reconciliation. This ruling applied before the limit of three divorces was set, which later made a final divorce irreversible. Both spouses have mutual rights: the wife has the right to be provided for, and the husband has a degree of responsibility over her. Men are considered more responsible due to their physical, financial, and societal roles. Women should not allow anyone their husband dislikes to sit on their mat, and if they do, they can be disciplined gently. Both spouses are expected to respect each other's needs and rights within reasonable limits, as established in the guidance provided by the Prophet Muhammad.
