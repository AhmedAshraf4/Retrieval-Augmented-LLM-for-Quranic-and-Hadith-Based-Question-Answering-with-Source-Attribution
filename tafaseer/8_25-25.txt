Tafsīr Explanation: Allah warns the believers of a Fitnah, a trial that affects not only the wrongdoers but also those around them if the sins are not stopped. This includes the entire community, as failure to prevent evil can lead to collective punishment. Imam Ahmad recorded a narration where the Companions did not initially realize that the warning applied to them, believing it only concerned the wrongdoers. Ibn Abbas explained that this Fitnah would affect everyone if the evil is not curbed, and the righteous would also suffer. This is further emphasized in hadiths, where the Prophet Muhammad said that if the righteous fail to enjoin good and forbid evil, Allah will send a punishment that will affect all, including the righteous. In another hadith, the Prophet likened the situation to people on a boat, where if some decide to make a hole in their part to benefit themselves, everyone will be doomed unless they prevent it. The Prophet also warned that when sins become widespread, the punishment of Allah will encompass all, even if there are righteous people among them.
