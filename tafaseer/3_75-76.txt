Allah describes among the Jews some who, if entrusted with a great amount of wealth, would return it without hesitation, while others, even if entrusted with a single silver coin, would not repay it unless constantly pursued. This illustrates the disparity in their trustworthiness. The reason for their deceit is their belief that it is permissible to take the property of the Arabs, whom they regard as "unlettered," without sin. Allah condemns this false claim, stating they lie against Him knowingly. Allah asserts that they would not be allowed such property unless it was rightfully theirs. Allah then reminds the People of the Book that those who fulfill their covenant with Him, fear Him, and adhere to the Shari`ah will be loved by Allah.
