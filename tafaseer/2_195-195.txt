Tafsir Explanation: The command to spend in the cause of Allah is highlighted in the verse, emphasizing the importance of contributing towards Allah's cause and not neglecting this duty. The phrase "do not throw yourselves into destruction" refers specifically to abandoning Jihad and returning to personal affairs, as understood by the Ansar after the establishment of Islam. They were tempted to focus on their estates and families once Islam had become victorious, but this verse was revealed to prevent such neglect. Abu Ayyub Al-Ansari clarified that the verse was meant for them, the Companions of the Prophet, to prevent them from retreating into personal comfort and away from continuing their support for Islam. The verse also advises against refraining from spending for the sake of Allah, especially in the context of supporting the Muslim community in warfare, as such avoidance leads to destruction. The verse calls for excellence in acts of obedience, as those who do good are loved by Allah.
