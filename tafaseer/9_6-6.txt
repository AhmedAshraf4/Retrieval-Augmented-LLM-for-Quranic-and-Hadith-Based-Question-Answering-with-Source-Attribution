When idolators request safe passage, Muslims are commanded to grant it, escorting them until they reach a secure place after hearing the Qur'an. This applies to those coming from enemy lands seeking to learn about Islam. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) granted safe passage to emissaries like Urwah bin Masud, Mikraz bin Hafs, and Suhayl bin `Amr, who witnessed the respect Muslims showed him, which led many to embrace Islam. On the other hand, when Musaylimah's emissary claimed him to be a messenger of Allah, the Prophet threatened to kill him but spared him due to the sanctity of emissaries. Such requests for safe passage were granted to those coming for business, peace treaties, or negotiations, allowing them to return to their homeland safely after hearing the message of Islam.
