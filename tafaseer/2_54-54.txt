Moses told the Children of Israel that they had wronged themselves by worshipping the calf and commanded them to repent to their Creator, emphasizing the gravity of associating others with the One who created them. Their repentance was not verbal but required a concrete act: they were ordered to kill one another. Those who had worshipped the calf sat down, and those who had not stood with knives and killed them without distinction between relatives. A darkness descended while the killing took place, and when it lifted, seventy thousand had been killed. Both those who carried out the killing and those who were killed were forgiven by God, and this act of mutual killing was accepted as their repentance, after which God turned to them in forgiveness, as He accepts repentance and is merciful.
