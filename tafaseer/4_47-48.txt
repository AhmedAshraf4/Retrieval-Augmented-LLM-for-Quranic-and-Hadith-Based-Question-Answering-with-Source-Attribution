Allah commands the People of the Book to believe in what He revealed to Muhammad, which confirms the messages they already have about him. He warns that if they do not, He may efface their faces and turn them backwards, making them walk in reverse, symbolizing the severity of ignoring the truth and turning to misguidance. This punishment mirrors the deviation of those who refuse guidance, as described by the parable of the iron collars around the necks. Upon hearing this warning, Ka'b al-Ahbar embraced Islam, fearing the fulfillment of this threat. Allah also declares that He does not forgive shirk (associating partners with Him) unless the person repents, but He forgives other sins as He wills. Associating partners with Allah is described as a tremendous sin, the greatest wrong one can commit, as it involves setting rivals to the Creator.
