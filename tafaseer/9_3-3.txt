This declaration from Allah and His Messenger was made on the greatest day of Hajj, the Day of Sacrifice, marking the culmination of the Hajj rituals with the largest gathering. Allah and His Messenger are free from any obligations to the idolaters, and the idolaters are invited to repent from their polytheism, which is better for them. If they refuse to repent, they cannot escape Allah's power and will, and they are promised a painful punishment. During this Hajj, Abu Bakr was sent with the message that no idolater would be allowed to perform Hajj after that year, nor would anyone be allowed to perform Tawaf while naked. This message was later reiterated by Ali ibn Abi Talib, who was tasked with proclaiming the declaration of innocence at Mina on the Day of Sacrifice, ensuring no idolater could perform Hajj from then on. The declaration also stated that anyone with a covenant with Allah’s Messenger would have it honored until its expiration.
