Those described are the people who have faith in the unseen, meaning they affirm and trust in what cannot be seen, including Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, resurrection after death, Paradise, Hellfire, and meeting Allah, and this faith is not limited to belief alone but is realized through speech and action. Faith here includes inner conviction, verbal affirmation, and practical obedience, and it necessarily involves fear of Allah that leads to compliance. They establish prayer by performing it consistently and correctly, preserving its times, purification, bowing, prostration, recitation, humility, and required acts. They also spend from what Allah has provided them, which includes obligatory charity from wealth and spending on those under their care and others in need, according to what Allah has granted them. Faith, prayer, and spending are linked together as outward actions that confirm inward belief, and believing without seeing is treated as a complete and superior form of faith because it relies on trust in revelation alone.
