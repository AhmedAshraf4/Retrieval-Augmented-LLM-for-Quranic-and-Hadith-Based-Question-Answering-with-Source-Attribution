Allah commands frequent remembrance of Him, especially in the mornings and afternoons, emphasizing worship at these times. Before the Isra' night and the institution of the five daily prayers, this instruction was revealed in Makkah. The remembrance should be done humbly and in secret, avoiding loudness in words. When the Companions questioned whether they should raise their voices or speak in secret, Allah revealed that He is close, responding to the supplications of those who call on Him. The Prophet also reminded the people to avoid raising their voices excessively, as Allah is near and hears all. These instructions encourage constant Dhikr, particularly during these times, so that believers are not negligent in remembering Allah. Allah also praises the angels who worship Him tirelessly, setting an example for humans to follow in their devotion. Prostration is permitted upon hearing the angels' prostration to Allah, and the Hadith encourages Muslims to align in prayer as the angels do. This was the first instance in the Qur'an where prostration upon hearing the recitation was legitimized.
