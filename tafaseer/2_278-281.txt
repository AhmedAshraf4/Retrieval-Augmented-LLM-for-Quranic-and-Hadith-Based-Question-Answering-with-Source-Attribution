Allah commands the believers to have Taqwa, meaning they should fear Him and be conscious of His awareness over all their actions. He instructs them to abandon any remaining Riba (interest) from past transactions, especially when they truly believe in His command to prohibit it. The Ayah was revealed concerning two tribes, Bani Amr bin Umayr and Bani Al-Mughirah, who were involved in Riba transactions from the pre-Islamic era. After both tribes embraced Islam, one tribe demanded repayment of the Riba, but the other refused, citing Islam’s prohibition. The Prophet conveyed Allah’s command to cease dealing with Riba, warning that those who continued would face war from Allah and His Messenger. If the debtors repent, they are entitled to their original capital, without any increase from Riba. Allah also advises kindness towards debtors who face financial hardship, offering them time to repay or forgiving the debt entirely, which will result in greater reward. The Prophet emphasized the great reward for those who ease the burden of debtors. Ultimately, believers are reminded of the Day of Judgment when everyone will be held accountable for their deeds, and no one will be wronged. This was the last verse revealed in the Qur'an, stressing the importance of Taqwa and the certainty of the Hereafter.
