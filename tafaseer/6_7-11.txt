The disbelievers rejected the truth, even if they were shown clear signs like a Message descending before their eyes on paper or a gate opening from the heavens. They would still deny and claim it was magic or deception. Allah explains that if He had sent an angel, it would have led to immediate judgment, and no respite would be given to them. If the angel were to appear as a man, it would only confuse them further, just as they were confused by human Messengers. Allah’s wisdom in sending human Messengers is that people can relate to them, ask questions, and benefit from their teachings. Those who mocked earlier Messengers faced the consequences of their disbelief, as torment overtook them. Allah advises the disbelievers to travel the earth and reflect on the fate of those who denied truth before them, who suffered punishments in this life and the Hereafter, while His Messengers and believers were saved.
