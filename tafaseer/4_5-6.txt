Tafsir Explanation: Allah prohibits giving the unwise control over property, which includes young people, those with mental impairments, or those lacking sound judgment. This ruling also applies in cases of bankruptcy when debts exceed assets. The property of orphans should be held by their guardians until they reach maturity, which is typically marked by puberty or the age of fifteen. Once an orphan shows maturity in both religion and financial matters, their property can be returned to them, and guardians must ensure it is not spent wastefully, especially out of fear the orphan will grow up. If the guardian is rich, they should not take compensation from the orphan’s wealth, but if they are poor, they may take what is just. Guardians are required to release the property in the presence of witnesses once the orphan reaches adulthood and exhibits wisdom, and Allah is the ultimate reckoner over the process.
