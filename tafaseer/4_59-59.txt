Tafsir Explanation: The verse emphasizes the obligation of obeying those in authority, as long as their commands align with righteousness. This was exemplified when the Prophet Muhammad sent Abdullah bin Hudhafah on a military expedition, illustrating that obedience to authority was critical unless the command was sinful. A notable incident involved a leader who ordered his troops into a fire, and a young man reminded them that obedience to the leader should be in line with the Messenger’s teachings. The Prophet affirmed that obedience should only be to righteousness, not to acts of disobedience to Allah. The verse also highlights that when disputes arise, believers must refer to the Qur'an and the Sunnah for resolution, showing that true belief involves following these sources in all matters. Those who fail to do so are not considered true believers. Referring to the Qur'an and the Sunnah ensures a better outcome and a more suitable final judgment.
