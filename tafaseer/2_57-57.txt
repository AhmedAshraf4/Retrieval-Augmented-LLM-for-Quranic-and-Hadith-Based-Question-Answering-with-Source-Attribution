God shaded the Children of Israel with white clouds during their wandering in the desert, protecting them from the heat. He sent down manna, which descended daily and was eaten the same day because it spoiled if kept overnight; only before the Sabbath they gathered enough for two days, since no work was done then. He also provided quail, birds that could be caught each day in sufficient amount, with the same rule of daily use and double provision before the Sabbath. They were commanded to eat the good and lawful food provided to them. Their later disobedience did not harm God; it harmed themselves through rebellion after witnessing these clear provisions and miracles.
