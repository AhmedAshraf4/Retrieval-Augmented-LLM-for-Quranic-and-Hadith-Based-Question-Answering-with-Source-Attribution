Tafsir Explanation: The Arabs before Islam falsely prohibited certain cattle, designating them as Bahirah, Sa'ibah, Wasilah, and Ham, and claimed these prohibitions were divinely ordained. Allah, however, refutes these innovations by stating that He created all cattle—male and female sheep, goats, camels, and cows—for the benefit of humans, providing them with food, milk, transportation, and other benefits. Allah challenges their claim, asking them to provide proof of His prohibition on such cattle. He mocks their false practices, asking if they were present when Allah decreed these prohibitions. He declares that no one is more unjust than those who invent lies about Allah to mislead others. The person most responsible for these innovations was `Amr bin Luhay, who altered the religion of the Prophets and introduced these false prohibitions.
