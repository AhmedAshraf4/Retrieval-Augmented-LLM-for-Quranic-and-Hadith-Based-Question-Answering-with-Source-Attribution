The story of Habil and Qabil, the two sons of Adam, illustrates the destructive consequences of envy, injustice, and transgression. Qabil, driven by envy over Habil's accepted sacrifice, killed him. Habil, in contrast, accepted his brother's threat without retaliation, stating that he feared Allah. Qabil’s murder was prompted by his conscience, which made the act seem justifiable, but this decision led to his eternal loss. Allah sent a crow to show Qabil how to bury his brother, prompting Qabil to regret his actions. Habil’s sacrifice was accepted because of his piety, while Qabil’s was rejected. The narrative emphasizes the importance of righteousness and the severe consequences of wrongdoing, marking Qabil as the first to establish murder.
