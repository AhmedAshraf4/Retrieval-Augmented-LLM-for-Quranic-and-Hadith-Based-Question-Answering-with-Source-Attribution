The victory at Hunayn showed that Allah grants success through His aid, not by numbers or supplies. When the Muslims, prideful of their large army, were ambushed by the Huwazin, they initially fled in retreat, leaving only a small group with the Prophet Muhammad. Despite this, the Prophet remained steadfast, declaring his identity and calling on the Companions to return. His uncle, Al-‘Abbas, called the Muhajirin and Ansar to rally, and gradually, the Muslims regrouped. The Prophet then threw a handful of sand, causing confusion among the enemy, which allowed the Muslims to defeat them. Allah sent tranquility upon the Prophet and the remaining believers, along with unseen forces, likely angels. The Huwazin were later forgiven and embraced Islam, and the Prophet gave them a choice between taking their prisoners or their lost spoils of war, which they chose the former. He released six thousand prisoners and distributed the spoils to win the hearts of the newly converted, including generous gifts to Malik bin ‘Awf and others.
