Tafsir Explanation: Allah reminded Isa of the many favors He granted him, including his miraculous birth from Maryam without male intervention, making him a clear sign of Allah's power. Allah also supported him with the angel Jibril, enabling him to speak in the cradle and testify to his mother's chastity. He was taught the Book, wisdom, and the Tawrah, and performed miracles like creating a bird from clay, healing the blind and lepers, and raising the dead, all by Allah's permission. When the Children of Israel rejected him and accused him of magic, Allah protected him, raising him to Himself and purifying him from harm. Allah also inspired his disciples, the Hawariyyun, to believe in him and his message. When they requested a table spread with food from the sky, Isa prayed to Allah, who granted their request but warned that anyone who rejected afterward would face severe punishment.
