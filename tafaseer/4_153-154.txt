Tafsir Explanation: The Jews demanded that a book be sent to them from heaven, similar to how the Torah was revealed to Moses, showing their stubbornness and disbelief. This request was driven by their defiance, mirroring the demands made by the Quraysh to Muhammad. Despite witnessing numerous miracles, including the drowning of Pharaoh's army, the Jews later worshiped the calf, defying Moses' teachings. They asked Moses to make for them a god like other nations had, showing their lack of faith. After Moses' return, they were commanded to kill those who worshipped the calf for their repentance, and those who remained obedient did so. Allah forgave them and granted Moses clear authority. When they refused to follow the Torah, Allah raised a mountain above their heads, threatening them, and they prostrated in fear. They were also ordered to enter Jerusalem in a humble manner but did so in defiance, and Allah commanded them not to break the Sabbath, which they violated. A strong covenant was taken from them, but they continually rebelled, breaking their promises, denying God's signs, and killing prophets unjustly. They falsely claimed to have killed Jesus, but Allah raised him to Himself, and the matter remains uncertain among those who dispute it.
