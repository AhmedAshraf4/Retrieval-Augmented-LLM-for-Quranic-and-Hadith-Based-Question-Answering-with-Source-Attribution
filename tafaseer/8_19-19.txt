The disbelievers, led by Abu Jahl, called upon Allah for a judgment on the Day of Badr, asking for victory between the two camps, the pagans and the Muslims. They invoked Allah to decide between them, saying that whoever severed the bonds of kinship and introduced something unfamiliar should be destroyed. Allah responded by granting the judgment they requested, leading to the victory of the Muslims. The disbelievers had also prayed for victory before leaving Makkah, holding onto the Ka'bah's curtains, seeking to determine which group was more honorable. Allah's answer was clear: Muhammad and his followers achieved victory, proving that Allah stands with the believers. The verse also emphasizes that if the disbelievers cease their disbelief and reject their opposition to Allah and His Messenger, it will be better for them. However, if they return to their rejection, Allah will bring about their defeat again, no matter how large their forces are. The verse underscores that those whom Allah supports, the believers, cannot be defeated.
