The verse presents the example of a wealthy man who initially works to please Allah, but later allows Satan to lead him into disobedience, which nullifies his good deeds. In his old age, while his children are too weak to care for him, a destructive whirlwind strikes his garden, burning its fruits and destroying its trees. This symbolizes the disbeliever on the Day of Resurrection, who, having lost his good deeds due to his past misdeeds, finds himself without any means of refuge or excuse before Allah. Just as the man cannot regrow his garden due to his weakened state and lack of help from his children, the disbeliever will be deprived of any good deeds when he most needs them. The parable emphasizes the importance of maintaining righteous deeds and warns of the consequences of turning to evil, especially when one’s situation becomes desperate.
