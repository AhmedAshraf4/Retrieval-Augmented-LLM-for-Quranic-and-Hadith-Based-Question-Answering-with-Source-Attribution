Tafsir Explanation: If a man cannot afford to marry a free believing woman, he is allowed to marry a believing slave girl. However, the marriage requires the permission of the slave girl's owner, who is also responsible for the marriage of male slaves. The dowry must be given to the slave girl in a good manner, without belittling her status as a slave. The slave girl must be chaste and not engage in illicit sexual relations. If a married slave girl commits adultery, her punishment is half of that of a free unmarried woman, with lashes rather than stoning. This provision is for those who fear harm to their religion or chastity but it is better to practice patience and refrain from marrying slave girls, as it is more virtuous.
