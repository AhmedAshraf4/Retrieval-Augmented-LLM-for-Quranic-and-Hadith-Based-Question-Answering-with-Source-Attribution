Tafsir Explanation: Allah commanded the Children of Israel to uphold their covenant with Him, which included obedience to His Messenger and support for the truth. He had taken a pledge from twelve leaders of their tribes, and similarly, the Prophet Muhammad took a pledge from twelve leaders of the Ansar, representing their tribes in the allegiance of `Aqabah. Allah promised to forgive their sins and grant them Paradise if they upheld the covenant, performing prayers, giving charity, believing in His messengers, supporting them, and spending in His cause. However, those who broke this covenant went astray, and as a result, Allah cursed them, hardened their hearts, and led them to distort His words. They abandoned parts of the message and plotted against the Prophet Muhammad and his companions. The Christians, too, broke their covenant by failing to follow the Prophet and the true message, leading to enmity and division among their sects. Allah will inform them of their actions on the Day of Resurrection, condemning their false beliefs, especially their attribution of a son to Allah.
