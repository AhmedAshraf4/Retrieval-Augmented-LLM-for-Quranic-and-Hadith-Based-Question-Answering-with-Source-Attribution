Tafsir Explanation: Hud's people rejected his call to worship Allah alone, continuing to worship idols like Suda', Samud, and Al-Haba'. When Hud warned them of the wrath and scorn from Allah, they disputed over these idols, which they and their ancestors had made. Hud challenged them, asking if they had any proof or authority from Allah for their idol worship. Despite his warning, they refused to believe in him and accused him of madness, claiming their gods had seized him with evil. Hud trusted Allah, declaring his innocence and awaiting Allah’s judgment. Allah then saved Hud and his followers from the destruction of their people, severing the roots of those who denied His signs. The people of ‘Ad, who had once been powerful and rebellious, were destroyed by a violent wind sent by Allah, which lifted them into the air and shattered them. Hud had repeatedly warned them against their arrogance and corruption, but they persisted in their defiance until their complete annihilation.
