Allah commands the believers to obey Him and His Messenger and warns them against disobedience and imitating the disbelievers who reject Him. He forbids turning away from His Messenger, emphasizing that hearing the truth is not enough unless it is followed by action. Those who pretend to hear but fail to obey are compared to the most wicked creatures, the deaf and dumb, who are unable to understand the truth. These individuals are likened to animals, being heedless and lost. Allah points out that had there been any good in them, He would have made them listen and understand, but because of their stubbornness and lack of goodness, they would have still turned away even if they had comprehended the message.
