Allah commanded Abraham and Ishmael to purify the Kabah from idols, filth, and sins for those performing Tawaf and for those in Itikaf. Abraham prayed for Makkah to be a secure city and for its people to receive sustenance, specifying believers, while Allah allowed provision for disbelievers temporarily before their punishment. Abraham left Ishmael and his mother in the barren valley near the Kabah with water and dates; when the water ran out, his mother searched between As-Safa and Al-Marwah, then Gabriel caused Zamzam to gush, providing water for her and her child. The tribe of Jurhum settled there, and Ishmael grew, married, and learned Arabic. Abraham visited them, instructed Ishmael regarding the gate of his house, and together they raised the foundations of the Kabah while supplicating for acceptance, submission to Allah, a submissive progeny, guidance in Manasik, and forgiveness. Abraham and Ishmael performed Tawaf, prayer, and construction, establishing the rituals of Hajj under divine guidance. Later, Quraysh rebuilt the Kabah, observing Ibrahim’s foundations and ensuring purity in their resources. A dispute over the Black Stone was resolved by appointing Muhammad, known as Al-Amin, to place it, uniting the tribes in lifting it together. Ibn Az-Zubayr later rebuilt the Kabah on Ibrahim’s foundations, adding a door and extending the Hijr, following the Prophet’s wishes. The Kabah will be destroyed by Dhus-Sawiqatayn from Ethiopia after Gog and Magog, yet Hajj and Umrah will continue. Abraham and Ishmael’s supplication for submission, a Muslim progeny, correct Manasik, and forgiveness was fulfilled, with Ibrahim learning the Hajj rituals from Gabriel, including Tawaf, Sai between Safa and Marwah, stoning Iblis at the Jamrah, and visiting Mina, Arafat, and Al-Mashar Al-Haram.
