The people of the village were divided into three groups: one group violated the Sabbath by fishing on the day it was prohibited, another group forbade the transgression, and a third group neither participated nor forbade the act. The third group questioned the preachers, asking why they advised those who were doomed to destruction by Allah. The preachers replied that they were simply fulfilling their duty to enjoin good and forbid evil, hoping that perhaps the wrongdoers might repent and avoid Allah's punishment. When the wrongdoers ignored the advice, Allah saved those who prohibited evil and punished the transgressors with a severe torment. The fate of the passive group is not mentioned, as they neither did good nor committed wrong. The torment of the wrongdoers was described as severe and painful, and they were despised and humiliated.
