Tafsir Explanation: Allah describes the Battle of Badr, where the Muslim army camped on the near side of the valley, closer to Al-Madinah, while the idolators camped on the farther side, near Makkah. The Quraysh caravan, led by Abu Sufyan, was stationed below them, closer to the sea. If the Muslims and idolators had made an appointment to meet, they would have hesitated due to their smaller numbers. However, Allah arranged for them to meet without prior appointment to fulfill His decree, ensuring the victory of Islam and disgrace for the idolators. When the Muslims captured two boys bringing water to the Quraysh army, they mistakenly thought they belonged to Abu Sufyan's caravan. The boys confirmed they were from Quraysh, revealing that the army consisted of around 900 to 1,000 men, including prominent leaders like Utbah bin Rabi'ah and Abu Jahl. Allah's plan was to ensure that those destined for destruction would do so after clear evidence, while those destined for salvation would believe based on undeniable proof. The encounter at Badr served to elevate the truth and establish clear evidence, leaving no room for doubt.
