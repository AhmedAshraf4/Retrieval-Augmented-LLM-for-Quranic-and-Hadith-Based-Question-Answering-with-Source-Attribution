The story of Balam bin Baura, a man from the Children of Israel, illustrates the consequences of abandoning divine guidance. Allah gave him knowledge of His Ayat, including the knowledge of His Greatest Name, but he rejected it in favor of worldly desires. When the people of the city of the tyrants (Jerusalem) sought his help to oppose Prophet Musa, Bal`am initially resisted but was eventually swayed and prayed against Musa, leading to the loss of the knowledge Allah had granted him. His refusal to use the knowledge correctly resulted in his fall from grace. He became like a dog that pants regardless of being driven or left alone, symbolizing the misguided person who cannot benefit from advice or faith. His rejection of guidance is a warning, as it led to his disgrace in this life and humiliation in the Hereafter. The parable shows the danger of following vain desires and ignoring divine truth.
