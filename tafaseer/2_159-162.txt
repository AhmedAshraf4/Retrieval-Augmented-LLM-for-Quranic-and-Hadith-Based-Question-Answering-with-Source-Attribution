The Ayah condemns those who hide clear religious truths revealed by Allah, specifically those who conceal the description of Prophet Muhammad. This act of hiding knowledge is met with a curse from Allah, the angels, and all creatures, including the fish in the sea and birds in the air. The punishment for such concealment will continue into the Hereafter, with the person being cursed eternally in Hell, where their punishment will never be reduced or postponed. However, those who repent, correct their actions, and reveal the truth they had concealed will receive Allah's forgiveness. Those who persist in disbelief until their death will face the combined curse of Allah, the angels, and all humanity, resulting in eternal damnation. This Ayah also addresses the permissibility of cursing disbelievers, allowing it in certain contexts, such as during supplications, but with caution regarding specific individuals, as the end of their lives is ultimately known only to Allah.
