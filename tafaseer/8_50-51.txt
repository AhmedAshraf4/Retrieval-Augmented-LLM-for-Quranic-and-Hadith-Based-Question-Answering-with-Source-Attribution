When the angels capture the souls of the disbelievers at death, they strike their faces and backs, warning them to taste the punishment of the blazing Fire. This action recalls the events of the Battle of Badr, where the Muslims smote the faces of the idolators with swords, and when they fled, the angels struck their backs. Though the description here refers to Badr, it applies to all disbelievers. At the time of death, the angels forcibly remove the disbeliever's soul, as it resists leaving the body, while announcing Allah's anger and torment. The soul is taken out like a needle from wet wool, with veins and nerves still attached. This punishment is a direct consequence of the disbeliever’s evil deeds, and Allah is not unjust to His servants, for He rewards or punishes them according to their actions.
