Allah commands that believers must distance themselves from disbelievers, even if they are close family members such as parents, children, or siblings, if these relatives choose disbelief over faith. This is demonstrated in the case of Abu Ubaydah, who killed his father during the Battle of Badr when the father persistently praised idols. The verse reveals that those who truly believe in Allah and the Last Day will not form alliances with those who oppose Allah and His Messenger. This principle is further emphasized by the command to prefer Allah, His Messenger, and jihad in His cause over family, wealth, and worldly comforts. If a person prioritizes these worldly attachments over Allah and His cause, they are warned to wait for Allah's punishment. The verse also highlights that true faith requires loving the Prophet more than one's own self, as shown by Umar's declaration of love for the Prophet after realizing this truth. The Prophet further warned that abandoning jihad and focusing solely on worldly pursuits would lead to disgrace, which would only be lifted when people return to their faith.
