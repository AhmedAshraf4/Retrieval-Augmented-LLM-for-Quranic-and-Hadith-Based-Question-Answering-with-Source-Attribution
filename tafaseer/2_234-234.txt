The Ayah commands widows whose husbands have died to observe an Iddah (waiting period) of four months and ten nights, regardless of whether the marriage was consummated. This period is meant to confirm if the woman is pregnant. If she is, her Iddah ends when she gives birth, even if this happens immediately after her husband's death. An example of this is Subayah Al-Aslamiyah, whose husband died while she was pregnant, and after giving birth, she was allowed to remarry. The wisdom behind this waiting period is to ensure that any pregnancy is clear and to respect the mourning process. During the Iddah, the widow must refrain from beautification, including wearing perfume and adornments, to signify mourning. Mourning lasts for the entire period, with no remarriage allowed before the Iddah ends. After the Iddah, the widow can adorn herself without sin if she chooses to remarry. This period applies to all widows, regardless of age, social status, or belief, as indicated by the general meaning of the Ayah.
