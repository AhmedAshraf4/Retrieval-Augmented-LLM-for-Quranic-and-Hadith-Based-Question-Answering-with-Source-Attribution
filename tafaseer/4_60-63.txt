Tafsir Explanation: Allah condemns those who claim to believe in His revelations yet turn to judgments outside the Qur'an and Sunnah, as seen in the dispute between a Muslim and a Jew, where the Muslim man suggested referring to Ka'b bin Al-Ashraf, while the Jew wanted to go to Muhammad. The verse addresses hypocrites who prefer judgments of falsehood, which are described as Taghut. Allah rebukes them for rejecting the Prophet’s judgment, much like the polytheists who refuse to follow Allah’s guidance and instead follow their forefathers. True believers, on the other hand, accept the Prophet’s judgment without question. When a catastrophe strikes these hypocrites due to their misdeeds, they come to the Prophet, apologizing and claiming they only sought reconciliation. Allah warns that He knows what is in their hearts, and they will be punished accordingly. The Prophet is advised to turn away from them, admonish them with effective words, and offer guidance privately to help them repent.
