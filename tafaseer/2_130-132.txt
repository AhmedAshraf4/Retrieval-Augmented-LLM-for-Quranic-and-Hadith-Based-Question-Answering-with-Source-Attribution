Allah affirmed that Ibrahim worshipped Allah alone with complete sincerity, rejecting all other deities and refusing to associate partners with Him. He declared his innocence from his people’s idols and committed fully to the Creator of the heavens and the earth. Ibrahim initially prayed for his father’s forgiveness due to a promise, but when it became clear that his father was an enemy of Allah, he dissociated from him, remaining patient and humble. Allah honored Ibrahim with guidance, gratitude, obedience, and righteousness, granting him good in this world and placing him among the righteous in the Hereafter. Ibrahim submitted to Allah’s command and enjoined his sons, including Jacob, to follow this path of submission. Allah made true monotheism a lasting word among his descendants, and Jacob preserved this teaching, building Bayt Al-Maqdis and advising his children to adhere to it. Allah warned that turning away from Ibrahim’s religion is self-harm and great injustice, while those who follow his path, including Prophet Muhammad and the believers, are supported by Allah. Ibrahim’s sons were instructed to live and die as Muslims, maintaining obedience and righteous deeds, ensuring they remain on the path Allah chose for them, with divine guidance determining their ultimate outcome.
