When the People of the Book were told to believe in what God sent down to Muhammad, they claimed belief only in what was revealed to them in the Torah and Gospel and rejected what came after, even though the revelation to Muhammad was true and confirmed what they already possessed, which made it a proof against them. They recognized Muhammad as a true messenger yet denied him. Their claim of belief was contradicted by their past actions, because they killed prophets who came affirming the law they claimed to follow, doing so out of transgression, stubbornness, and injustice, accepting only what suited their desires. Moses had previously come to them with clear signs proving his messengership and God’s oneness, including major miracles, yet after Moses left to speak with God at the mountain, they worshipped the calf as a deity instead of God. This act of calf worship was deliberate wrongdoing despite their knowledge of the truth, and only afterward did they realize their misguidance and seek forgiveness.
