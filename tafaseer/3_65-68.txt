The Jews and Christians disputed before the Prophet Muhammad about the religion of Ibrahim, each claiming him as one of their own. The Jews said Ibrahim was Jewish, while the Christians claimed he was Christian. Allah rebuked both groups, pointing out that Ibrahim lived long before the Torah or Christianity existed. He asked why they disputed about something they had no knowledge of, warning them against arguing about matters beyond their understanding. Allah clarified that Ibrahim was neither a Jew nor a Christian but a true Muslim who rejected idolatry. The people most entitled to follow Ibrahim were those who followed his path, including Muhammad and the believers. Allah is the protector of the believers.
