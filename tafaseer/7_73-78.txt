The tribe of Thamud descended from Thamud bin `Athir, and they lived between Hijaz and Ash-Sham. Prophet Salih was sent to them to call for worshipping Allah alone, as was the message of all Prophets. Thamud demanded a miracle, asking Salih to bring forth a pregnant camel from a stone. Salih prayed to Allah, and the miracle occurred, with a pregnant she-camel emerging from a rock as requested. Thamud was commanded to let the camel drink from their well on alternate days. Despite this clear sign, they grew increasingly hostile, and eventually, a group from Thamud, urged by women seeking revenge, killed the camel. Salih warned them of Allah's punishment, telling them to enjoy their homes for three days before destruction came. On the first day, their faces turned yellow; on the second, red; and on the third, black. On the fourth day, a thunderous cry and a tremor destroyed them, leaving only Salih and his followers alive. The torment spared a disbeliever named Abu Righal, but when he left the Sacred Area, he too was struck down.
