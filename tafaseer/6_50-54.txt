The Messenger does not possess Allah's treasures nor knowledge of the unseen, as only Allah holds such knowledge and authority. He does not claim to be an angel, but a human who follows the revelations from Allah. Allah commands Muhammad to warn those who fear being gathered before Him on the Day of Judgment, where no intercessor or protector will be present except Allah, urging them to be conscious of Allah and perform good deeds. Allah instructs the Prophet not to turn away those who worship Him in sincerity, even if they are among the weak or poor, and to avoid desiring the worldly glitter that others may pursue. The weak followers of the Prophet were initially mocked by the disbelievers, who questioned if Allah would choose them over the noblemen. Allah responds by highlighting that true value lies in sincerity to Him, and those who strive in His cause will be guided. Allah assures that He does not look at appearances, but at hearts and deeds. When those who believe in Allah’s signs approach, they should be honored, and the Prophet is reminded that Allah’s mercy prevails over His anger, offering forgiveness to those who repent and amend their actions.
