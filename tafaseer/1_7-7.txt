Guide us to the straight path refers to a servant asking Allah for guidance, and Allah grants what is asked. The straight path is defined as the way of those whom Allah has favored, meaning those who obey Allah and His Messenger and are among the prophets, the truthful, the martyrs, and the righteous. This path is marked by knowledge of the truth and acting upon it through obedience and avoidance of what Allah has forbidden. It excludes the path of those who incurred anger, who knew the truth but deliberately turned away from it, and the path of those who went astray, who lacked correct knowledge and wandered from guidance. Those who incurred anger are identified as the Jews because they possessed knowledge but failed to act upon it, while those who went astray are identified as the Christians because they sought worship without correct knowledge. Both groups are misguided, but anger is more closely associated with the Jews and misguidance with the Christians. The straight path, in contrast, combines correct knowledge with correct action, and it is the path Allah grants as a favor to those He guides.
