The Jews mocked Allah's call for a goodly loan, questioning if He had become poor. In response, Allah condemns their claim, stating He has heard their blasphemous words, and He will record them, punishing them for their unjust actions, including the killing of prophets. They will face the torment of the Fire as a consequence of their deeds. Allah also refutes their demand for a miracle of fire consuming charity, pointing out that previous messengers had brought clear signs, including the very fire they requested, yet they still killed those prophets. Allah warns them for their hypocrisy and defiance, comforting the Prophet Muhammad by reminding him that earlier messengers were also rejected despite bringing clear evidence and divine books.
