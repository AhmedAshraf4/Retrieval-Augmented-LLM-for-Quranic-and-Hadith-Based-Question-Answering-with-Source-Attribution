Obeying the Messenger Muhammad is equivalent to obeying Allah; disobeying him is disobeying Allah. The Messenger's words are not from his own desires but divine revelation. Whoever obeys the Messenger and the Amir (leader) obeys Allah, and disobeying them results in disobeying Allah. The Messenger's role is to convey the message, and he is not responsible for those who turn away. Those who obey him will gain success, and he shares in their reward, while those who disobey will suffer loss, but the Messenger bears no burden for their actions. The hypocrites claim obedience, but secretly plot against the Messenger when they leave him. Allah records their secret actions and will punish them for their betrayal. The Messenger is advised to turn away from them and trust Allah, who is sufficient as a protector and helper.
