The Jews and Christians claimed that only those belonging to their communities would enter Paradise and treated this claim as a guaranteed outcome without proof, while also asserting that punishment would be brief and temporary. They were challenged to produce evidence for this claim. Entry into reward was instead tied to submitting oneself fully to God with sincere actions that conform to the divinely revealed way, since deeds are accepted only when they are both sincerely done for God alone and aligned with the message brought by the Messenger sent to all people. Actions lacking conformity, even if sincere, or actions done for display without sincerity, are rejected. Those who truly submit and act correctly are promised reward, future safety, and freedom from regret. The Jews and Christians also openly denied the validity of each other’s religion while both recited scripture, despite each scripture affirming the prophets and revelations the other rejected. This mutual denial reflected ignorance similar to earlier and later peoples who dismissed revealed guidance. God will gather all disputing groups on the Day of Resurrection and judge between them with complete justice regarding what they differed over.
