Tafsīr Explanation: The prohibition of consuming animals not slaughtered in Allah's Name is emphasized in this ayah, which states that such actions are disobedient. Even if slaughtered by a Muslim, meat is unlawful if Allah's Name is not invoked during the slaughter. This principle is supported by various hadiths, including those of the Prophet Muhammad, where he instructed to mention Allah's Name when releasing a trained hunting dog or during slaughter. The ayah also references the devil inspiring his followers to challenge this ruling, leading some idolaters to question why Muslims eat what is slaughtered but avoid what Allah causes to die. Allah warns that obeying such influences would equate to polytheism. Additionally, Allah condemns those who prefer others' rulings over His, equating such actions to shirk, as seen in the example of Jews and Christians who followed their religious leaders in matters of law, thus elevating them to a status of worship.
