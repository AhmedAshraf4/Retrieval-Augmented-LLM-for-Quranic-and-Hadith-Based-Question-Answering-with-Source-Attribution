After witnessing clear signs, including a murdered man being brought back to life when struck with part of the cow, questioned about his killer, accusing his nephews, and then dying again, the Children of Israel still denied the truth, and their hearts became hardened. Their hearts were compared to stones or harder, because they no longer accepted admonition despite direct miracles. Stones can release rivers, split to let water flow, or fall from heights out of fear of God, while their hearts showed none of this response. Some stones are therefore softer and more responsive than their hearts. The statement describes two conditions among them: some hearts were as hard as stone, and others were harder than stone. God was fully aware of their actions despite their denial.
