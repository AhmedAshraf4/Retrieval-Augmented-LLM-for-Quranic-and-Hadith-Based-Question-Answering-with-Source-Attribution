Muslims are allowed to consume the slaughtered animals of the People of the Book (Jews and Christians) because they believe in the prohibition of slaughtering for other than Allah and mention His Name during the process, despite their deviant beliefs. The Prophet and his companions ate from the Jewish slaughtered animals, even parts they considered forbidden, like fat, as proven by a narration from the conquest of Khaybar. This ruling applies to both eating from their slaughtered animals and feeding them from Muslims' slaughtered animals, ensuring fair treatment. Additionally, chaste women from the People of the Book are lawful for marriage, provided they are not involved in fornication or taking lovers, with the dowry paid respectfully. This exception to prohibiting marriage with idolaters aligns with earlier Qur'anic rulings, emphasizing the importance of chastity and lawful conduct in both men and women.
