Allah informed that He would take Isa and raise him to Himself while he was asleep, as He takes the souls of people during sleep. This event fulfilled the claim of the disbelievers that they killed Isa; however, Allah raised him and did not allow them to harm him. Those who followed Isa were made superior to those who disbelieved, and this would remain until the Day of Resurrection. The followers of Isa later split into different sects, with some holding extreme views, including divinity for Isa. These changes were further altered by Constantine, who shaped a new version of the religion. Meanwhile, the true followers of Isa, the Muslims, believe in him as a servant and messenger of Allah. Allah promised that the true religion would dominate, and the Muslims would eventually conquer lands once controlled by disbelievers. Allah then warned the disbelievers that they would face torment in both this life and the Hereafter. In contrast, those who believe and do righteous deeds would be rewarded with victory and Paradise. Finally, the story of `Isa, his life, and his miraculous ascension to Allah was revealed as the truth, with no dispute in it, and it is Allah who decrees all things by His will.
