The Prophet Muhammad prayed facing Jerusalem for sixteen or seventeen months after migrating to Medina, as commanded by Allah, while he desired to pray towards the Kabah in Makkah. He observed the sky awaiting Allah’s command, and then Allah revealed that he should face the Kabah. The first prayer he led towards the Kabah was the Asr prayer, and those praying with him immediately turned towards it, even while in the bowing position. Allah reassured that the prayers offered towards Jerusalem before the change were not lost. The Jews and other disbelievers questioned the change, and Allah affirmed that all authority belongs to Him and He guides whom He wills. The Qiblah initially faced Jerusalem to distinguish those who would obey the Messenger from those who would turn away, and its change to the Kabah tested faith and obedience. The Companions complied immediately, demonstrating perfect obedience. Allah chose the Kabah, built by Abraham, as the permanent Qiblah, granting the reward for prayers towards both directions and showing His mercy. The Muslims were made a just and balanced nation to bear witness over mankind, and the Prophet would testify on the Day of Resurrection alongside his Ummah for previous messengers’ messages. Allah’s care and guidance ensured that sincere followers were rewarded and protected, exemplifying His mercy greater than even a mother’s care for her child.
