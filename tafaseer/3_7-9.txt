The Qur'an contains both Muhkamat (clear) and Mutashabihat (unclear) verses. The Muhkamat verses are the foundations of the Book, providing clear guidance on rulings, obligations, and prohibitions. In contrast, the Mutashabihat verses have multiple meanings, some aligning with the Muhkamat, while others may lead to misinterpretation. Those with deviations in their hearts follow the Mutashabihat to mislead others, altering the meaning to support false beliefs, as seen in claims about Jesus' divinity. True guidance is found by understanding the Muhkamat, which are unequivocal and cannot be misinterpreted. Only Allah knows the true interpretation (Ta’wil) of the Mutashabihat, though scholars can explain them within the limits of their knowledge. The believers affirm that both the clear and unclear verses are from Allah and do not contradict each other. They pray for firm guidance, asking Allah not to let their hearts deviate after being guided, and for mercy that strengthens their faith. The verse also emphasizes that on the Day of Judgment, Allah will gather everyone and judge their deeds.
