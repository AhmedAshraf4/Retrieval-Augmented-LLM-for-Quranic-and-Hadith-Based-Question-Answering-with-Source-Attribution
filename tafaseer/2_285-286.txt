Tafsir Explanation: The last two ayat of Surat Al-Baqarah were revealed to the Prophet Muhammad during his Isra journey when he ascended to the Sidrat al-Muntaha in the sixth heaven. They were given to him as a special gift, alongside the five daily prayers and forgiveness for his followers who did not associate others with Allah. These ayat emphasize belief in Allah, His angels, books, and messengers, affirming that believers do not differentiate between the messengers, all of whom are considered truthful and righteous. The verses highlight Allah’s mercy in not burdening anyone beyond their capacity, as well as the responsibility for one’s actions—good or bad. Believers are instructed to seek Allah's forgiveness for forgetfulness or errors, and to ask for relief from heavy burdens and trials. The verses also call for Allah's mercy, forgiveness, and protection from further mistakes, while urging believers to seek victory over disbelievers.
