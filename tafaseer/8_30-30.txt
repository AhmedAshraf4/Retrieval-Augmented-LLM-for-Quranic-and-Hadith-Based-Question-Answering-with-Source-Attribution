The Makkans plotted to imprison, kill, or expel the Prophet. Their leaders gathered at Dar An-Nadwah, where Iblis appeared in the guise of an old man from Najd. He advised them to deal with Muhammad by imprisoning him, expelling him, or even killing him. Some suggested imprisoning him like past poets, but Iblis warned that this would only allow Muhammad’s followers to free him. Others proposed expelling him, but Iblis cautioned that this could lead to Muhammad gaining more supporters, eventually turning against the Quraysh. Abu Jahl proposed a final plan: selecting strong young men from each tribe to kill Muhammad simultaneously, making his tribe, Banu Hashim, accept the blood money. The leaders agreed, and preparations began. However, Jibril informed the Prophet of the plot, warning him not to sleep in his bed that night. This marked the beginning of the Prophet’s migration to Madinah. Allah’s response was a reminder of His power: while the disbelievers plotted, Allah too was planning, and His plan triumphed over theirs, safeguarding the Prophet and leading to his migration.
