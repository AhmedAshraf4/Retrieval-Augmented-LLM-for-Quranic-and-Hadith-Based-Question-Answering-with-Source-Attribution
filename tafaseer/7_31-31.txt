Tafsir Explanation: Allah commands that people take adornment when going to the Masjid, specifically refuting the practice of the idolators who would perform Tawaf around the Sacred House while naked. These idolators, both men and women, would do so at different times of the day, with women making statements about unveiling parts of their bodies. Allah instructs that people wear clean, proper clothing that covers their private parts when performing acts of worship, particularly prayers. This is further emphasized by the Sunnah, which encourages wearing the best clothes for prayers, especially on Fridays and `Id, and for men to use perfume and Siwak as part of their adornment. Additionally, Allah permits eating and drinking, but prohibits extravagance and arrogance in these actions. The Prophet Muhammad also advises moderation in eating, suggesting that a person should only fill a third of their stomach with food, a third with drink, and leave a third for breathing, emphasizing balance and avoidance of excess. Allah does not like those who exceed limits in what He has allowed or prohibited.
