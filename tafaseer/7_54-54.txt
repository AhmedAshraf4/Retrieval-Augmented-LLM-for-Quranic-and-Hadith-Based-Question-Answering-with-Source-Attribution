Tafsir Explanation: Allah created the universe, including the heavens, earth, and all that exists within them, over six days, with the final creation, Adam, completed on Friday. On each day, specific acts of creation took place: the dust on Saturday, mountains on Sunday, trees on Monday, unpleasant things on Tuesday, light on Wednesday, creatures on Thursday, and Adam after Asr on Friday. The term "As-Sabt," meaning stoppage or break, signifies that no creation occurred on Saturday, the seventh day. Allah then rose over the Throne, affirming His unique attributes without comparison to creation. The day and night cycle serves as a sign of Allah's control, where the night covers the day, and vice versa, always in succession, with each following its ordained path. The sun, moon, and stars are all under Allah’s command, and He is the source of creation and all decrees, with the universe moving according to His will.
