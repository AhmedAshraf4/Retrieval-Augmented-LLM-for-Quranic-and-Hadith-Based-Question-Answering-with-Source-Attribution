Tafsir Explanation: Allah promised support for the Muslims through the angels in their battles, specifically mentioning that the Muslims would be aided with three thousand angels during the Battle of Badr. This support was prompted when the Muslims learned that the enemy had received reinforcements, which troubled them, leading to the revelation of this promise. The angels, distinct with marks, were sent to assist them. Initially, one thousand angels were sent, followed by three thousand, and eventually five thousand angels, ensuring victory for the Muslims. However, during the Battle of Uhud, the Muslims did not receive angelic aid because they lacked patience and taqwa. The angels would have rushed to their aid if they had maintained patience and fear of Allah. The angels at Badr were distinguishable by their white wool clothing and markings on their horses.
