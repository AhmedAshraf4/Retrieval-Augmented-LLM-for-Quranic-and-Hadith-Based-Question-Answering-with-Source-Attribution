Tafsir Explanation: The idolators, led by Abu Jahl, left Makkah heading for Badr with the intention of boasting and being seen by others. They planned to celebrate with camels, alcohol, and music, aiming to make a memorable statement among the Arabs. However, this arrogance led them to their downfall, as they eventually faced death at Badr, where they were defeated and thrown into the well, humiliated and condemned to eternal punishment. Shaytan, disguised as Suraqah, encouraged them, claiming no one could defeat them that day and promising his support. But when the battle ensued and the Muslims received divine aid, Shaytan fled in fear, admitting he saw what they could not. Meanwhile, the hypocrites among the idolators mocked the Muslims, believing them to be deceived due to their small number. However, Allah’s might and wisdom proved stronger, as those who relied on Him were granted victory.
