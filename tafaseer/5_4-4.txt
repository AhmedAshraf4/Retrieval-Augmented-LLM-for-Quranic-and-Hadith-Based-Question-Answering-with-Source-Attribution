Allah clarifies what is lawful, specifying the types of food and sustenance permitted for Muslims. While certain foods are prohibited due to their harmful effects on the body or religion, Allah allows exceptions in cases of necessity. The lawful foods are referred to as "At-Tayyibat," which include all pure and good provisions. The game hunted with trained animals like dogs and falcons is also permissible, provided the animal does not eat from the catch before the owner arrives. The trained predator, when sent after the game, must do so in Allah’s Name. If the game is caught and remains untouched by the animal, it is lawful to eat; however, if the animal eats from it, the game is not permissible. The key action here is to mention Allah's Name when releasing the hunting animals or when shooting arrows to ensure the game remains lawful.
