The ayah refers to a group of Jews who transgressed the sanctity of the Sabbath in the village of Aylah, located by the sea. They defied Allah's command to observe the Sabbath by using a trick: they allowed fish to appear openly on the Sabbath, when they were prohibited from fishing, and caught them then. On other days, when fishing was allowed, the fish stayed hidden. This act of deceit was a test from Allah, who allowed the fish to appear on the forbidden day to expose their rebellion. The punishment for this transgression came swiftly. The verse serves as a warning to the Jews of Muhammad’s time not to repeat the mistakes of their forefathers by violating Allah’s prohibitions with deceitful tricks.
