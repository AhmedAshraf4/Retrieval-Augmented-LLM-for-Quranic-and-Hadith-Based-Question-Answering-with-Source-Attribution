The verse states that salvation was promised to anyone among the believers, Jews, Christians, and Sabians who believed in God and the Last Day and acted righteously, and this applied to people before the mission of Muhammad, because each community that followed its own prophet and law was rightly guided and rewarded. After Muhammad was sent as the final messenger to all humanity, no deeds were accepted unless they conformed to his law, and belief required obedience to him and avoidance of what he forbade. The Jews are identified as the followers of Moses who judged by the Torah, and their name reflects repentance. The Christians are identified as the followers of Jesus, called helpers because they supported him in God’s cause. The Sabians are described as a group without a distinct revealed law, neither Jews nor Christians nor idolaters, who lived without enforcing a specific religion, which is why the pagans used the term for anyone who abandoned existing religions. Those who truly believed and obeyed God and His messenger were promised security from fear of future events and grief over past loss, a promise fulfilled for the obedient believers until the Day of Judgment.
