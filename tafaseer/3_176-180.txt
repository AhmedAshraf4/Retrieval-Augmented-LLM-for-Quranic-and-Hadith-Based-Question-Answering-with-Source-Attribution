The disbelievers' haste to reject faith does not harm Allah, for He has decreed that they will have no share in the Hereafter and will face great torment. They may think that their wealth and power signify Allah's favor, but this delay in their punishment only increases their sin. Allah allows trials to reveal the true believers and hypocrites, as seen in the Battle of Uhud, where faith and hypocrisy were exposed. Allah does not disclose the unseen, but chooses His messengers to guide His people. Belief and fear of Allah result in great reward. Those who hoard wealth, thinking it benefits them, will face its torment on the Day of Judgment when their greed will be tied around their necks like a collar. This punishment reflects the refusal to pay zakah, as described by the Prophet, where wealth becomes a poisonous snake that torments its owner. Allah controls all things, and true benefit lies in spending His wealth for the Hereafter, as He is aware of all deeds and intentions.
