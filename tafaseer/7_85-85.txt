Shuayb, a prophet from the descendants of Midyan, was sent to the people of Madyan, a tribe living near Maan. His name in the Syrian language was Yathrun (Jethro). He called his people to worship Allah alone and warned them of the clear signs he had brought from their Lord. He commanded them to give full measure and weight in their transactions, prohibiting the dishonest practice of cheating in buying and selling. He emphasized that they should not wrong others, as this behavior was condemned. Shu`ayb was known as the "Speaker of the Prophets" due to his eloquent speech and advice. His message focused on upholding fairness in commerce and justice.
