Allah commands Muhammad to tell the People of the Scripture that they have no true religion unless they follow and implement the Torah and the Gospel, believing in all the books Allah revealed to His Prophets. These books call for following Muhammad and accepting his prophecy, while adhering to his Law. Despite the increased rebellion and disbelief from many upon receiving Muhammad's message, Muslims are told not to grieve for those who disbelieve. Allah then clarifies that those who believe, including the Jews (entrusted with the Torah), the Sabians (a sect of Christians and Magians), and the Christians (entrusted with the Gospel), will be saved if they believe in Allah and the Hereafter, and perform righteous deeds in accordance with Muhammad's Law. Such individuals will have no fear or sadness regarding what is to come, nor will grief affect them.
