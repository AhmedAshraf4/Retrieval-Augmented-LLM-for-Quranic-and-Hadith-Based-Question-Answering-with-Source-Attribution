Allah commands His Prophet to convey the message of being guided to the straight path, a firm and established religion, exemplified by the faith of Ibrahim, who was Hanif (pure monotheist) and not of the idolaters. The Prophet is instructed to follow Ibrahim's religion, which is the path of submission to Allah alone. Unlike previous Prophets, the Prophet Muhammad is the final Messenger, bringing the perfect and complete version of this faith, making him the leader of all humanity, and his intercession will be sought on the Day of Judgment. The Prophet is also directed to declare that his prayer, sacrifice, life, and death are all dedicated to Allah alone, opposing the practices of idolaters who worship and sacrifice to others beside Him. All Prophets, from Ibrahim to Musa and Isa, followed the religion of Islam, submitting to Allah’s will, and their laws were abrogated by the final law brought by Muhammad. This law will remain in effect until the Day of Resurrection, and it will be clear and triumphant. The Prophet’s declaration of his submission, prayer, and sacrifices aligns with the universal message of Islam, emphasizing sincerity in worship and devotion to Allah alone.
