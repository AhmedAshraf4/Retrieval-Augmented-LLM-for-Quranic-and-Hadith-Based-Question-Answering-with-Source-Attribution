The verse distinguishes between believers who stay home and those who strive in Jihad. It was revealed when Ibn Umm Maktum, a blind man, expressed his desire to participate but was hindered by his disability. Allah clarified that those who are unable to join due to disability, such as blindness or lameness, are not the same as those who could join but choose not to. The reward for those who fight in Allah's cause is greater than those who stay behind without a valid excuse. Allah promises a tremendous reward for the Mujahidin, including higher ranks in Paradise, forgiveness, and mercy. The verse emphasizes that Jihad is a collective obligation (Fard Kifayah) and not mandatory for every individual, but those who participate are granted a greater reward, with a special mention of the hundred grades in Paradise prepared for the Mujahidin.
