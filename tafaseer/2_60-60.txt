God reminded the Children of Israel of His favor when He answered the supplication of their prophet Moses for water, commanding him to strike a stone with his staff, after which water burst out forcefully and formed twelve distinct springs. Each tribe was assigned its own spring, so every group drank from its designated source without crowding or travel. They were also sustained by manna and quails and drank the provided water without effort or hardship. They were commanded to worship the One who provided these favors and warned not to repay this provision with disobedience and corruption on the earth, because such behavior causes divine favors to be withdrawn.
