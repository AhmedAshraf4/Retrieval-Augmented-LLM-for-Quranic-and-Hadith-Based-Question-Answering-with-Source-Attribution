Tafsir Explanation: Allah commands that the property of orphans be returned to them in full when they reach adolescence, prohibiting caretakers from using or exchanging it. This includes not substituting inferior items for those of higher quality, such as replacing a healthy animal with a weaker one or swapping good currency with fake money. Mixing the orphans' property with one's own to unjustly consume both is a grave sin. Allah also forbids marrying orphan girls without giving them a fair dowry. If a guardian fears he cannot treat an orphan girl justly or provide her a proper dowry, he is instructed to marry other women instead. Men are allowed to marry up to four women but must treat them justly. If a man fears he cannot be fair, he should marry only one or take female captives. Dowries for wives are obligatory, and if a wife willingly remits part of her dowry, the husband may accept it without harm.
