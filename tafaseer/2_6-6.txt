Those who disbelieve are described as people who covered and hid the truth. Because God had already decreed their state, warning them or not warning them produces the same result, and they do not believe in what Muhammad was sent with. This meaning is supported by other statements that those upon whom God’s judgment has become binding do not believe even when every sign reaches them, and that the People of the Book would not follow the prayer direction even if all signs were brought to them. Guidance does not reach those for whom misery was written, and misguidance is not removed once it is decreed. Muhammad was eager for all people to believe, but belief occurs only for those for whom happiness was decreed, and rejection occurs only for those whose rejection was already written. The role of the messenger is limited to delivering the message, while the final reckoning belongs to God.
