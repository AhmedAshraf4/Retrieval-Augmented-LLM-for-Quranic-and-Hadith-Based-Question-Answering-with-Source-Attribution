In pre-Islamic times, men could forcefully inherit women after a relative's death, deciding whether to marry them, prevent their remarriage, or marry them off. This practice was prohibited by the verse, which forbids inheriting women against their will. Allah also prohibits men from coercing women into giving back their dowries, unless they commit adultery, in which case the dowry may be reclaimed in exchange for a divorce. Men are commanded to treat their wives with kindness, as the Prophet Muhammad showed through his gentle, playful, and compassionate behavior with his wives. If a man dislikes his wife, Allah may bring good through patience, such as the birth of a child. It is forbidden to take back the dowry if a man divorces his wife to marry another, even if the dowry is a large sum. Allah emphasizes the sacred nature of the marriage contract, which grants the husband rights over his wife. Marrying a father's wife is strictly prohibited, as it was a disgraceful practice. This prohibition aims to honor the father and prevent animosity between a son and his father.
