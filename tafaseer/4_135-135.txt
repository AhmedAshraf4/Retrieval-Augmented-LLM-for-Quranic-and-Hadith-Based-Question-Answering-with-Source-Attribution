Allah commands the believers to uphold justice and fairness, regardless of external pressures or personal interests, even if it means testifying against themselves, their parents, or their close relatives. Testimony must be truthful, unaltered, and for the sake of Allah, even if it causes harm to those involved. Allah reminds that both the rich and poor are equally under His protection and care, so testimony should not favor one over the other based on their status. Believers are urged not to let personal desires, hatred, or enmity influence their judgment, as justice is closely tied to piety. The Prophet's companion, Abdullah bin Rawahah, demonstrated this when he refused to be swayed by a bribe from the Jews of Khaybar, insisting that his love for the Prophet Muhammad and commitment to justice outweighed his personal feelings. Allah warns against distorting or withholding testimony, as these actions are sinful, and emphasizes that He is fully aware of all actions, promising reward or punishment accordingly.
