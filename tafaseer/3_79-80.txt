This verse emphasizes that no Prophet, Messenger, or scholar ever claimed to be worshipped alongside Allah. Prophets are messengers conveying Allah's commands, not figures of worship. The verse critiques the false claims of misguided rabbis and priests who distort the truth. Prophets, including Muhammad, only call people to worship Allah alone. The verse highlights that Prophets do not order the worship of angels or themselves, nor do they lead people to disbelief after they have embraced submission to Allah. Instead, they teach the knowledge of the Book, urging followers to be wise, learned, and forbearing, guiding them to true worship of Allah. Prophets convey the message that there is no god but Allah, and anyone who calls to the worship of others is leading people to disbelief.
