Tafsir Explanation: Allah commands the Muslims to prepare for war against the disbelievers, urging them to gather whatever strength they can, including warhorses. Horses used for jihad are especially blessed, as their food, dung, and even their footprints earn rewards for the owner. Those who prepare horses for gambling or self-interest incur sin. The aim of these preparations is to strike fear into the hearts of Allah's enemies, including the disbelievers, hypocrites, and other enemies such as the Persians or the tribe of Bani Qurayza. Allah assures that whatever is spent for His cause in jihad will be fully repaid without injustice. This promise is further emphasized by the parable of a seed that grows into seven ears, with each ear bearing a hundred grains, symbolizing the manifold reward for charity in Allah's way.
