When the Prophet Muhammad migrated to Al-Madinah, Allah initially commanded him to face Bayt Al-Maqdis in prayer. He continued to face it for over ten months while desiring to face the Kabah in Makkah, supplicating and awaiting Allah’s command. Allah then revealed the change, instructing him to turn his face toward the Kabah. The Jews disliked this change, questioning why Muslims shifted their Qiblah, but Allah affirmed that the direction of prayer belongs to Him and that the previous Qiblah toward Jerusalem was a test to distinguish those who follow the Messenger from those who turn away. Allah commanded facing Al-Masjid Al-Haram from wherever one is on earth, allowing exceptions for voluntary prayers while traveling, during battle, or when the Qiblah is uncertain, without burdening anyone beyond their capacity. The People of the Book already knew from their scriptures that the true Qiblah would be the Ka`bah, but they denied it out of envy and disbelief, while Allah remained fully aware of their actions.
