Tafsir Explanation: Allah praised the Tawrah given to Moses, the Injil given to Jesus, and then emphasized the Qur'an as the final and truthful scripture revealed to Muhammad. The Qur'an confirms the previous scriptures, fulfilling prophecies within them, and increasing the faith of those who are sincere and knowledgeable. Those given knowledge before the Qur'an, when it is recited, fall in prostration, declaring that the promise of Allah’s Messenger has been fulfilled. The Qur'an is described as trustworthy, a witness, and dominant over previous scriptures, confirming the truth in them and rejecting any contradictions. Muhammad is commanded to judge by what Allah revealed in the Qur'an, not to follow the desires of people or deviate from the truth. Allah established different laws for different nations as a test of obedience. If He had willed, He could have made all people follow one law, but He chose to test them through varying laws. The Qur'an is to be followed in all disputes, and those who turn away from it are punished for their sins. Most people, despite clear signs, remain rebellious, and those who seek judgment outside of Allah's law, including following customs from the pre-Islamic era, are criticized. Allah’s judgment is the best for those who truly believe and understand. The verse highlights the importance of adhering strictly to Allah’s laws and avoiding misguidance from worldly desires or previous misinterpretations.
