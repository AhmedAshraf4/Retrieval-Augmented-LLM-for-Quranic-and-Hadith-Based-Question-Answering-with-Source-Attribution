The verse condemns those who reject Allah's commands by not implementing His revealed laws, labeling them as disbelievers and unjust. This applies to those who fail to administer justice, especially when it involves punishing the oppressor or avenging the oppressed as Allah has commanded. In the case of retaliation, the general rule is "life for life, an eye for an eye," ensuring equality for free Muslims, men and women alike, as well as for slaves. Retaliation for injuries should only occur after the victim's wound has healed. If a person disobeys this and proceeds with retaliation prematurely, they will have no further claim for compensation if their condition worsens. However, if the victim pardons the wrongdoer and forgoes retaliation, this act of forgiveness is seen as an expiation for the wrongdoer's sin and a reward for the victim. The verse also addresses the greater and lesser forms of disbelief, injustice, and sin, highlighting the severity of neglecting divine law.
