Tafsir Explanation: After the completion of Hajj rituals, Allah commands His remembrance, urging people to remember Him more than they remembered their forefathers. During Jahiliyyah, people would mention the deeds of their ancestors, but Allah directed them to remember Him with greater devotion. The verse encourages not only remembering Allah but also calling upon Him in supplication, as this increases the likelihood of acceptance. Some people would only ask for worldly gains, neglecting the Hereafter, for which Allah criticizes them. He praises those who ask for the good of both this life and the Hereafter, seeking safety from the Fire and blessings in Paradise. The supplication covers all aspects of well-being in this world, including health, wealth, knowledge, and comfort, and seeks refuge from harm and evil. The best good of the Hereafter includes attaining Paradise and safety from the horrors of the Day of Judgment. Allah also emphasizes the importance of patience, gratitude, and remembrance in both this life and the Hereafter. This supplication is encouraged in the Sunnah, and it was even recited by the Prophet when visiting a sick companion, advising him to seek Allah’s mercy for both worlds rather than the suffering of the Hereafter.
