Tafsir Explanation: Allah created all produce, fruits, and cattle, yet idolators misused these provisions by dividing them into permissible and prohibited categories based on their misguided beliefs. Some fruits are trellised, like grapevines, while others grow wild. Allah commands that the due charity be given from the harvest, which includes providing for the poor on the day of harvest. Before Zakah was obligatory, people would give portions of their produce, including grain and hay, to the needy. Allah warns against extravagance, exemplified by an incident where someone tried to give all his date palm fruits in charity, only to end up with nothing. Allah advises moderation in consumption, ensuring that nothing is wasted. He created cattle for various purposes: some for riding or work, and others for food, milk, and clothing. Allah commands that these provisions be consumed without following Shaytan’s misguided rules, which led people to prohibit what Allah has allowed. Shaytan is an open enemy, urging people to follow his destructive path, as he seeks to lead them into punishment.
