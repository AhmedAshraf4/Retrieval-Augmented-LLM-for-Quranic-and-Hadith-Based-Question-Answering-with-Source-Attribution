The people of Pharaoh rejected the signs and miracles brought by Moses, claiming they would not believe regardless of the proof. In response, Allah sent various plagues to punish them, starting with a heavy rain that destroyed crops, followed by locusts that consumed vegetation, and then grain bugs that ruined their stored grain. Despite these afflictions, they repeatedly promised to believe and release the Children of Israel if Moses invoked Allah to remove the punishments, but they broke their promises each time. Allah then sent frogs that swarmed their homes and food, and when they again sought relief, the frogs were removed without their belief. Finally, Allah turned all their water sources into blood, and once again, they promised to believe if the affliction was lifted, but they did not follow through. Each punishment came in succession, yet Pharaoh and his people persisted in disbelief and failed to honor their commitments.
