Allah reminded the Children of Israel of His favor when He provided manna and quails, a pure and beneficial food given without hardship, but they grew bored and asked Moses to replace it with inferior foods that grow from the earth, including herbs, cucumbers, garlic, lentils, and onions, because they could not tolerate eating the same food repeatedly. Moses rebuked them for wanting to exchange what was better for what was lower and told them to go to any city, where such ordinary foods were easily available, showing that their request was unnecessary and based on arrogance rather than need, so it was not fulfilled. As a consequence of their persistent behavior, humiliation and misery were imposed upon them, meaning lasting disgrace, poverty, and subjugation, and they returned carrying the wrath of Allah. This punishment was the result of their disbelief in Allah’s signs, their rejection and killing of prophets without right, and their continual disobedience and transgression of limits, which caused divine anger to descend upon them and remain with them.
