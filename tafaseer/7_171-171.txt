When the Jews rebelled, Allah raised Mount Tur over them as a threat, holding it above their heads. This act was a direct fulfillment of Allah's command, as the angels lifted the mountain, making it appear as if it were a canopy. When the people saw this, they were forced to accept the commands that Moses had delivered from Allah. These commands had previously been too burdensome for them, but the threat of the mountain compelled them to comply. This event highlights the severity of their disobedience and the extreme measure Allah used to enforce His command.
