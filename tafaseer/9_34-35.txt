The verse warns against corrupt scholars and misguided worshippers, symbolized by the Jewish rabbis (Ahbar) and Christian monks (Ruhban). These figures, who misuse their positions for personal gain, mislead others by distorting truth and preventing people from following the true path of Allah. They devour wealth unlawfully, hinder others from Allah’s way, and present false righteousness while leading people towards damnation. The verse also addresses those who hoard wealth (Kanz) without spending it in Allah’s cause, promising them severe punishment in the Hereafter. Such hoarders will face torment, with their wealth branded on their bodies in Hell. This punishment reflects the destructive attachment to wealth over obedience to Allah, as illustrated in the example of those who value gold and silver more than fulfilling religious duties, leading to their eternal regret.
