Tafsir Explanation: Surah Al `Imran was revealed in Medina, with the first eighty-three verses addressing the delegation from Najran, which arrived in Medina in the ninth year of Hijrah. The Surah emphasizes that the Qur'an was revealed in truth to Prophet Muhammad, confirming previous scriptures and their teachings. The Qur'an affirms the revelations of the Torah to Moses and the Gospel to Jesus, as guidance for their respective peoples. It also mentions the Qur'an as the "criterion," distinguishing between truth and falsehood. Those who reject Allah’s signs and the prophethood of Muhammad will face severe punishment on the Day of Judgment, as Allah is mighty and capable of retribution.
