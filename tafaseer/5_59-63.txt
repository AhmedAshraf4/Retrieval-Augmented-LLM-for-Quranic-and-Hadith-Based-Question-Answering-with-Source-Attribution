The People of the Scriptures criticize the believers simply because of their faith in Allah and the revealed scriptures. They mock the believers for following the guidance sent to them, but they have no legitimate reason for their animosity. Allah points out that those who oppose the believers are rebellious and have deviated from the straight path. On the Day of Resurrection, these critics will face a worse punishment, as they will have incurred Allah's curse, anger, and eternal displeasure. Allah will transform some of them into monkeys and swine, a punishment already mentioned in earlier scriptures. The hypocrites among them pretend to believe, but they inwardly harbor disbelief and continue to act in sinful ways, deceiving others while Allah knows their hidden intentions. They hasten to commit sins, engage in transgression, and unlawfully consume others' wealth, particularly through bribes and interest. The scholars, such as the Rabbaniyyun and Ahbar, are criticized for not forbidding these sinful actions. Their failure to stop the evil committed by the people will lead to dire consequences, as the people will face punishment for their unchecked sins.
