Tafsir Explanation: Allah is Al-Ghani, self-sufficient and free from need, while all creatures rely on Him. He is merciful, and if He wills, He can destroy the people who defy Him and replace them with obedient successors, just as He raised them from the descendants of other nations. Allah's ability to replace them with others who obey Him is easy, and He can even bring about a new creation if He wishes. The disbelievers are promised the Resurrection, and no one can escape it; they will be resurrected even after becoming dust and bones. Muhammad is warned to continue his way, and the disbelievers are to continue theirs, and both will soon know who will have the happy end in the Hereafter. The disbelievers will not succeed, as Allah's promise to make His Messenger victorious came true, with Muhammad triumphing over his enemies, including the conquest of Makkah, and the spread of Islam across the Arabian Peninsula. Allah’s victory for His Messengers and believers is guaranteed, and on the Day of Judgment, the wrongdoers’ excuses will not save them. The righteous will inherit the land, fulfilling Allah's decree.
