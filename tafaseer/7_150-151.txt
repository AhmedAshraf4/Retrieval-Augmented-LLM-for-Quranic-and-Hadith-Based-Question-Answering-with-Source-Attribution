Tafsir Explanation: Musa rebuked his people for worshiping the calf in his absence, criticizing their haste in acting against Allah’s will. He threw down the Tablets in anger upon seeing their actions, demonstrating that witnessing the event firsthand had a greater impact than being informed of it. Musa then seized his brother Harun by the hair and dragged him, fearing that Harun had not done enough to prevent the wrongdoing. Harun explained that he had been weak and nearly killed by the people, urging Musa not to shame him. Before this, Harun had already tried to guide the people, calling them to follow him and obey Allah. After the confrontation, Musa asked Allah for forgiveness for himself and his brother, seeking His mercy.
