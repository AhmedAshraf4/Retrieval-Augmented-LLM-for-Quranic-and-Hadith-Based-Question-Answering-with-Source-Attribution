After the magicians believed in Musa, Firawn threatened them, accusing them of plotting with Musa to overthrow his rule. He falsely claimed that Musa had taught them magic, but in reality, Musa had never met them before. Firawn, in an attempt to deceive the masses, warned the magicians of severe punishment if they did not recant, promising to cut off their hands and feet and crucify them. However, the magicians responded with unwavering faith, declaring their return to Allah and their belief in His superior punishment. They asked for patience and requested to die as Muslims. They rejected Firawn’s threats, declaring that he could only rule in this world, while Allah’s reward and punishment were eternal. Despite Firawn's threats, the magicians remained steadfast in their belief, and they ended their day as martyrs after enduring the cruel punishment.
