Tafsir Explanation: Allah commands His followers to fear Him through Taqwa, which involves refraining from prohibitions and disobedience. He then instructs them to seek Wasilah, a means of approach to Him, by obeying His commands and performing acts that please Him. Wasilah also refers to the highest rank in Paradise, reserved for the Prophet Muhammad, and those who ask for it are promised his intercession. Allah further commands believers to strive in His cause through Jihad to achieve success, including eternal reward in Paradise, where they will live in comfort and security. In contrast, Allah warns the disbelievers that no amount of wealth, even if they possessed the entire earth’s worth in gold, will be accepted as ransom on the Day of Judgment to escape His torment. They will experience eternal, painful punishment in Hell, unable to escape, and their suffering will be unending, as they are repeatedly driven back into the flames.
