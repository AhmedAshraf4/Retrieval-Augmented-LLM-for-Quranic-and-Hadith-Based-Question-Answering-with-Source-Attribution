Adam committed the error, then turned back to God in repentance and supplication. He acknowledged his wrongdoing and asked for forgiveness and mercy, stating that without forgiveness he would be among the losers. Adam received specific words from his Lord, in which he appealed to God’s prior mercy, recalled being created by God, having life breathed into him, and being shown mercy at his creation, and he affirmed that the act had occurred by God’s decree. Adam asked whether repentance would lead to his return to Paradise, and God accepted his repentance. God accepted Adam’s repentance and forgave him, because God accepts the repentance of the servant who regrets the sin and returns to Him, and God is forgiving and merciful.
