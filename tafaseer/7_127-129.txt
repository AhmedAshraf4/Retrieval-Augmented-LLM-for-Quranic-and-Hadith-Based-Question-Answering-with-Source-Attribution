The chiefs of Pharaoh's people conspired to prevent Musa and his people from spreading their message, fearing they would lead the people to abandon Pharaoh's gods and worship Allah. They accused the Children of Israel of causing mischief, although it was Pharaoh and his people who were the true mischief-makers. Pharaoh ordered the killing of the Israelite boys and the sparing of the women, a decree he had enforced even before Musa's birth to prevent his survival. Despite Pharaoh's efforts, Allah granted victory to the Children of Israel, humbling and drowning Pharaoh and his soldiers. When the Children of Israel complained to Musa about their suffering, both before and after his arrival, Musa assured them of Allah’s promise that their enemy would be destroyed, urging them to trust in Allah's plan and remain patient. The Children of Israel would inherit the earth, and the righteous would prevail.
