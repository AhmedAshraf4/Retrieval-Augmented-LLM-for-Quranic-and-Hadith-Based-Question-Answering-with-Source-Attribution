Tafsir Explanation: When the Muslims sought help from Allah during the Battle of Badr, Allah responded by sending a thousand angels in succession, with five hundred under the leadership of Jibril and five hundred under Mika'il, to aid the believers. This assistance was a sign of glad tidings for the Muslims, bringing peace to their hearts and assuring them that victory comes only from Allah. During the battle, an Ansari man reported hearing a whip-like sound followed by the fall of an idolater, which the Prophet confirmed as a reinforcement from the third heaven. The Prophet expressed great honor for those who participated in Badr, comparing them to the angels who fought alongside him. Allah’s support was not out of necessity, as He is capable of granting victory without the angels, but it was a test for the believers. The defeat of the disbelievers by the hands of the believers was more humiliating for them, as exemplified by the death of Abu Jahl and the punishment of Abu Lahab, which was more degrading than natural death or calamities. Allah's might and wisdom in commanding such events show that He grants victory to His messengers and the faithful in this life and the Hereafter.
