Allah instructs the believers in the proper conduct of war, emphasizing courage and patience when facing the enemy. Muslims are commanded to stand firm during battle and avoid running or showing cowardice. They are reminded to remain steadfast in their faith, remembering Allah, seeking His support, and trusting in Him for victory. The Prophet Muhammad, after waiting for the sun to decline during a battle, advised not to wish for confrontation but to be patient if it occurs, reminding them that Paradise lies under the shadows of swords. He prayed for victory, invoking Allah as the Revealer of the Book and the Defeater of the Confederates. Muslims are also commanded not to dispute amongst themselves, as disunity can lead to defeat. Through their unwavering obedience to Allah and His Messenger, the Companions achieved remarkable victories, spreading Islam across the world in a short time, despite being outnumbered by the powerful empires of their time.
