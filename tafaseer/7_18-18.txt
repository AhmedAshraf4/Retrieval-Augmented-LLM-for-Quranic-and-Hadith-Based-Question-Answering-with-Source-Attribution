The command to "Get out from this (Paradise), Madh'uman Madhura" refers to Iblis being expelled from Paradise, belittled and despised. He is cast out in disgrace, as he is cursed and banished for refusing to prostrate to Adam. This expulsion is further emphasized by the statement that anyone who follows Iblis will be led to Hell, a fitting punishment for their disobedience. Similar themes are echoed in a previous verse, where Allah warns Iblis that those who follow him will be recompensed in Hell, and he is allowed to mislead them through his voice and promises, but Allah’s servants remain under His protection, beyond Iblis' influence.
