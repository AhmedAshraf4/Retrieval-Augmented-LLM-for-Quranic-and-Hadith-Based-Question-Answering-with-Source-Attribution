The Muslim believes in all that Allah revealed and all the Prophets, without distinguishing between them. Allah commanded the believers to accept what was sent to Muhammad and what was revealed to previous Prophets, some named and others not, and to avoid saying, "We believe in some but reject others," which is disbelief. The People of the Book used to read the Torah in Hebrew and translate it into Arabic, but the believers were instructed to say, "We believe in Allah and in what has been sent down to us," without fully accepting or rejecting their words. The Messenger of Allah often recited this affirmation along with, "We believe in Allah and bear witness that we are Muslims" during voluntary prayer. The Children of Israel were divided into twelve tribes, each descending from one of Jacob's sons, among whom Allah sent Prophets and appointed leaders. Allah commanded the believers to believe in Him, His Books, and His Messengers, including the original Torah and Gospel, though they were not required to implement their rulings.
