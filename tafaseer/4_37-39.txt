Allah condemns those who are stingy with their wealth, failing to spend it on the causes He has commanded, such as supporting parents, relatives, orphans, the poor, neighbors, travelers, and servants. These individuals do not fulfill their duty by sharing their wealth, spreading selfishness instead of generosity. The Messenger of Allah warned that stinginess led to the destruction of those before, causing them to sever ties and commit sins. Those who hide Allah’s blessings are ungrateful, as their wealth is not visibly reflected in their conduct or their giving. Allah has prepared a disgraceful punishment for the ungrateful. Stinginess is compared to kufr, as it involves concealing Allah’s favors. The verse also addresses those who spend only to show off, without sincerity for Allah’s sake. Allah knows the intentions behind their actions, whether good or evil. If these people had faith in Allah and the Last Day, they would spend their wealth sincerely, seeking Allah’s pleasure.
