The verse, known as the Ayah of the Sword, refers to the end of the four-month grace period during which fighting the idolators was prohibited. Once these months passed, Allah commanded the believers to fight the idolators wherever they found them, except in the Sacred Area. The verse instructed capturing or killing them and laying siege to their strongholds, ambushing them strategically, and gathering intelligence to limit their options to either death or embracing Islam. If they repented, prayed, and gave Zakah, they were to be left unharmed, as Allah is forgiving and merciful. This directive, used by Abu Bakr As-Siddiq to justify fighting those who refused Zakah, emphasizes the key pillars of Islam: testimony, prayer, and Zakah. The verse abrogated all peace treaties with idolators, as explained by scholars like Ad-Dahhak bin Muzahim and Ibn `Abbas, marking a shift from previous agreements and promises of safety.
