Allah commanded Muhammad to turn his face toward Al-Masjid Al-Haram for prayer, fulfilling the Prophet’s wish and establishing the Kabah as the Qiblah. This command was repeated to confirm that facing the Kabah is the truth from Allah, not influenced by the People of the Book. The change from Bayt Al-Maqdis to the Kabah refuted Jewish claims and honored the Arabs, who revered the Kabah. The shift ensured no argument could be raised against the Muslims, as the previous Qiblah could have been used by the Jews to challenge them. Muhammad obeyed both commands, first facing Bayt Al-Maqdis and then the Ka`bah, showing full submission to Allah, and his Ummah follows this guidance. Allah instructed not to fear the doubts of the unjust but to fear Him alone and stated that this ruling completes His blessings and guides the Ummah to what others were led astray from, establishing them as a favored and guided nation.
