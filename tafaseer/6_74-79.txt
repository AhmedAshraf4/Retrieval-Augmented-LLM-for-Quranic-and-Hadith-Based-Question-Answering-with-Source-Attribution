Tafsir Explanation: Ibrahim advised his father Azar against worshipping idols, declaring that they could neither hear, see, nor benefit him in any way. He pointed out that both his father and his people were lost in confusion, following a misguided path. Ibrahim argued that the celestial bodies, such as the star, moon, and sun, could not be gods, as they were subject to a fixed course and disappeared daily. He proclaimed his belief in the One who created the heavens and the earth, rejecting any form of idolatry. Despite his father’s threats, Ibrahim continued to seek forgiveness for him, but when his father died an idolater, Ibrahim ceased praying for him. Ibrahim's debate with his people centered on the futility of idol worship, as he explained that the celestial bodies they revered were simply creations of Allah, who alone deserved worship.
