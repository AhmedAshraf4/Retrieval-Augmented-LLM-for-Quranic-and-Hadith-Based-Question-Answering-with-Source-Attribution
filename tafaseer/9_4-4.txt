Peace treaties with the Muslims, once established, remained valid until their agreed termination date. For those with a treaty without a specified time, it was extended to a maximum of four months, allowing them time to seek sanctuary wherever they could. Any treaty that had a fixed end date ended when that date arrived. Those bound by such agreements were obligated to honor them by not aiding non-Muslims against Muslims. Allah commands the importance of fulfilling these treaties, as the verse underscores that Allah loves those who uphold their promises.
