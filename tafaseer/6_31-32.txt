Tafsir Explanation: The disbelievers will regret their actions when the Hour suddenly arrives, realizing their neglect of good deeds and the consequences of their evil ones. They will express sorrow, saying, "Alas for us that we gave no thought to it." Their burdens, the weight of their misdeeds, will be visibly carried on their backs, symbolizing the evil they bear. Upon entering the grave, an ugly figure representing their deeds will accompany them, showing the ugliness of their actions through a foul odor and dirty clothes. This figure will remain with them, carrying them until they are resurrected, where their deeds will lead them to the Fire. The life of this world is described as fleeting amusement, but the Hereafter is far better for those who have Taqwa, emphasizing the value of righteous actions over worldly distractions.
