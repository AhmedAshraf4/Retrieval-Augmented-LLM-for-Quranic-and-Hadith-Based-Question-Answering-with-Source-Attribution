Tafsīr Explanation: Allah has sovereignty over the heavens, the earth, and everything within them, and He observes both apparent actions and what is concealed in the hearts of His servants, no matter how small. He holds His servants accountable for both their deeds and the hidden intentions within their hearts. The Companions were deeply concerned when this was revealed, fearing that even their hidden thoughts would be reckoned and diminish their good deeds. When they expressed their worry, the Prophet Muhammad advised them to say, "We hear and we obey," and seek forgiveness from Allah. This response led to the abrogation of the previous verse and the revelation of a new verse that alleviated the burden, stating that Allah does not burden a soul beyond its capacity. Allah forgives what is forgotten or done in error, and the believers are only held accountable for their actions. Additionally, Allah pardoned His followers for internal thoughts as long as they were not acted upon or spoken. The Prophet Muhammad also explained that Allah does not record thoughts as sins unless they are carried out, and intentions for good deeds are rewarded even if not fulfilled.
