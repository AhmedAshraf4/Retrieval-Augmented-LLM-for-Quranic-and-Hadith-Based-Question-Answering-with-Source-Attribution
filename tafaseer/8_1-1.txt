The verse addresses the issue of the division of spoils of war (Anfal) following the Battle of Badr. Initially, there was a dispute among the Muslim fighters over the share of the spoils. Some claimed rights based on their actions, such as collecting the spoils, while others argued based on their roles in defeating the enemy or protecting the Prophet. The verse was revealed to clarify that the Anfal belong to Allah and His Messenger, and the division is ultimately under their authority. The Prophet Muhammad was instructed to settle the disputes and divide the spoils justly among the Muslims. The division followed the guidance of the Prophet, who would typically give a quarter or a third of the spoils to those involved in a surprise attack or confrontation. The verse further commands the believers to fear Allah, reconcile their differences, and obey the Messenger in matters of division.
