The verse calls on the believers to answer Allah and His Messenger when they are called to something that will give them life. This includes responding to the call to Jihad, which grants strength after weakness and victory over the enemy. The Prophet Muhammad once rebuked Abu Sa'id bin Al-Mu'alla for not answering him when he was called during prayer, reminding him of this command. The verse also emphasizes that Allah can intervene between a person and their heart, preventing belief or disbelief as He wills. This power of Allah over hearts is further illustrated in the Hadiths, where the Prophet often prayed for his heart to remain firm on faith, acknowledging that all hearts are in Allah's control, and He can direct them as He wishes.
