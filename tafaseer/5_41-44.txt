Tafsir Explanation: The revealed verses address the Jews and hypocrites who reject Allah's law while pretending to follow it. They distort the teachings in their scripture, such as changing the punishment for adultery from stoning to flogging, hoping to manipulate the Prophet's judgment in their favor. The Prophet, when consulted about a case of adultery, demanded the Jews present the Torah. When they concealed the stoning verse, one of them admitted the truth, and the Prophet ruled that the adulterers be stoned, in line with the Torah. The verses criticize their reluctance to follow the law of Allah, highlighting their preference for falsehood, bribes, and their lustful desires, and declare that those who do not judge by Allah's revelation are disbelievers. The passage also emphasizes that the Torah, given to Moses, was guidance and light, and those who truly followed it did not alter it, unlike the hypocrites who sought judgments based on personal interests rather than divine law.
