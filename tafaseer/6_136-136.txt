Tafsir Explanation: Allah criticizes the idolaters who invented innovations, associating partners with Him in His creation, despite Him being the sole Creator of all things. They would designate parts of their produce and cattle as belonging to Allah and others to their idols, claiming that what was designated for Allah could not reach their idols, while what was designated for the idols would reach Allah. If the land or produce assigned to Allah was mixed with that for the idols, they would consider the idol’s share as poor and transfer it to the idol’s portion. Additionally, they assigned sacred value to animals like the Bahirah, Sa'ibah, Wasilah, and Ham, claiming they did so to seek closeness to Allah. However, these practices were a clear distortion of the rightful divisions, as they would sacrifice animals for idols without mentioning Allah's name. Allah condemns this behavior, declaring their judgments as evil, as they wrongly divided what belonged to Him and misused His creations.
