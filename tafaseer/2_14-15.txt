When the hypocrites meet the believers, they openly claim faith to deceive them and to share in any benefit the believers may gain, but when they withdraw to their devils—meaning their leaders and allies among disbelieving Jews, hypocrites, and idolaters—they declare loyalty to them and admit that their display of belief was only mockery meant to mislead the believers. Their statement that they were “only mocking” refers to deliberately ridiculing and deceiving the followers of Muhammad. In response, Allah mocks them in a manner of just recompense, not by play, but by repaying their deceit with a punishment resembling their act: He grants them respite, prolongs their apparent ease, and leaves them to advance further into rebellion. This divine mockery is fulfilled by allowing them to persist in misguidance while believing themselves secure, until their confusion deepens and they are unable to recognize guidance or escape disbelief, because their hearts are sealed and their perception is blinded, causing them to wander blindly in the transgression that has overtaken them.
