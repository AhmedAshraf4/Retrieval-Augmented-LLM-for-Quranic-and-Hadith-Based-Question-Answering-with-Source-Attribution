This ayah commands the pilgrims to stand at Arafat and then proceed to Al-Muzdalifah to remember Allah at Al-Mashar Al-Haram, contrasting with the pre-Islamic practice of Quraysh, who stayed in Al-Muzdalifah claiming they were the people of Allah's Town. The Prophet Muhammad was instructed to stand at Arafat and then depart from there, as opposed to the Quraysh's previous custom. This was fulfilled when the Prophet performed the pilgrimage by standing at Arafat, as reported by those who observed him during the event. The ayah also encourages seeking forgiveness from Allah, who is described as forgiving and merciful, emphasizing the importance of asking for His pardon after acts of worship. The Prophet would regularly seek forgiveness after prayer, teaching supplications such as asking for Allah’s forgiveness and mercy, including the famous supplication for forgiveness where one acknowledges their faults and Allah’s sovereignty, with the promise of Paradise for those who sincerely recite it.
