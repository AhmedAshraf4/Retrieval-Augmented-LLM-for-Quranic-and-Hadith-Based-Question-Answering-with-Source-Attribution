Tafsir Explanation: The Quraysh, after their defeat at Badr, sought to avenge their losses and turn their wealth to oppose Muhammad by funding a campaign against him. Abu Sufyan, having returned with the caravan, was approached by those who had lost family members in the battle. They used their wealth to fight Muhammad, believing they could extinguish the truth. However, their spending would only lead to grief as their money would not help them, and they would eventually be defeated. Despite their efforts to suppress the message, Allah would ensure His Light prevailed. The verse reveals that the disbelievers' wealth, used to hinder the truth, would be of no benefit to them and would only bring them anguish. Ultimately, those who disbelieve would be gathered to Hell, facing eternal punishment. This trial serves to distinguish the wicked from the righteous, with the former being cast into Hell as losers in both this life and the Hereafter.
