The Prophet asked his companions for their opinion on how to deal with the prisoners of war from Badr, stating that Allah had given them victory over them. `Umar bin Al-Khattab suggested executing the prisoners, but the Prophet did not respond to him and repeated the question. Abu Bakr As-Siddiq recommended pardoning them and accepting ransom, which led to the Prophet agreeing to this suggestion. Allah revealed that, had it not been for a prior decree allowing the spoils of war and ransom, the Muslims would have faced severe punishment for taking captives. This decree was known to the Prophet, who had been granted specific privileges, such as the lawful acceptance of war booty, which was not allowed for previous nations. The Muslims then accepted ransom for the prisoners, and the Prophet set a fixed amount for them. The handling of prisoners was ultimately left to the leader’s discretion, with options including execution, ransom, or exchange, as demonstrated in various instances such as the case of Bani Qurayzah or the exchange of prisoners involving a woman and her daughter.
