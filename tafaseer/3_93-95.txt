Tafsir Explanation: A group of Jews asked the Prophet about four matters that only a prophet would know. The Prophet agreed to answer, but on the condition that they pledge to follow him in Islam if his answers were correct. They agreed. The Prophet then explained that Isra'il (Jacob) had vowed to prohibit camel meat and camel milk for himself after an illness, and they confirmed this was true. He then clarified that the male discharge is thick and white, while the female discharge is thin and yellow; whichever fluid predominates determines the child's sex. They acknowledged this, and the Prophet called Allah to witness. The Prophet further explained that while he sleeps, his heart remains awake, which they again confirmed. Finally, when they asked about his supporter among the angels, the Prophet identified Jibril (Gabriel) as his Wali, which caused the Jews to reject him. Allah revealed a verse to challenge their disbelief, stating that all food was lawful to the Children of Israel except what Isra'il prohibited before the Tawrah was revealed. The Jews' denial of this, and their refusal to follow `Isa (Jesus) or Muhammad, despite evidence of abrogation in their own scriptures, was criticized. Allah then emphasized that those who lie about Him are wrongdoers, and affirmed the truth of the Qur'an and the call to follow the religion of Ibrahim, the Hanif, which is the correct path.
