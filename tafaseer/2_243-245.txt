The residents of the village of Dawardan, who fled from a plague, sought refuge in a fertile valley. Allah commanded them to die instantly, and they perished, their bodies decaying. After a long time, the Prophet Hizqil (Ezekiel) passed by and prayed to Allah to resurrect them. Allah commanded their bones to come together, be covered with flesh, and for their souls to return to their bodies. They were brought back to life and praised Allah. This event serves as proof of physical resurrection on the Day of Judgment. It also demonstrates that no precaution can avert death, as these people sought to escape death but met it anyway. Despite their efforts, death was inevitable, and this incident teaches that destiny cannot be altered by human actions.
