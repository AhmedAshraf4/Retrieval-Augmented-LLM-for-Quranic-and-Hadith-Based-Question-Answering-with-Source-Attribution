Martyrs who die in the way of Allah are alive with Him and provided for in Paradise. Their souls are inside green birds with lamps, hanging under Allah's Throne, roaming Paradise freely before returning to their lamps. When Allah asks if they desire anything, they express a wish to be returned to their bodies to be martyred again, which Allah declines, knowing they have no other desires. The martyrs enjoy the bounties of Paradise and are free of fear or sorrow. Allah conveys this to their brethren, urging them to persist in jihad. On the day of Hamra' Al-Asad, after being wounded in battle, the Muslims responded to Allah's call and marched to confront the idolators, demonstrating their strength and faith. Despite threats, they said, "Allah is sufficient for us," and returned with Allah's grace, free from harm. Allah advises believers not to fear the threats of Shaytan and reminds them that His help will ensure their victory.
