Musa told Fir'awn that he was a messenger sent by the Creator, Lord, and King of all things. He emphasized that it was his duty to speak only the truth from Allah, given his knowledge of Allah’s power. Musa presented clear proof from his Lord, claiming that the evidence he brought confirmed his message. He demanded the release of the Children of Israel, allowing them to worship Allah, their Lord, as they were descendants of the honorable Prophet Israel (Jacob). Fir'awn rejected Musa's claim and demanded a sign to prove his truthfulness. He challenged Musa to show the evidence if he truly spoke the truth.
