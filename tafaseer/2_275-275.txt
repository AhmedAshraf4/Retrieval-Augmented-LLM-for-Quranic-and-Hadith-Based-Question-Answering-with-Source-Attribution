Tafsir Explanation: Those who deal in Riba (usury) will be resurrected in a state of madness and seizures, akin to someone possessed by Shaytan. This condition reflects the punishment they face for unlawfully acquiring others' wealth. The disbelievers falsely equated trade with Riba, claiming both were similar, but Allah clarified that trade is permitted while Riba is forbidden due to its harm. Those who repent after realizing the prohibition of Riba will be forgiven for past actions, but those who persist after knowing its unlawfulness will face punishment and eternal damnation. The Prophet also annulled past Riba cases from the pre-Islamic era without demanding the return of interest. Furthermore, actions that resemble Riba, such as certain forms of sharecropping and trade in unripe produce, were also prohibited to avoid any involvement in usurious practices. Riba is among the gravest sins, with the Prophet cursing those involved in it, including the payers, receivers, witnesses, and recorders of the contract.
