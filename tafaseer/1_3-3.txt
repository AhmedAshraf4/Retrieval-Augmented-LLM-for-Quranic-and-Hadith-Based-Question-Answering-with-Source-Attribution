“The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.” These two Names describe Allah’s mercy and forgiveness and were already explained with the opening invocation. They are stated after “the Lord of all worlds” to combine warning with encouragement. Lordship implies authority, accountability, and punishment for wrongdoing, while being Most Gracious and Most Merciful affirms abundant forgiveness and compassion. Allah declares that He is forgiving and merciful, yet His punishment is severe, and He is swift in retribution while remaining forgiving and merciful. The Messenger Muhammad said that if a believer fully knew the punishment with Allah, no one would hope for Paradise, and if a disbeliever fully knew the mercy with Allah, no one would despair of His mercy.
