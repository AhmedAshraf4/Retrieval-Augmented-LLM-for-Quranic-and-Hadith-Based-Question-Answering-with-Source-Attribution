Tafsir Explanation: Allah commands that the Messenger must be obeyed by those to whom he is sent, and obedience is granted only by Allah's leave. The verse emphasizes that when sinners wrong themselves, they must come to the Messenger for forgiveness, where Allah will pardon them. Faith is not complete unless people turn to the Prophet for judgment in all their disputes, accepting his decisions without resistance. The Prophet's judgment is the truth, and believers must fully submit to it. This was illustrated when Az-Zubayr and an Ansari disagreed over water rights, and the Prophet gave Az-Zubayr the decision, even though the Ansari objected, leading to further clarification. Additionally, when a dispute was taken to Abu Bakr and then `Umar, the refusal to accept the Prophet's judgment resulted in the death of the one who rejected the decision, demonstrating the importance of accepting the Prophet's rulings as a condition of faith.
