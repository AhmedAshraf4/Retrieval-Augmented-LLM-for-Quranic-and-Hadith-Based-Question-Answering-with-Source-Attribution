The Muttaqin, Allah's pious servants, are those who proclaim their belief in Allah, His Book, and His Messenger, and seek forgiveness for their sins, asking for mercy and protection from the Fire. They demonstrate patience in obedience, truthfulness in their faith, and submission to Allah’s commands. They spend from their wealth on acts of obedience, including helping others in need. They also seek Allah's forgiveness during the last part of the night, a time of great virtue, as evidenced by the actions of the Prophet’s companions. Allah descends to the lowest heaven in the last third of every night, calling for those who ask, invoke, or seek forgiveness. This time is significant for prayer and repentance, as shown by the example of Ya'qub, who waited until the last part of the night to ask forgiveness for his children, and by the actions of the Prophet and his companions, such as Abdullah bin Umar, who prayed and sought forgiveness during this time.
