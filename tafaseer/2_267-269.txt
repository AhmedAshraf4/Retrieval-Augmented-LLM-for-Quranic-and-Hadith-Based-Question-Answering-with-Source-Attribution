Allah commands believers to spend charity from the purest and finest of their wealth, including the fruits and vegetables He has provided. He prohibits spending impure or dishonest money, as He is pure and accepts only that which is pure. This command was revealed after some of the Ansar added inferior dates to those they hung for the poor in the mosque, thinking it was acceptable. Allah instructs them not to give what they would not accept for themselves. Allah is rich and has no need for charity, but it is meant to reduce the distance between the rich and the poor. Satan tempts people with the fear of poverty and encourages immoral behavior, but Allah promises forgiveness and bounty for those who spend in His way. Allah also grants wisdom to whomever He wills, helping them to understand the Qur'an’s deeper meanings. Only those with sound minds and understanding can benefit from this guidance.
