Tafsir Explanation: Allah warns those who revert to disbelief after having believed and persist in disbelief until death, stating that their repentance will not be accepted. Even if they offer the earth's full gold as ransom on the Day of Judgment, it will not be accepted. This applies to those who die as disbelievers; their good deeds, regardless of how generous they appear, will be worthless. The Prophet was asked about a man who had been generous, but he explained that unless a person sincerely repents and asks for forgiveness from Allah during their lifetime, such actions will not benefit them. Those who reject belief and die in disbelief will find no intercession, compensation, or redemption for their actions, and they will face painful torment with no one to help them escape Allah’s punishment. The disbeliever will wish to ransom themselves with all the wealth of the earth but will find that it is of no use, as they had previously been offered easier opportunities to repent but refused.
