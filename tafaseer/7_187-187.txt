The Quraysh, who denied the Last Hour, questioned the Prophet about its timing, asking when it would occur. Allah revealed that only He knows the appointed time, and no one else, not even the closest angels or messengers, has knowledge of it. The knowledge of the Hour is so heavy that it is hidden in the heavens and the earth, and its sudden arrival will catch people off guard, as exemplified by the Prophet's description of everyday activities ceasing abruptly when the Hour begins. The Prophet also described signs leading up to the Hour, including widespread confusion and violence, with killing becoming common. This shows that, while the Prophet knew of the signs, the exact time remains with Allah alone. Even great figures like Ibrahim, Musa, and Isa, when asked about the Hour, admitted ignorance of its timing, focusing instead on what they knew of the signs leading to it. The Prophet was commanded to refer all knowledge of the Hour to Allah, affirming that the majority of people do not understand this truth.
