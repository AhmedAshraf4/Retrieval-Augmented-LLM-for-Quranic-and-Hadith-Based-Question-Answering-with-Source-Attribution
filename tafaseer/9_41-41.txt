The verse emphasizes that all Muslims, regardless of their physical condition, age, or wealth, are obligated to join in the jihad, as commanded by Allah. It was revealed in the context of the Battle of Tabuk, where the believers were called to march and fight the disbelievers, including the Romans. Some people, citing old age or illness, thought they were excused from participating, but this verse made clear that there was no such excuse. Abu Talhah exemplified this obedience, continuing his jihad even in his old age, eventually dying in battle. The verse also emphasizes the importance of striving with both wealth and life for the sake of Allah, promising that such sacrifices are better in both this life and the Hereafter. Allah assures the believers that the hardships they may dislike could bring them greater benefits, and that His wisdom surpasses their understanding. The reward for those who engage in jihad is a guarantee of Paradise or, if they return, the spoils of war.
