Allah made the Kabah a place of resort and safety for mankind, where visitors are protected from enemies and conflict. People visit it repeatedly, fulfilling the prayer of Abraham for hearts to be drawn to the House. The Kabah was first built by Abraham and Ishmael, with Abraham standing on a stone, the Maqam, while Ishmael handed him stones to construct the walls. The Maqam became a designated place of prayer, where the Prophet performed Tawaf and prayed two Rakahs behind it. Abraham’s footprints remained visible on the stone, marking the spot where he completed the building. The Maqam was originally next to the Kabah’s wall and was later moved near Al-Hijr by Umar bin Al-Khattab. Whoever enters the House is secure, and the Kabah remains a blessed refuge, honoring both its construction by Abraham and its role as a sanctuary for worshippers.
