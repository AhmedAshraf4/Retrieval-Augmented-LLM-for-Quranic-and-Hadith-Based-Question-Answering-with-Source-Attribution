The pursuit of water game, such as fish, is permitted for those in a state of Ihram, whether it is eaten fresh or salted. The use of water game for food extends to both those traveling by sea and those inland. A notable incident occurred when the Prophet’s army, led by Abu Ubaydah, was stranded without food and survived for eighteen days after consuming a giant fish they found by the shore. In contrast, hunting land game is prohibited during Ihram. If a person in Ihram hunts land game intentionally, they must pay expiation, and they cannot consume the meat. Even if it is hunted by someone else, a Muhrim is not allowed to eat it if it was specifically for them. A man once gave the Prophet a zebra as a gift, but the Prophet returned it, explaining it was because they were in Ihram. If land game is hunted by someone not in Ihram, and no Muhrim directly assisted in its capture, the Muhrim may eat it. Allah has set the Ka'bah, the Sacred Month, and symbols like the garlanded animals as a means of security and benefit for mankind. These symbols, including the Ka'bah, were revered even before Islam, marking sacred rituals like Hajj. Allah’s perfect knowledge encompasses all that is in the heavens and the earth, ensuring that every action, whether hidden or revealed, is known to Him. He is both severe in punishment for those who defy Him and merciful to those who repent. The Prophet’s duty is to convey the message, and Allah will hold all accountable for their deeds, visible or concealed.
