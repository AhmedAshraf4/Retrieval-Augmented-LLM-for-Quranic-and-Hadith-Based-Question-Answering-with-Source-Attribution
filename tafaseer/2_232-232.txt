This verse addresses the prohibition of preventing a divorced woman from remarrying her ex-husband if both parties agree after the Iddah (waiting period) has ended. The case is illustrated by Maqil bin Yasar, whose sister was divorced, and when her ex-husband sought to remarry her after her Iddah, Maqil refused. Allah revealed this verse to prevent guardians from blocking the remarriage if both the woman and her former husband desire it. The verse also affirms that a woman cannot give herself in marriage; she requires a Wali (guardian) to act on her behalf. It highlights the importance of obedience to Allah’s command in matters of marriage, as it is purer and more virtuous for the believers. The incident also teaches that Allah knows what is best for His creation, even when people may not understand the full benefits of His commands.
