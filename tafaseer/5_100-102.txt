Tafsīr Explanation: Allah instructs the believers not to equate the bad with the good, even if the bad seems appealing. The permissible, even if limited, is better than the abundance of what is forbidden. Believers are urged to avoid the impermissible and rely on what is lawful for success in both this world and the Hereafter. Allah then advises against asking unnecessary questions, as they may lead to troubles or difficult rulings. If a question is asked while the Qur’an is being revealed, the answer will be provided. However, asking about matters without clear rulings may result in prohibitions, as seen with the question about whether Hajj is required every year. Some before the believers asked questions that led them to disbelief, as they did not seek guidance but only to mock.
