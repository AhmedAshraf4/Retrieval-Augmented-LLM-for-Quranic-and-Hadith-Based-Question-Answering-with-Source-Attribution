Tafsir Explanation: The believers are those who, when Allah is mentioned, experience a fear in their hearts, leading them to fulfill His commands and avoid His prohibitions. This includes performing the obligatory acts of worship like prayer, paying zakah, and maintaining sincerity in faith. When the Qur'an is recited, their faith increases, and they put their trust solely in Allah, recognizing that everything happens by His will alone. They are characterized by their commitment to the prayer, fulfilling it at its proper time with proper purification, bowing, and prostration. They also spend from what Allah has provided, including the obligatory zakah. These actions reflect true belief, for which they will be rewarded with varying ranks and forgiveness from Allah. The highest ranks are reserved for those who truly follow the path of faith, as seen in the example of the Prophets, with the righteous, like Abu Bakr and Umar, among those with the highest status in Paradise.
