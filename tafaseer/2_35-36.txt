Allah honored Adam by commanding the angels to prostrate before him, and they obeyed except Iblis. Adam was allowed to dwell in Paradise with his wife and eat freely, except from one forbidden tree, which served as a test. Hawwa was created before Adam entered Paradise, formed from his rib while he slept, and she was made as comfort for him. After Allah joined them, Adam and Hawwa were warned not to approach the tree or become wrongdoers. Shaytan caused them to slip because of the tree, leading them to eat from it, and as a result they lost their clothing, secure dwelling, and ease. Allah then removed them from the state they were in and commanded them to descend, establishing enmity between them, with the earth as their dwelling, sustenance, and enjoyment for a limited time. Adam was created very tall, and when his covering fell away after eating from the tree, he fled in shame until Allah called him, and Adam admitted his shyness. Adam’s stay in Paradise was brief, and his descent marked the transition from comfort and protection to earthly life and trial.
