The Qur'an, revealed to Muhammad by Allah, is a book sent to warn the disbelievers and remind the believers. Allah commands Muhammad not to doubt the message or hesitate in delivering it, as he is tasked with conveying the Qur'an clearly and without fear. He is to remain patient, as the earlier strong-willed messengers did. The people are urged to follow the guidance brought by the unlettered Prophet, who received the revelation from the Lord of everything. They are warned not to follow other sources of authority besides Allah, as doing so would lead them astray. Despite the clarity of the message, most people will not believe, and many will even attribute partners to Allah.
