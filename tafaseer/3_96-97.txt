The Ka'bah, built by Ibrahim (Abraham) in Bakkah (Makkah), is the first house of worship for all people, where they perform Tawaf, prayer, and I'tikaf. It is blessed and a guidance for mankind. Makkah, also known as Bakkah, is a sanctuary where people find safety, a refuge for those in fear, as it was during the time of Jahiliyyah. The Maqam of Ibrahim, the stone on which Ibrahim stood while building the Ka'bah with his son Isma'il, marks a clear sign of Allah’s blessing. Makkah is a sacred area where no harm can be done, no trees cut, no game hunted, and no bloodshed is allowed. It is unlawful for anyone to carry weapons in Makkah. The duty of performing Hajj to the Ka'bah is a requirement for those able to undertake the journey, which includes having provision and transportation. Denying the obligation of Hajj is considered disbelief.
