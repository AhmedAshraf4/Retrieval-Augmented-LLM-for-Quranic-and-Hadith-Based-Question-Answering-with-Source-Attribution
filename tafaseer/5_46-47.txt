Tafsir Explanation: Allah sent `Isa, the son of Maryam, after the Prophets of the Children of Israel, confirming the Tawrah that preceded him. He was given the Injil, which contained guidance and light, directing people to the truth and resolving disputes. The Injil also affirmed the Tawrah, though it abrogated some of its rulings, such as allowing things previously forbidden. The Injil served as guidance and admonition for those with Taqwa, warning against sin and error. Allah commanded the people of the Injil to judge by what He revealed in it, which includes the announcement of the coming Prophet Muhammad, whom they were to believe in and follow. Those who do not judge by Allah’s revelations are rebellious, as seen in the case of the Christians, who failed to adhere to the Injil’s commands.
