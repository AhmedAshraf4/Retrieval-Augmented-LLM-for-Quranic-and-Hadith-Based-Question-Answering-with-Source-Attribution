Tafsir Explanation: Allah encourages the Prophet and the believers to fight against their enemies, assuring them of His support and victory despite being outnumbered. The Prophet used to urge the Companions to fight with the promise of Paradise, as exemplified by `Umayr bin Al-Humam, who, upon hearing this promise, eagerly sought martyrdom and was killed in the battle. Allah stated that a small number of steadfast Muslims could overcome a much larger enemy force—twenty Muslims could defeat two hundred enemies, and a hundred Muslims could defeat a thousand. This initial ruling was later abrogated, as the burden was lightened and the ratio of endurance was adjusted. The obligation to fight was then made easier, allowing Muslims fewer in number to avoid hostilities when facing a much stronger force.
