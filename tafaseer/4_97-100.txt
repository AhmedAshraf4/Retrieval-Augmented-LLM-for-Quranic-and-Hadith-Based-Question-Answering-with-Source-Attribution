Tafsir Explanation: The verse addresses Muslims who resided among disbelievers while being able to migrate to a Muslim community, emphasizing their wrongdoings. Ibn Abbas reported that some Muslims joined idolaters in the Battle of Badr, leading to their deaths among the disbelievers. This verse warns those who choose to remain in such environments without performing Hijrah, condemning them for injustice against themselves. The angels question those who failed to emigrate, asking why they did not leave, to which they reply they were oppressed and unable to move. However, the angels remind them that Allah's earth is vast, implying they had the means to leave. Allah excused those unable to find a way out, describing them as weak and incapable of planning. Allah promises forgiveness for them, as He is pardoning and forgiving. The verse encourages Hijrah by highlighting the rewards for those who migrate for Allah’s cause, even if they die on the way. The reward for Hijrah is tied to the individual's intention, as shown by the story of a man who murdered 100 people and sought repentance by migrating to a righteous land. His proximity to the destination determined his salvation, as the angels of mercy captured his soul when he died closer to the land of goodness.
