Tafsir Explanation: Allah criticizes the believers for dividing into two groups over the fate of hypocrites who had returned to Al-Madinah before the battle of Uhud. One group wanted to kill them, while the other objected, questioning the right to kill those who only lacked Hijrah but still claimed Islam. Allah rebukes this division, stating that those whom He has caused to go astray cannot be guided by anyone. The hypocrites wish for the believers to abandon their faith, but Allah commands not to take them as allies unless they emigrate in the way of Allah. However, if they join a group with whom the Muslims have a treaty or show reluctance to fight, peace must be maintained. Allah allows no way to harm those who seek peace or withdraw from hostilities. The hypocrites secretly support the idolaters, pretending to be with the Muslims while aligning with their enemies when alone. Allah commands that these people be fought unless they offer peace, refrain from fighting, and show true submission.
