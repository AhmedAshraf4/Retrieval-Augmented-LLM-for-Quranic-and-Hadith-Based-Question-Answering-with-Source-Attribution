After the death of Moses, Allah commanded the Children of Israel to dwell in the land, and they were divided into various sects, with some righteous and others not. They were tested through both ease and hardship to guide them back to Allah. However, a later generation inherited the Torah but chose to prioritize the temporary pleasures of this world, even indulging in what was unlawful. They believed that Allah would forgive their sins without repentance and continued in their disobedience. Some among them took bribes for judgment, and others would do the same if given the chance, despite admonishments. Allah had taken a covenant from them to speak the truth about Him, but they violated it, hiding the truth for worldly gain. Allah reminded them that the Hereafter is far better for the righteous, urging them to abandon their indulgence and focus on following His commands. He also praised those who held fast to the Book, upheld prayer, and performed righteous deeds, assuring them that their efforts would not be wasted.
