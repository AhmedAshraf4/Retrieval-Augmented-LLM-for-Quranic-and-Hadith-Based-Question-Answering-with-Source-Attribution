Tafsir Explanation: The terms Bahirah, Sa'ibah, Wasilah, and Ham refer to pre-Islamic practices in which certain animals were designated for idol worship. The Bahirah was a female camel whose milk was reserved for idols, and no one was allowed to milk it. The Sa'ibah was a female camel set free for pasture for idols, with nothing carried on it. The Wasilah referred to a female camel that gave birth to two female offspring consecutively, and it was freed for idol worship. The Ham was a male camel that was freed from work after performing a set number of copulations. These practices were initiated by Amr bin Amir Al-Khuza`i, the first to introduce idol worship to the region, and were later forbidden by Islam. The Prophet Muhammad prohibited such rituals, emphasizing that all wealth and animals provided by Allah are lawful for people. Those who claimed these acts were divinely sanctioned were inventing lies and disregarding the truth revealed by Allah.
