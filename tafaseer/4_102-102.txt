Tafsir Explanation: The Fear prayer is performed when facing danger, such as in battle, with variations depending on the situation. It can involve one, two, or more Rakahs, and may be performed individually or in congregation. During intense conflict, when prayer in congregation isn't possible, each person prays while remaining alert, even while walking or fighting. The prayer can be reduced to one Rakah, as narrated by Ibn Abbas. The Prophet Muhammad led the Fear prayer twice, once in Usfan and once with the Banu Sulaym, during which the followers alternated between guarding and praying. One group would pray one Rak`ah with the Prophet while the other group stood in defense. After completing their prayer, they switched roles. The Prophet emphasized holding weapons during the Fear prayer, as indicated in the verse, although it was allowed to set them aside if rain or illness posed difficulty, while still remaining vigilant. This prayer structure ensures that the worshippers can maintain readiness and devotion even in the midst of danger.
