Tafsir Explanation: Divorce was initially more lenient, allowing a man to take back his wife even after multiple divorces during the Iddah period. This practice was deemed harmful to the wife, so Allah restricted divorce to three instances. After the first two divorces, the husband may either reconcile or release her with kindness. After the third divorce, the woman becomes irrevocably divorced and may not remarry her first husband unless she marries another man and that marriage is consummated. If the wife voluntarily returns part of the dowry, the husband may accept it. In cases of irreconcilable differences, the wife may initiate a divorce (Khul) by returning her dowry, but this must be done with the intention to avoid injustice. Any divorce without justification is considered unlawful for the woman, and the Quran sets clear boundaries to prevent transgressions. Pronouncing three divorces at once is forbidden, as shown when the Prophet condemned such actions. The Prophet also made it clear that a woman cannot return to her first husband after three divorces unless she has married and been consummated by another man, ensuring the marriage was not for the sole purpose of reinstating the original marriage.
