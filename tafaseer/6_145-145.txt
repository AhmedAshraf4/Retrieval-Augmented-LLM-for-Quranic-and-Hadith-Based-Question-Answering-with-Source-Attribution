Allah commands Muhammad to tell those who claim that certain things are prohibited by divine revelation, that there is nothing forbidden in the revealed laws except for dead animals, poured blood, and the flesh of swine. The prohibition of blood refers specifically to poured blood, not blood that remains in the meat. The Hadiths clarify that what Allah has allowed is lawful, and what He has prohibited is unlawful, while anything not mentioned is neutral. For example, Ibn Abbas refutes the claim that donkey meat was forbidden, citing the ayah as evidence. When a sheep of Sawdah bint Zam'ah died, the Prophet Muhammad allowed her to use its tanned skin, reinforcing the principle that what Allah has not prohibited is permissible. If someone is forced to consume what Allah has prohibited due to necessity, without exceeding limits, Allah is forgiving and merciful. This verse contradicts the idolaters' customs of prohibiting certain animals based on their baseless beliefs, such as the Bahirah and Sa'ibah, which Allah did not prohibit.
