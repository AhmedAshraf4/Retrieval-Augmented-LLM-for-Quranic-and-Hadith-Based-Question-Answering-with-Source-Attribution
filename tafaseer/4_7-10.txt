Tafsir Explanation: The idolators used to give inheritance only to adult men, excluding women and children, but Allah revealed that everyone has a share in the inheritance, with portions determined by their relationship to the deceased. A woman whose daughters were left with nothing after their father died sought the Prophet’s help, and this verse was revealed as a response. When inheritance is divided, orphans and poor relatives who do not inherit are also present, and Allah commands that they should receive a share as an act of mercy. The verse on wills warns that those preparing to leave a will should act justly and consider the rights of their heirs, advising against giving too much to charity at the expense of one’s family. The Prophet confirmed this by advising Sa’d bin Abi Waqqas not to give away too much of his wealth. The verse also warns against unjustly consuming the wealth of orphans, likening it to eating fire, with a stern consequence in the hereafter. Those who exploit orphaned wealth or engage in other grave sins, such as murder, usury, and false accusations, will face severe punishment in Hell.
