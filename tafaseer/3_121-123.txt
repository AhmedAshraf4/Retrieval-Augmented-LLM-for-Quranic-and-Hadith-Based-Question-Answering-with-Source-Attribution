Tafsīr Explanation: The Battle of Uhud occurred in Shawwal, in the third year of Hijrah, after the idolators, seeking revenge for their losses at Badr, mobilized an army of three thousand. They were led by Abu Sufyan, with Khalid bin Al-Walid and Ikrimah ibn Abi Jahl commanding the cavalry. The Prophet Muhammad, after consulting his companions, chose to march out to face the enemy, despite the advice of Abdullah bin Ubayy, who suggested staying in Al-Madinah. When the Muslims, numbering around seven hundred, reached Uhud, the Prophet stationed fifty archers on a hill to protect their rear, instructing them to remain at their posts regardless of the battle's outcome. The Quraysh army, positioned with their cavalry on both flanks, attacked, and the battle unfolded with intense fighting. At one point, two Muslim groups, Bani Harithah and Bani Salamah, wavered, but Allah supported them, ensuring their victory, as He had done at Badr. This reminder emphasized that victory is granted by Allah, not by numbers or resources.
