Allah commands Dhikr after the Fear prayer, emphasizing it more than other prayers due to the movement and disruption of the usual prayer structure in such a situation. This remembrance is to be done in all states—standing, sitting, or lying down—until safety and calm are restored. Once free from danger, the prayer must be performed with full devotion, completing all its obligatory actions like bowing and prostration. Allah enjoins prayers at fixed times, just like Hajj. Allah also encourages persistence in pursuing the enemy despite injuries, highlighting that both sides suffer similarly, but believers have hope for Allah's reward, victory, and aid, which the enemy lacks. Allah is All-Knowing and All-Wise in His decisions.
