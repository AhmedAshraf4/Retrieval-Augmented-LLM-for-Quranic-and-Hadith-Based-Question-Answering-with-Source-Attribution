Tafsir Explanation: Unintentional oaths, mentioned earlier in Surat Al-Baqarah, refer to statements like "No by Allah" or "Yes, by Allah" made unintentionally. For deliberate oaths, the expiation involves feeding ten poor people with an average of what one feeds their family, or clothing them, each with a suitable garment, or freeing a believing slave. If none of these are possible, fasting for three days is required. The options are presented from easiest to hardest: feeding the poor is the easiest, followed by clothing, then freeing a slave. The expiation serves as a legal remedy for broken oaths. The verse emphasizes fulfilling the expiation and protecting one's oaths, with Allah clarifying His signs to encourage gratitude.
