Tafsir Explanation: Hatib bin Abi Balta'ah wrote a letter to the Quraysh, informing them of the Prophet’s impending march towards Makkah. The Prophet was told by Allah of this betrayal, and he sent a Companion to retrieve the letter. When Hatib was confronted, he admitted to his actions. Umar bin Al-Khattab proposed executing him for betraying Allah, His Messenger, and the believers, but the Prophet pardoned Hatib, citing his participation in the Battle of Badr and Allah’s forgiveness for its participants. Although this incident prompted the revelation, the prohibition of betrayal applies broadly, covering both minor and major sins that harm others. The ayah underscores that wealth and children are trials from Allah, meant to test one’s obedience. Allah rewards those who are devoted to Him above worldly attachments, as stated in other verses. The love for the Prophet is essential for faith, surpassing all other attachments, including to oneself, family, and wealth.
