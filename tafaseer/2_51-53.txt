The Children of Israel worshipped the calf while Moses went to the appointed meeting with his Lord for forty nights, after they were delivered from Pharaoh and crossed the sea safely. God forgave them for the worship of the calf and reminded them of this favor. After the destruction of earlier generations, God gave Moses the Scripture, the Torah, together with the Criterion that distinguishes truth from falsehood and guidance from misguidance. This giving of the Scripture occurred after their deliverance and was meant to serve as clear insight, guidance, and mercy so that they would be rightly guided and remember.
