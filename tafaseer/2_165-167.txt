Tafsir Explanation: Allah describes the condition of polytheists, both in this life and the Hereafter. They assign rivals to Allah, worshiping them and loving them as they love Allah, despite Allah having no equal or partner. The believers, in contrast, love Allah more deeply and worship Him alone, recognizing His greatness. On the Day of Judgment, the polytheists will witness the torment that awaits them, realizing too late that all power belongs to Allah. Those they worshiped will disown them, with angels and jinn declaring their innocence, stating they were not worshiped by the disbelievers. The false deities, including jinn and idols, will reject their worshipers and become enemies to them, showing them their regrets as their deeds vanish like ashes. The disbelievers will wish for a return to life to disown their false gods, but this is a lie; if given another chance, they would return to their sinful ways. In the end, they will never escape the Fire, and all their efforts will be in vain, as their works are shown to them as mere regret and loss.
