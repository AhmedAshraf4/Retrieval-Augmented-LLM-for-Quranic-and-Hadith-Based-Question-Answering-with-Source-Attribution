In their hearts is a disease refers to doubt in religion, not a physical illness, and Allah increased their disease by increasing that doubt and its consequences. This increase manifested as deeper deviation, disgrace, and shameful behavior because they persisted in lying. The cause stated is their continual falsehood, which includes denying unseen truth while outwardly claiming belief. Some hypocrites were known by name, including a group who plotted during the expedition of Tabuk to frighten the Prophet’s camel at night so he would fall from a hill; Allah exposed this plot, and their names were given to Hudhayfah. Others were not individually identified, because the Prophet was informed of their traits rather than every person, and many concealed their hypocrisy. The most prominent hypocrite was Abdullah bin Ubayy bin Salul, whose hypocrisy was confirmed by Zayd bin Arqam. Despite this, the Prophet did not order his killing, so it would not be said that he killed his own Companions, and he prayed over him at his death, stating that he had been given a choice and chose to pray, even expressing willingness to ask forgiveness repeatedly if it would benefit him.
