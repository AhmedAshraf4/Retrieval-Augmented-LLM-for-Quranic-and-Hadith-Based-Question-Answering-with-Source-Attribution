“You we worship, and You we ask for help” states exclusive worship and exclusive reliance. Worship means complete submission joined with utmost love, humility, and fear, and it is directed to God alone, negating all forms of polytheism. Seeking help is directed to God alone, negating any claim of independent power or strength by the servant and affirming that all affairs are under His control. Worship is mentioned before seeking help because worship is the objective and help is the means to carry it out. The wording shifts from speaking about God to directly addressing Him, because after praising Him, the servant stands before Him in direct supplication. This statement combines devotion and reliance and represents the core of the religion. In the prayer, God responds that this statement is shared between Him and the servant, and that the servant will receive what is requested.
