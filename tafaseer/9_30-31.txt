Allah instructs the believers to fight the polytheists, disbelieving Jews, and Christians for their false beliefs. The Jews claimed that Uzayr was the son of God, while the Christians falsely elevated Isa. Allah rejects these claims as lies, emphasizing their resemblance to the misguidance of past nations. The Jews and Christians took their rabbis and monks as lords, obeying them in matters of prohibition and permission, which the Prophet Muhammad clarified to `Adi bin Hatim, explaining that by following the rabbis' decrees, they were in effect worshipping them. Allah commands that worship be directed only to Him, exalted above any association or partners. None has the right to be worshipped except Allah, who alone determines what is lawful and unlawful.
