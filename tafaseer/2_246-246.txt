Tafsir Explanation: The Children of Israel, after the time of Moses, strayed from the right path, some even worshipping idols. Despite this, Allah sent prophets to guide them back to righteousness and enforce the Torah’s laws. However, their continued disobedience led to their enemies overpowering them, resulting in the loss of many lives and the capture of large portions of their land. At one point, their enemies even took the Tabut and the Torah, leaving only a few who had memorized it. Prophethood ceased among them until a woman from the lineage of Levi, who had been praying for a child, gave birth to a boy named Shamwil, meaning “Allah has heard my pleas.” Shamwil grew up to be a righteous man, and when he reached the age of prophethood, Allah commanded him to call his people to worship Him alone. The Israelites, having lost their kingship, asked Shamwil to appoint a king so they could fight their enemies. Shamwil warned them that if Allah appointed a king, they must fulfill their vow to fight under him. Despite their earlier plea to fight for Allah, when the time came, most of them refused, with only a few keeping their promise, and Allah knew the wrongdoers.
