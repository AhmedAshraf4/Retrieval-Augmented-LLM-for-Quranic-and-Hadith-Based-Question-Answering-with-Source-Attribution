Surat Al-Ma'idah, revealed in Madinah, discusses various rulings, including the necessity of fulfilling obligations, such as treaties and covenants. It explains that lawful food includes cattle like camels, cows, and sheep, but forbids certain foods like dead animals, blood, and swine. It emphasizes the importance of not violating the sanctity of sacred months or the rituals of Hajj, such as not hunting while in the state of Ihram. The Surah also outlines how individuals should treat others, ensuring justice, even in the face of past enmity, and stresses the need to support righteousness while avoiding sin. Furthermore, it clarifies that hunting is permitted once Ihram ends, and anyone forced by necessity can eat prohibited items, as long as they do not exceed limits. Finally, it reinforces the sanctity of the Sacred House in Makkah, forbidding harm to those traveling there for Hajj or seeking Allah’s pleasure.
