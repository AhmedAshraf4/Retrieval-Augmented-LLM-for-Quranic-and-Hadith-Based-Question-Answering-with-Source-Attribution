Tafsir Explanation: Allah commands His believers to take necessary precautions against their enemies by preparing weapons, supplies, and increasing the number of troops fighting in His cause. This is reflected in the call to go forth either in groups or all together. The verse also addresses the hypocrites who stay behind from jihad, such as Abdullah bin Ubayy bin Salul, who would refrain from joining the battle and discourage others from doing so. If misfortune strikes the believers, the hypocrite would claim that Allah favored him by not being part of the fight. However, if victory and bounty come to the believers, he would wish he had participated, hoping for a share of the spoils. The verse encourages believers to engage in jihad, where those who fight in the cause of Allah, whether killed or victorious, will receive a great reward from Allah. Allah promises that the mujahid will either be granted Paradise if killed or return home safely with a reward or spoils.
