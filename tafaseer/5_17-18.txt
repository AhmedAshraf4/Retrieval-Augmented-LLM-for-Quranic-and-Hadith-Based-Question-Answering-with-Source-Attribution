The Christians are described as disbelievers for claiming that Isa, son of Maryam, is Allah, which contradicts His holiness. Allah reminds them of His absolute power over all things, stating that if He chose to destroy Isa, his mother, and all on earth, no one could prevent it. Everything in existence belongs to Allah, who creates what He wills, and He is never questioned about His actions. Allah also refutes the Christians' and Jews' claims of being His children and loved ones, pointing out that if they were truly so, they would not be punished for their sins. Instead, they are human beings, like all of Allah's creation, and He forgives or punishes whom He wills. The dominion of the heavens, earth, and everything in between belongs to Allah, and all will return to Him for judgment, where He will be just in His decisions.
