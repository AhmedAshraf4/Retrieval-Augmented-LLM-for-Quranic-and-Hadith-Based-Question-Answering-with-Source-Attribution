There was little expectation that the Jews living at the time of the Prophet would truly accept faith, because they descended from scholars who had witnessed clear signs, then hardened their hearts and knowingly distorted the Scripture after understanding it. A group of their rabbis heard the Word of God in the Torah, comprehended it correctly, then deliberately altered its meanings, reversing lawful and unlawful rulings and concealing the truth for worldly gain. They judged by the revealed Book when it suited their interests and switched to a corrupted version when bribed, while still commanding righteousness to others and neglecting it themselves. They recognized the truth of Muhammad and found his description foretold in their Book, yet denied him. When they met the believers, they claimed belief, but in private they warned one another not to reveal what God had disclosed to them about the Prophet, fearing it would become evidence against them. God knew both their hidden denial and their public claims of belief, including what they concealed among themselves and what they openly declared.
