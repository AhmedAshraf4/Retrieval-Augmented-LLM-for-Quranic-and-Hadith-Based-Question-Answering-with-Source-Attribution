The verse forbids marrying women who are already married, except for those captured in war, provided they are not pregnant. This was clarified when some men, who had captured women already married, sought the Prophet's guidance. They were then allowed to marry these women after ensuring they were not pregnant. The verse also permits marrying other women through legal means, such as paying a dowry and seeking chastity, not fornication. Any sexual enjoyment with these women requires the payment of the agreed-upon dowry. The verse also addresses the prohibition of Mut'ah marriage, which is a marriage with a predetermined end date. The Prophet later prohibited Mut'ah marriage for all time. If a dowry is agreed upon but later reduced or forfeited by mutual consent, there is no harm. Allah is described as knowing and wise in His decrees.
