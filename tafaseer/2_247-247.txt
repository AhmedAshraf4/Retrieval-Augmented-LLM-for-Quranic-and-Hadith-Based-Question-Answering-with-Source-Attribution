Tafsīr Explanation: The Israelites requested their Prophet to appoint a king over them, and he chose Talut (Saul), a soldier, despite his lack of royal lineage from the tribe of Judah. They questioned his kingship, arguing that they were more deserving and that Talut was poor, lacking the wealth they believed was necessary for a king. Some even claimed Talut's humble profession as a water carrier or skin dyer. The Prophet responded that Allah had chosen Talut, granting him greater knowledge, strength, and patience, which made him more suitable for kingship. Allah alone gives His kingdom to whom He wills, and His knowledge and wisdom are perfect, knowing who is deserving of such authority.
