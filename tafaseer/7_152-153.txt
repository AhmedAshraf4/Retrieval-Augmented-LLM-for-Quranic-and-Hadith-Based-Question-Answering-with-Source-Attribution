In this ayah, Allah commands the Israelites to repent by turning to Him and killing the guilty among themselves as a form of purification. This act, though harsh, was deemed better for them in the eyes of their Creator, and in return, Allah accepted their repentance, showing His mercy. The disgrace mentioned refers to the humiliation that the Jews suffered in this world due to their falsehoods and innovations in religion. Those who invent lies or innovations against Allah’s message will bear a weight of disgrace, even if they appear to prosper outwardly. Allah’s mercy is emphasized through His willingness to accept repentance for any sin, even for those who have committed severe transgressions like shirk or kufr, as long as they repent, believe, and return to Him in sincerity.
