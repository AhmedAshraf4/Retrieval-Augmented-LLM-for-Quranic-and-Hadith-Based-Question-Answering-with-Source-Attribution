The knowledge of inheritance, Al-Fara'id, is derived from this and related verses and is encouraged to be learned, as it impacts all people. The verse was revealed in response to specific incidents, such as when Jabir bin Abdullah was unconscious and later asked the Prophet about his inheritance. Another incident involved the daughters of Sa'd bin Ar-Rabi', whose inheritance was wrongfully taken by their uncle. Allah commanded that males receive twice the inheritance of females, as men have greater financial responsibilities. The verse also established fixed shares for parents, children, and spouses, canceling earlier practices where parents only inherited by will. The mother receives a share of one-sixth or one-third, while the father receives a larger portion. The inheritance is distributed first after debts and wills. Allah ordained these shares to ensure justice, highlighting that He alone knows what is best for His servants.
