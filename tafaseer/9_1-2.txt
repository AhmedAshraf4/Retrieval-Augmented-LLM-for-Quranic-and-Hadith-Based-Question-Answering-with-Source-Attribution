This Surah was revealed in Makkah, and it was one of the last Surahs revealed to the Prophet Muhammad. It begins without the Basmalah because it was not included in the official Mushaf compiled under Caliph Uthman. The first part was revealed after the Battle of Tabuk during the Hajj season, when the Prophet considered attending but was concerned about the idolators performing Hajj rituals improperly. As a result, he sent Abu Bakr to lead the Hajj and instructed him to announce to the idolators that they would no longer be allowed to participate in Hajj. The message was later delivered by Ali ibn Abi Talib, who declared that Allah and His Messenger were disassociated from the idolators, and they were given a four-month period to travel freely throughout the land. The terms of the treaties with idolators varied based on their specific durations, but those with a set term would not expire until the agreed period had passed.
