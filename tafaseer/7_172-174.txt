Allah took the descendants of Adam from his loins and made them testify that He is their Lord, confirming His exclusive right to worship. This testimony was given both by words and by the circumstances in which they were placed. Allah created mankind upon this natural disposition, the Fitrah, as a monotheistic nature, but the devils led many astray, turning them to other religions. Allah then separated Adam's offspring, distinguishing those destined for Paradise and those for the Fire. On the Day of Judgment, those from the Fire will be reminded that Allah had already commanded them not to associate anything with Him when they were in Adam's loins, yet they chose to do so. Allah also showed Adam his descendants, including the light of David, and granted him extra years, which he later forgot about. This forgetfulness and deviation from Allah's covenant is a shared trait of Adam and his descendants.
