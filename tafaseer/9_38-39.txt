Allah admonishes the believers who stayed behind during the battle of Tabuk, when the heat was intense, the fruits were ripe, and the shade was tempting. They preferred the comforts of this world over answering the call for Jihad in the cause of Allah. Allah rebukes them for clinging to the earth and being satisfied with the temporary pleasures of worldly life, instead of striving for the eternal rewards of the Hereafter. He reminds them that the enjoyment of this life is insignificant compared to the rewards of the Hereafter, likening the world’s pleasures to a tiny amount of water one’s finger might collect when dipped into the sea. Allah warns that if they refuse to participate, He will replace them with others who will support His Prophet and uphold His religion. Those who lag behind cannot harm Allah, for He is capable of achieving His will without their help.
