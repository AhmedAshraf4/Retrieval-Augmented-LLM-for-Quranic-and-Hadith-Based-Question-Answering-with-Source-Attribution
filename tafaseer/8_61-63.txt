If a group fears betrayal from an enemy, they may sever the peace treaty to establish equal footing. If the enemy seeks peace, the group should incline towards it and accept their offer, just as the Prophet Muhammad did when the pagans of Quraysh sought a peace treaty at Hudaybiyah for nine years. Allah commands to trust Him in such matters, for even if the enemy's peace offer is a trick, He will be sufficient. Allah reminded the believers of His favor in uniting the hearts of the Muhajirun and Ansar, who were once enemies, but through His mercy, became brothers. This unity was not possible through any material means, for the hatred between the Aws and Khazraj tribes had been deep before Islam, but Allah brought them together. The Prophet Muhammad also reminded the Ansar of their guidance, enrichment, and unity through his efforts. Allah’s wisdom and might are evident in His actions, as He unites those who trust in Him.
