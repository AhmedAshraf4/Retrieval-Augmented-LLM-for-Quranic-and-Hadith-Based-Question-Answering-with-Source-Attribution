This verse instructs believers to record debt contracts for future fulfillment to ensure clarity, fairness, and prevent disputes. The command emphasizes that the terms of the contract, including the fixed period and amounts, should be written down to preserve evidence and prevent doubt. The scribe must accurately record the terms as agreed without alteration, and the debtor must not conceal any part of what they owe. If the debtor is unable to dictate the terms, a guardian may do so. Two witnesses, preferably two men or one man and two women, should be present to ensure the accuracy of the contract. Women are required to act as witnesses in pairs due to the described limitations in their testimony. If one of the women forgets, the other can remind her. The verse also exempts immediate transactions from being written down, and stresses that witnesses and scribes should not refuse their duties when called upon. Additionally, the verse condemns any harm caused by altering or concealing the truth in contracts, urging all parties to fear Allah and uphold justice in their dealings.
