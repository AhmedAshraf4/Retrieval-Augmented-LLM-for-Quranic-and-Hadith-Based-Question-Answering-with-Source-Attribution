Allah chose specific households above all people on earth, starting with Adam, whom He created with His Hand, breathed life into, and commanded the angels to prostrate to. Allah taught Adam the names of all things and allowed him to live in Paradise before sending him down out of wisdom. Allah chose Nuh as the first Messenger to a people who worshipped idols and rejected Allah. After years of calling them to worship, Nuh’s people only grew more defiant, leading him to supplicate against them, which resulted in their drowning while only his followers were saved. Allah also chose the household of Ibrahim, including Muhammad, the final Prophet, and the family of Imran, the father of Maryam, mother of Isa. `Isa, in turn, is a descendant of Ibrahim.
