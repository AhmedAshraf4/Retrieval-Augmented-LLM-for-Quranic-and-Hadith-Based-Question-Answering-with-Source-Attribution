The believer is like a person who was once dead, lost in confusion and misguidance, until Allah brought life to him by granting him faith and guiding him to obey His Messengers. This light, which guides him on the correct path, is the Qur'an or Islam. The disbeliever, in contrast, remains in darkness, trapped in ignorance and deviation, unable to find a way out. Allah created humanity in darkness, then showered His Light upon them, with those who received it being guided, while those who missed it are astray. This contrast between light and darkness, guidance and misguidance, is frequently mentioned in the Qur'an, illustrating the fundamental difference between the believer and the disbeliever. The disbeliever's misguidance appears fair to him, as Allah decrees according to His wisdom.
