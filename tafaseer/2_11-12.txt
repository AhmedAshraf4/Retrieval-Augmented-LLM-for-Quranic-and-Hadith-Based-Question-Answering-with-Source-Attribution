When they are told, “Do not cause corruption on the earth,” they reply, “We are only reformers,” but this statement refers to the hypocrites whose actions contradict their claim. The command not to spread corruption means not to commit disbelief and acts of disobedience, because disobeying God, abandoning obligations, doubting the religion, and persisting in what is forbidden are corruption on the earth. Their corruption also includes lying to the believers by displaying faith while concealing doubt, and giving loyalty, aid, and support to those who deny God, His scriptures, and His messengers against the believers. They imagine that seeking friendship with both believers and disbelievers, or claiming to reconcile between them, is righteous reform, but this behavior itself disrupts the order God established by separating loyalty between belief and disbelief. Their outward display of belief misleads the believers and strengthens the disbelievers, which is corruption, even though they do not recognize it as such.
