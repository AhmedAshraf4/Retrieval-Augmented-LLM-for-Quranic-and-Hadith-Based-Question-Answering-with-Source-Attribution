If people are in doubt about what was sent down to Muhammad, they are challenged to produce a single chapter comparable to the Qur’an and to seek help from anyone they choose besides God, whether people, partners, or objects of worship, yet they will never be able to do so. Their permanent failure confirms that the Qur’an is not human speech and that the challenge applies to all, individually and collectively, whether skilled in eloquence or not. Because they will never meet this challenge, they are warned to fear the Fire whose fuel is human beings and stones. The stones are either idols that were worshipped besides God or sulfur-like stones that burn intensely, and both are prepared as fuel. This Fire is already prepared and awaiting those who persist in disbelief, meaning those who choose the same denial being addressed. The warning directly follows the failed challenge, establishing a clear cause and consequence: inability to match the Qur’an leads to accountability and punishment prepared for disbelievers.
