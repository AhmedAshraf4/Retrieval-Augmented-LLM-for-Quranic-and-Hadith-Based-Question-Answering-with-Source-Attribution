The disbelievers await the commencement of the Last Hour or some of its signs. The day some of these signs appear, no one’s faith will benefit them if they had not believed earlier. Before the final Day of Resurrection, several signs will precede, such as the sun rising from the west, the appearance of the Beast, and the emergence of the Antichrist. When the sun rises from the west, all will believe, but that belief will no longer be accepted. This event signifies the imminence of the Last Hour. The sun will seek permission to rise again after setting, but will not receive it. It will rise from the west as it submits under the Throne, prostrates, and is granted permission no longer to rise in the east. The appearance of the Beast follows closely after the sun's rising from the west. The disbelievers who believe at that time will not be accepted, nor will any good deeds from those who had not repented or acted righteously earlier. This final moment serves as a stern warning to those who delay their repentance until it is too late, as they will not benefit from it when these signs manifest.
