Tafsir Explanation: Allah granted the Muslims slumber during the Battle of Badr as a sign of security amidst their fear of the enemy's overwhelming numbers and their own limited strength. This slumber, similar to what occurred in the Battle of Uhud, allowed the believers to feel calm and reassured of Allah's support. Additionally, Allah sent rain on the battlefield, making the sand firm for the Muslims while hindering the movements of the idolaters, allowing them to drink and purify themselves. This rain not only physically fortified them but also removed the whispers of Shaytan and strengthened their resolve. Allah sent angels to assist the Muslims in their fight, casting fear into the hearts of the disbelievers and commanding the angels to strike at their necks and limbs, ensuring their defeat. The battle saw the death of prominent disbelievers, including Abu Jahl, and a significant number of idolaters were killed or captured. This punishment was a direct result of their defiance against Allah and His Messenger, with the ultimate punishment of the Fire awaiting them.
