When Jesus realized the disbelief of his people, he asked, "Who will be my helper in Allah's cause?" The disciples responded, "We are the helpers of Allah; we believe in Allah and bear witness that we are Muslims." They supported Jesus and followed the message he brought. Similarly, during the early days of the Prophet Muhammad, the Ansar supported him when the Quraysh prevented him from conveying Allah's message. The disciples' declaration reflected their full commitment to the truth, as they pledged to follow Jesus and be among those who bear witness. Meanwhile, the disbelievers among the Children of Israel plotted to capture and crucify Jesus. They accused him of causing division and misleading people, prompting the king to act against him. When they surrounded Jesus' home, he was miraculously raised to heaven by Allah, and another man was made to appear like him. The enemies of Jesus, believing they had captured him, crucified the wrong man, while Allah's plan thwarted their efforts, leaving them in confusion. Allah's power over their plot showed that He is the best in planning, and the disgrace upon them will endure until the Day of Judgment.
