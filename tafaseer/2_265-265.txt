The verse compares a believer’s righteous deeds to a garden on a height, symbolizing consistent growth and reward. Just as the garden thrives with heavy rain, yielding a doubled harvest, the believer's good deeds are always fruitful, with Allah rewarding them abundantly. If heavy rain does not fall, light rain still nourishes the garden, indicating that even minimal effort is accepted and rewarded by Allah. This reflects the believer’s continuous submission to Allah’s commands, whose deeds are always nurtured and never barren. Allah sees all actions, ensuring that none of His servants' deeds are overlooked.
