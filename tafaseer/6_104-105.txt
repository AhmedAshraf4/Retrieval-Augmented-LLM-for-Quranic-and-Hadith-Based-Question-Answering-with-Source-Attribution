Basa'ir refers to the clear proofs and signs found in the Qur'an and the message of the Prophet Muhammad. The verse, "So whosoever sees, will do so for himself," aligns with the idea that those who receive guidance benefit themselves, while those who go astray harm themselves. It highlights that blindness affects the heart, not the eyes. The Prophet Muhammad is not responsible for guiding or misguiding people but merely conveys Allah's message. Allah explains His verses clearly so that even the disbelievers and idolaters cannot claim that the Prophet merely learned from others. The disbelievers accuse the Prophet of inventing lies, claiming his teachings are merely stories from the past. Allah's wisdom allows some people to be misled and others to be guided, and He provides signs for the believers to recognize truth and avoid falsehood. The Qur'an serves as a trial for those whose hearts are hardened, and it guides the believers while increasing the disbelief of the disbelievers.
