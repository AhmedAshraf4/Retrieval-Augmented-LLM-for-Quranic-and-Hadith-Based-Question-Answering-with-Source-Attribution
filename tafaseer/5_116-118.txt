Tafsir Explanation: On the Day of Resurrection, Allah will address Isa, son of Maryam, asking if he ever told people to worship him and his mother as gods alongside Allah. This question is a public rebuke of those who ascribed divinity to Isa and his mother, a severe warning to Christians. Isa will deny the claim, stating that had he said such a thing, Allah would have known it. He will affirm that he only told people to worship Allah, his Lord and theirs, and that he was a witness over them while he lived among them. After his death, Allah was the Watcher over them. Isa will further declare that if Allah punishes them, they are His servants, and if He forgives them, it is due to His will, for He alone is the Almighty and All-Wise. This statement confirms Isa’s submission to Allah’s authority, distancing himself from the false beliefs attributed to him. The narrative shows the Christians' error in elevating Isa and his mother to divine status, with Allah remaining above such claims. Ultimately, Allah has complete authority over all matters.
