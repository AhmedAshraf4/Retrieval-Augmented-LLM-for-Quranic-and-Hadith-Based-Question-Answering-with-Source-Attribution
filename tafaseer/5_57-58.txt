This Ayah prohibits taking disbelievers, such as the People of the Book and polytheists, as loyal friends. These disbelievers mock the core acts of Islam, such as prayer, which are essential for both this life and the Hereafter. They view these acts with mockery and fun due to their misguided understanding. The verse emphasizes the prohibition of befriending those who treat the religion with contempt, including idolaters. It also highlights the behavior of those who mock the call to prayer (Adhan), treating it as a joke due to their lack of understanding. This mockery reflects their disconnection from the true meaning of worship, and they are influenced by Shaytan, who distracts them from the prayer, causing them to forget parts of it. These individuals fail to grasp the seriousness of Allah's commands and the significance of prayer, as seen in their disdain for the Adhan and Iqamah, which are sacred reminders for the believers.
