Allah honored Abraham as an Imam for the people because he adhered to Allah’s commands and prohibitions and was obedient, Hanif, and not a polytheist. Allah tested Abraham with certain words, meaning commands and laws, which he fulfilled, including abandoning his people when commanded, disputing with Nimrod, being patient when thrown into the fire, migrating from his homeland, hosting guests despite material demands, and preparing to sacrifice his son. He implemented all these commands, and Allah made him a role model and leader for mankind. Abraham asked that leaders among his offspring be chosen, and Allah affirmed that unjust descendants would not benefit from His covenant or serve as Imams, while the righteous among them would. The “words” of testing refer to Allah’s laws, commandments, and truthful decrees, and fulfilling them led to Abraham being chosen as a model of obedience and submission. Ritual purity acts, such as circumcision, trimming nails, plucking underarm hair, and ablution, were part of the specific commands he followed. Allah granted him good in this world and the Hereafter, and his followers, including the Prophet Muhammad and believers, have the best claim to follow his path.
