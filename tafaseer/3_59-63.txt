Tafsir Explanation: Allah compares the creation of Jesus to that of Adam to demonstrate His power, as both were created without a father. Adam was formed from dust, and Allah commanded him to be, while Jesus was born from Mary without a father, highlighting Allah’s ability to create life however He wills. If the claim that Jesus is the son of God is made because of his fatherless birth, then it would apply to Adam even more, but such a claim is false for both. Allah emphasizes His ability to create beings without the usual male-female process, contrasting with the creation of other humans. Allah affirms the truth about Jesus and warns against doubts. He then commands the Prophet to invite those who dispute the truth about Jesus to the Mubahalah, where both parties would call upon Allah’s curse on the liars. A delegation from the Christians of Najran came to argue about Jesus, claiming he was divine or the son of God. The Prophet Muhammad invited them to a Mubahalah, but they refused, fearing its consequences. Instead, they chose to settle on their belief while requesting a judge for their disputes. Allah then affirms the absolute truth that there is no deity but Him and that those who reject this truth do so in mischief, and He is aware of them, ready to punish the wrongdoers.
