Allah sealed the hearts and hearing of the disbelievers and placed a covering over their eyes, so they do not understand guidance, hear it properly, or see it. This sealing occurred because they obeyed Satan and persisted in sin and disbelief until sin surrounded the heart from all sides. Sin first places a stain on the heart, then becomes a stamp, then a seal, and finally a lock when disbelief is complete. As sins accumulated, the heart gradually closed until it was fully sealed, and guidance could no longer enter. The seal applies specifically to the heart and hearing, while the covering applies to the eyes, preventing recognition of truth. This sealing is a punishment for disbelief, not an initial state, and it fulfills what Allah decreed after their continued rejection.
