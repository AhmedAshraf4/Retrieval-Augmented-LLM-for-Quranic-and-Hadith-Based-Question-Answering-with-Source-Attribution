At the beginning of Islam, the punishment for a woman committing adultery, proven by four witnesses, was confinement to her home until her death. This ruling was later abrogated when Allah revealed a new punishment for adultery and fornication. The new punishment for the married adulterer was one hundred lashes and stoning to death, while the unmarried offender was given one hundred lashes and banishment for a year. The punishment for homosexuality was death for both parties involved. If the offenders repented and corrected their behavior, they were to be left alone, as Allah is ever the one who accepts repentance and is most merciful.
