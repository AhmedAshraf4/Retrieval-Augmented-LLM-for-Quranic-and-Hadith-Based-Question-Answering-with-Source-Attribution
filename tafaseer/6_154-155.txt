Allah praised the Tawrah and the Qur'an together, highlighting the Qur'an as the straight path and the Tawrah as a guide and mercy, both revealed to humanity for their guidance. Allah had earlier mentioned the Qur'an as a confirming Book in Arabic and described the Tawrah as a complete and detailed Book, offering guidance and mercy. He also reminded the disbelievers that they had rejected both the Qur'an and the Tawrah, claiming them to be forms of magic. The Jinn acknowledged the Qur'an as confirming what came before it, guiding to the truth. Allah emphasized that His revelations to Musa, as well as to Muhammad, are both complete and provide detailed guidance for righteousness. He urged people to follow the Qur'an, which is a blessed Book, to gain mercy and protection from Allah, thereby ensuring their belief in the meeting with their Lord.
