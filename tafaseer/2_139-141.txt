Allah instructed His Prophet to respond to disputes about Him by saying that He is their Lord and the Lord of the Jews and Christians, that each is accountable for their own deeds, and that they are sincere in worship and submission to Him. This mirrors the statements of earlier Prophets, such as Abraham, who declared to his people that disputes about Allah cannot alter His Oneness and that he disowns those who reject Him. Allah clarified that Abraham and the Prophets who followed him, including Ishmael, Isaac, Jacob, and their tribes, were neither Jews nor Christians but true Muslims, submitting solely to Allah, and they were not among the idolaters. Those who conceal this testimony from Allah are most unjust, yet Allah is fully aware of all deeds and will reward each according to their actions. The passing nations earned the results of their deeds, and their descendants are accountable only for their own actions, not the deeds of their forebears, emphasizing submission to Allah and adherence to His Messengers.
