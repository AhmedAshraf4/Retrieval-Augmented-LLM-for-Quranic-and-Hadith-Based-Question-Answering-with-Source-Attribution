This verse presents a parable about the rewards of spending in the cause of Allah, illustrating that the reward for such acts is multiplied by Allah from ten to seven hundred times. The verse specifically refers to spending in Allah's obedience, including in acts like Jihad, and emphasizes how Allah grows the reward, much like a plant grows from fertile land. The multiplication of rewards up to seven hundred times is also reflected in Hadith, where a man who donates a camel in Allah's cause is promised seven hundred camels as reward on the Day of Resurrection. Additionally, all good deeds are multiplied, but fasting holds a unique status, as it is directly for Allah, who rewards it in a manner beyond measure. Allah's statement that He grants manifold increase to whom He wills reflects the vastness of His mercy, based on sincerity, and His all-encompassing knowledge of who deserves it.
