Tafsir Explanation: Allah warns the believers against obeying the disbelievers and hypocrites, as such obedience leads to destruction. He commands them to trust in Him as their protector and helper. Allah promises to instill fear of the Muslims in the hearts of the disbelievers, and they will face punishment in the Hereafter for their idolatry. The believers initially gained victory over their enemies at Uhud by Allah's permission, but when they faltered and disputed the orders, some sought worldly gain, leading to their retreat and defeat. Despite this, Allah forgave them. The defeat was compounded by the rumor of the Prophet's death, causing further distress. However, Allah reminded them not to grieve over missed victories or losses, as He was fully aware of their actions. During the battle, only a few companions, including Talhah and Sa'd, remained with the Prophet, while others fled. Mus'ab bin Umayr shielded the Prophet but was killed, and the Prophet killed Ubayy bin Khalaf, who had vowed to kill him. The Prophet sustained injuries, and his face was wounded. Fatimah tended to his wounds, and the blood stopped after ashes were applied.
