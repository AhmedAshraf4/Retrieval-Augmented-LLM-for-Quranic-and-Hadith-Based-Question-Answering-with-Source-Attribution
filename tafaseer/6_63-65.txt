Allah highlights His mercy and power, emphasizing that when people face distress, especially during storms at sea, they call on Allah alone, with sincerity, seeking His help. This is exemplified when sailors, caught in a storm, pray to Allah for deliverance, promising gratitude if saved. Yet, once they are safe, they return to associating others with Allah in times of ease. Allah has the power to send punishment from above or below, or cause division and strife among people, making them suffer at the hands of one another. The verse illustrates Allah’s control over the world, and His ability to bring about calamity if He so chooses, urging people to understand the consequences of disbelief and shirk. The Prophet’s supplications also reflected this theme, where he asked Allah to protect his followers from disasters such as drowning, famine, and internal division, with only the latter being denied. This illustrates the importance of unity and avoiding internal conflict, which leads to harm.
