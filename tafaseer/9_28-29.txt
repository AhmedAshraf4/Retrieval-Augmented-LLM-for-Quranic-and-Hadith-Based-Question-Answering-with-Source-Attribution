Idolators were prohibited from entering Al-Masjid Al-Haram after the ninth year of Hijrah, as Allah commanded the believers to expel them, declaring them impure in a religious sense. This decree was made concrete when the Prophet sent Abu Bakr and Ali to announce that no Mushrik would be allowed to perform Hajj or Tawaf from that year onward. This marked the final prohibition for idolators, who were also restricted from approaching the sacred area. The believers were assured that despite the disruption to their commerce, Allah would compensate them through the Jizyah from the People of the Book. The payment of Jizyah, with submission and humility, was a sign of the People of the Book’s disgrace and their failure to follow the true religion. This ruling was affirmed in the treaty terms set by Umar ibn Al-Khattab with the Christians of Ash-Sham, which imposed conditions ensuring their continued humiliation and subjugation under Muslim rule.
