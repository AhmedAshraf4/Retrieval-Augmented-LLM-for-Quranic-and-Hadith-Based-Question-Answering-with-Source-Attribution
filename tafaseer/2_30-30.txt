Allah informed the angels that He would place a successor on the earth, meaning a creation that would inhabit it and succeed one another generation after generation, not Adam alone. From this announcement, the angels understood that this type of creature would cause corruption and shed blood, either from their knowledge that humans would be created from clay or from the meaning of succession, which involves judging disputes and restraining injustice. They asked about the wisdom of creating such beings while they themselves constantly glorified and sanctified Allah, and their question was not opposition or envy but a request to understand the purpose. Allah answered that He knows what they do not know, meaning that the benefit of creating humanity outweighs the harm they mentioned. His knowledge included that from among these successors would come prophets, messengers, righteous believers, martyrs, worshippers, scholars who act on knowledge, and servants whose deeds are raised to Him in prayer, which the angels themselves witness. This unseen outcome explained why Allah proceeded with the creation despite the corruption the angels anticipated.
