Tafsīr Explanation: The idolators argued that Allah was aware of their shirk and their prohibition of certain things, and claimed that if Allah had willed, He would have guided them to faith and stopped their disbelief. They believed Allah's will was aligned with their actions. Allah refutes this by stating that those who made similar claims before them were destroyed for their falsehoods. He challenges them to provide evidence that Allah approves of their actions, highlighting that they follow mere speculation and lies. Allah makes it clear that He has perfect proof and could have guided everyone if He willed, but His will is carried out according to His divine decree. The disbelievers are warned not to follow the desires of those who reject His signs, associating others with Allah and failing to believe in the Hereafter.
