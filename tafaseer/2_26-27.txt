God states that He freely sets parables using even the smallest creatures, such as a mosquito or anything larger, and this declaration came after hypocrites objected to earlier parables and claimed such examples were beneath divine speech. Believers recognize these parables as true and are guided by them, while hypocrites reject them despite knowing their truth, which increases their misguidance. Misguidance through these parables applies only to the defiantly disobedient, identified as those who break God’s covenant after affirming it, sever what God commanded to be joined, and spread corruption on the earth. The covenant refers to God’s binding command to worship Him alone, obey His orders, and accept His messengers and revealed books, which was clearly established through revelation and proofs. Breaking this covenant occurs when people knowingly reject the truth, deny the messengers, or conceal what they were obligated to uphold. Severing what God commanded to be joined includes cutting family ties and abandoning all bonds God ordered to be maintained. These people are described as the losers because they forfeit their share of God’s mercy in the Hereafter through disbelief and hypocrisy, just as a trader loses his capital through ruinous trade.
