Allah highlights the creation of the heavens and earth and the alternation of night and day as clear signs for those who reflect. The sky, with its vastness, and the earth, with its diversity of features—planets, seas, mountains, plants, animals, and elements—demonstrate His power and wisdom. The variation in the length of day and night also points to divine design. Those who truly understand recognize these signs, unlike those who pass by them heedlessly. The faithful remember Allah in all states—standing, sitting, or lying down—reflecting on the creation of the heavens and earth, affirming that Allah created all things with purpose, not in jest. They pray for salvation from the torment of the Fire, acknowledging that only Allah’s justice prevails. They ask for forgiveness for their sins and for guidance to be among the righteous. When they heard the call to faith, they believed and followed the Messenger, praying for the fulfillment of Allah’s promises on the Day of Judgment. The Prophet, upon the revelation of this verse, would often recite it at night during his voluntary prayers, contemplating deeply on its meaning.
