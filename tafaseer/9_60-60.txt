Tafsīr Explanation: Allah has clarified the distribution of Zakah, emphasizing that He alone decides its division. The first recipients mentioned are the poor, who have urgent needs, followed by the needy, who do not beg but have insufficient means. The Prophet instructed that alms should not be given to the wealthy or those physically able. The Zakah funds can also be given to those collecting alms, except the Prophet's family, who are prohibited from receiving them. Al-Mu'allafatu Qulubuhum refers to individuals, such as Safwan bin Umayyah, who were given alms to strengthen their faith or embrace Islam. Riqab refers to slaves who may be freed by Zakah funds, with the reward being proportionate to the act of freeing. The indebted, especially those in financial hardship or who have repented from sin, are eligible for Zakah. It can also be given to those fighting in the cause of Allah or to a traveler in need. Allah's decree on Zakah is an obligation, with wisdom and knowledge behind the division.
