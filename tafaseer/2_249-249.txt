Tafsir Explanation: Talut, the king of the Children of Israel, led his army of eighty thousand soldiers, according to some reports, and ordered them to cross a river, which flowed between Jordan and Palestine. He warned them that anyone who drank from the river would not be considered part of his army, except those who took only a sip from the hollow of their hands. Most of the soldiers drank freely, but a few obeyed the command. After crossing the river, Talut and his faithful soldiers, feeling outnumbered by Jalut (Goliath) and his army, expressed doubt about their ability to defeat them. However, their scholars reminded them that victory comes from Allah alone, not from the size of the army, emphasizing that even a small group can triumph over a large one with Allah’s support, and patience is key.
