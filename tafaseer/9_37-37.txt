The idolators were condemned for altering Allah's law by allowing what was forbidden and forbidding what was allowed, based on their desires. They used to change the sanctity of the Sacred Months, especially Muharram, by postponing it to Safar, making one month sacred and the other non-sacred each year. This practice was done to adjust the number of sacred months to four, in line with Allah's original decree. One such person, Abu Thumamah, would announce the changes at Hajj, declaring one month sacred and another non-sacred, reversing it the following year. This alteration, known as the postponing of a sacred month, was considered an addition to disbelief, as it disregarded Allah's commands regarding the sanctity of the months.
