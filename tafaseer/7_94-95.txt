Allah sent afflictions, including sicknesses (Ba'sa') and poverty (Darra'), to earlier nations to compel them to humble themselves and supplicate for relief. However, they did not repent. In response, Allah replaced their hardships with prosperity, healing, and wealth, hoping they would be thankful, but they failed to do so. They claimed that their forefathers experienced similar cycles of hardship and ease, not realizing that these were tests from Allah. When prosperity came, they assumed it was just part of a cycle, failing to recognize the divine wisdom in both suffering and ease. Believers, however, are patient during hardship and grateful in times of prosperity, understanding these as tests. Allah seized the nations suddenly, catching them unaware, just as sudden death is described as a mercy for the believer but sorrowful punishment for the disbeliever.
