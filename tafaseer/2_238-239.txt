Tafsir Explanation: Allah commands that prayer should be performed properly and on time. The most beloved deed to Allah is offering prayers at their fixed times, followed by participating in jihad in Allah's cause and being dutiful to parents. Allah specifically mentions the Middle prayer, which the majority of scholars identify as the Asr prayer. This is supported by several companions, including Umar, Ali, Ibn Masud, and others. During the Battle of the Confederates, when the disbelievers delayed the Asr prayer, the Prophet Muhammad emphasized the importance of safeguarding it, stating that missing the Asr prayer is like losing one’s family and wealth. Allah also commands that during prayer, one should stand before Him in obedience, which prohibits talking. In cases of fear, such as in battle, the prayer can be performed while on foot or riding, as Allah allows for adjustments to the manner of prayer during times of danger. Once safety is assured, prayer should be performed as prescribed, in full.
