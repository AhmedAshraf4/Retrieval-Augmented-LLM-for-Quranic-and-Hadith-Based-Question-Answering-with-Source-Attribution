Allah forbids His believing servants from approaching prayer while drunk, as one cannot understand what they are saying in such a state. He also prohibits entering the Masjid while sexually impure, except when passing through without sitting. This ruling was revealed before the complete prohibition of alcohol, as shown by the incident with Umar, who requested further clarification after the initial verse was revealed. Later, alcohol was fully prohibited, and the Prophet declared that no drunk person could approach prayer. The verse also allows for Tayammum (using clean earth) when there is no water due to illness, travel, or other valid reasons. This concession was made in the case of a journey where the Prophet's wife, A’ishah, lost her necklace, and no water was available. Allah revealed the verses of Tayammum, and the people performed it in lieu of ablution.
