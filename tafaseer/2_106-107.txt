Allah states that He abrogates or suspends a revealed ruling, either by removing its wording, removing its ruling, or delaying its application, and then replaces it with a ruling that is more beneficial or similar in effect for those addressed. Abrogation applies only to commands, prohibitions, and legal permissions, not to historical reports. Some revelations were erased entirely, some had their rulings changed while the wording remained, and some were delayed or caused to be forgotten by divine will. When a ruling is replaced, the new ruling is either easier, more beneficial, or equal in obligation and purpose, including changes in what is commanded, permitted, or forbidden. Allah affirms His complete power and ownership over the heavens and the earth, and that no protector or helper exists besides Him. Because He alone owns creation, He legislates as He wills, repeals what He wills, and upholds what He wills, and His judgment cannot be challenged. The abrogation of rulings is a deliberate act of authority and testing, demonstrating obedience to divine commands. The denial of abrogation is rejected, because Allah has full dominion and authority to replace previous rulings, just as He did in earlier laws before the final revelation.
