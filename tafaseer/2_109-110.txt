Many from the People of the Book wished to turn the believers back to disbelief because of envy, even though the truth of the Messenger was clear to them from their own scriptures, and their rejection came from deliberate transgression rather than ignorance. They recognized the Messenger as the one described in the Torah and Gospel, but denied him because he was not from their own people. The believers were commanded at that stage to forgive and overlook their harm, to remain patient, and to continue obeying Allah by establishing prayer and giving alms, while awaiting Allah’s decisive command. The Prophet and his companions followed this order by showing restraint and forgiveness toward the disbelievers and the People of the Book. This command to pardon remained in effect until Allah permitted fighting, after which those decreed to be killed among the leaders of Quraysh were defeated by the Prophet’s forces. Alongside this, the believers were directed to focus on prayer, charity, and righteous deeds, with the certainty that every good action is preserved with Allah, who fully sees and repays all deeds without neglect.
