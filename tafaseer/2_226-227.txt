Tafsir Explanation: Ila' is a vow in which a man swears not to have sexual relations with his wife for a specified period, whether less or more than four months. If the vow lasts less than four months, the husband must wait for the vow to end before resuming relations, and the wife cannot demand that he end the vow earlier. If the vow lasts longer than four months, the wife can request the husband to either resume relations or divorce her. If the husband does not act after four months, the authorities may intervene to enforce the divorce. The vow applies to wives, not slave-women, and the husband must either return to his wife or divorce her after the four months. If the husband chooses divorce, it does not occur automatically after the four months but requires his decision. The vow and its fulfillment are based on specific actions: the husband swears to abstain, waits the required period, and then either reconciles with his wife or divorces her.
