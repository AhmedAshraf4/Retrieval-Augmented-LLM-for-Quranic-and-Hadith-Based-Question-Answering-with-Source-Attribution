Tafsir Explanation: Killing a believer by mistake requires two actions: the freeing of a believing slave and the payment of blood money to the deceased’s family. If the deceased was from a group at war with the Muslims, only the freeing of a believing slave is required, and if from a group with a peace treaty, blood money is owed. The blood money is usually twenty camels from the tribe of the killer, not the killer himself. If the killer cannot afford the payment, he must fast for two consecutive months. The verse addresses the case of accidental killing, exemplified by the story of Ayyash bin Abi Rabiah, who killed a man he thought was a disbeliever but had already embraced Islam. The Prophet Muhammad صلى الله عليه وسلم clarified that unintentional killing is pardoned through repentance, while intentional killing brings severe punishment, with no repentance accepted according to some scholars, though others say repentance is possible. In cases of intentional murder, the killer’s fate is tied to Allah’s will, though good deeds may avert punishment, and repentance could still be accepted if sincere.
