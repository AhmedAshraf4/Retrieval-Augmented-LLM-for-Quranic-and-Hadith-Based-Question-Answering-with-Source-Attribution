Tafsir Explanation: Mankind was initially one community, all following the guidance of Allah. However, they later disputed, leading Allah to send Prophets to guide them, bringing warnings and glad tidings. The first Prophet sent was Noah. The people disagreed on matters such as the day of congregation, the Qiblah, prayer, fasting, and the status of figures like Abraham and Jesus. For example, Jews chose Saturday and Christians chose Sunday for the day of gathering, while Allah guided the Muslim Ummah to Friday. Similarly, the Jews and Christians disputed the true Qiblah and the nature of Jesus, but Allah guided the Muslim Ummah to the truth, directing them to the Ka’bah and establishing Jesus as His servant and a spirit from Him. Allah guides whom He wills to the straight path, by His leave, and this is exemplified in the guidance provided to the Prophet Muhammad and his followers, as seen in prayers asking for guidance to truth amidst disputes.
