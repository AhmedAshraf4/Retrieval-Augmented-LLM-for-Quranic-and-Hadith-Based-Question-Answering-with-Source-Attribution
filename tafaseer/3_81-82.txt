Tafsir Explanation: Allah took a pledge from all the Prophets, from Adam to Jesus, that when a Messenger comes confirming what they have received, they would believe in and support him. This pledge, known as "Isri," required the Prophets to acknowledge and commit to supporting the future Messenger. Allah asked, "Do you agree to this covenant?" and the Prophets affirmed, saying, "We agree." Allah then commanded them to bear witness to this pledge. If any Prophet or their followers turned away from this commitment, they would be considered rebellious. Muhammad, as the final Prophet, was the one all earlier Prophets pledged to support if they were alive during his time. This is why Muhammad led the Prophets in prayer during the night of Isra' in Jerusalem and why he alone will carry the responsibility of intercession on the Day of Judgment. This station of intercession, known as Al-Maqam Al-Mahmud, is reserved for him, a task that no other Prophet will undertake.
