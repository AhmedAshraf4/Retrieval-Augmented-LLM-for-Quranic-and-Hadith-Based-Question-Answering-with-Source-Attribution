Tafsir Explanation: Allah commands that a group within the Muslim community should rise to call to righteousness, enjoin good, and forbid evil, with those fulfilling this task being the successful ones. This duty is not only for a special group but also for every individual according to their ability. The Prophet Muhammad emphasized that witnessing evil requires changing it, first with action, then with words, and finally with the heart, as the weakest form of faith. If this duty is neglected, a punishment from Allah may come, and prayers for mercy may not be answered. Allah forbids division, warning the Muslim Ummah not to imitate the discord of previous nations, who abandoned righteousness despite clear proofs. The Prophet mentioned that the Muslim Ummah would split into many sects, with only one group following the correct path. On the Day of Judgment, those who follow the Sunnah will have bright faces, while those who follow innovation and division will have dark faces, as they will be asked if they rejected faith after accepting it. Those with white faces will enter Allah's mercy in Paradise, where they will remain forever, while those with black faces will face torment for their disbelief. These verses reveal the truth of this world and the Hereafter, and Allah, the Just, does not wrong His creation. All matters belong to Him, and He has the ultimate authority over all affairs.
