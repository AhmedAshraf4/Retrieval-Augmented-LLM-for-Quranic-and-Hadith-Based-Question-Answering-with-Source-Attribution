Allah commands that trusts be returned to their rightful owners, whether these are obligations to Allah, such as prayer, Zakah, and fasting, or the rights people entrust each other with. The Prophet Muhammad emphasized this in a hadith, urging to return the trust to those who entrusted it to you and not to betray those who betrayed you. This includes all kinds of trusts, whether recorded or not. On the Day of Resurrection, those who neglect this command will be held accountable. The Prophet’s return of the key of the Ka'bah to Uthman bin Talhah after conquering Makkah was a direct fulfillment of this command. Allah also commands justice when judging between people, particularly for those in authority, with a hadith stating that Allah supports a judge as long as he is just. The ayah underscores the importance of returning rights and judging fairly, reminding that Allah hears and sees all actions and words.
