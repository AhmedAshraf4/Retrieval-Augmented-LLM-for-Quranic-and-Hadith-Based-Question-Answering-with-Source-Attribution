When a Book from God came to the Jews, meaning the Qur’an sent to Muhammad, confirming what they already had in the Torah, they rejected it despite prior knowledge. Before Muhammad was sent, they used to pray for his coming and seek victory through him against polytheists and against the Arab tribes of Aws and Khazraj, saying that a final prophet would come and they would defeat their enemies with him. They described this prophet and invoked God to send him, expecting he would be from among them. When Muhammad was sent to the Arabs and they recognized that he matched what they knew from the Torah, they denied him because he was not from their own people. They claimed that he did not bring what they recognized and that he was not the prophet they had spoken of. Their rejection came after recognition, driven by envy of the Arabs, even though they knew he was God’s messenger, and this denial after knowledge is the reason for the condemnation stated in the verse.
