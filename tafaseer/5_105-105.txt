Allah commands believers to focus on reforming themselves and performing righteous deeds. He assures them that if they guide themselves aright, they will not be harmed by the errors of others, including their family members. Imam Ahmad narrates that Abu Bakr As-Siddiq clarified the meaning of this verse, explaining that it is often misunderstood. He cited the Prophet Muhammad's warning that if people witness evil and do not intervene, Allah's punishment will eventually encompass them. Abu Bakr also warned against lying, as it contradicts faith. The verse emphasizes personal responsibility for one’s actions and guidance, stating that no harm will come from those who are misguided if one follows the right path.
