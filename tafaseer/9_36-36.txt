The year consists of twelve months, with four sacred months: Dhul-Qa'dah, Dhul-Hijjah, Muharram, and Rajab. The Prophet emphasized their sanctity, urging that they should not be violated. He highlighted the importance of the day of Nahr (the Day of Sacrifice), the month of Dhul-Hijjah, and the sacred city of Makkah, noting that the sanctity of blood, property, and honor is paramount, just as the sanctity of these times and places. He also warned against reverting to misguidance and emphasized that those present should relay his message to those who are absent, as they may understand it better. The four sacred months were established to ensure ease in performing the Hajj and 'Umrah. Dhul-Qa'dah, the month before Hajj, was sacred to prevent fighting; Dhul-Hijjah is for the rituals of Hajj; Muharram allows for safe return from Hajj; and Rajab, situated in the middle of the lunar year, allows for safe travel for 'Umrah. Sin during these months is worse, and Allah's law on justice and fighting in these months is clear: while fighting is prohibited, if initiated by the enemy, retaliation is allowed, but warfare should not begin during these sacred times.
