Among the People of the Book were unlettered people who could not write and had no real knowledge of the revealed Book, so they did not understand what it contained and relied only on false claims, invented statements, and empty wishes spoken by their tongues. Their assumptions were lies and corrupt ideas about God, not knowledge. Another group among them deliberately wrote texts with their own hands, altered the revelation, and then falsely claimed that these writings were from God in order to gain a small worldly profit. This act involved conscious fabrication, deception, and unlawful consumption of people’s wealth. Woe and destruction were declared upon them for what they physically wrote as lies and for what they earned through this falsehood, because their actions combined altering revelation, attributing it to God, and profiting from misguidance.
