Tafsir Explanation: Ibn Mas`ud described the Straight Path as the path of Islam, with its beginning at the Prophet Muhammad’s time and its end in Paradise. On this path, there are other paths to the right and left, where men call to lead people astray. Those who follow these other paths will end up in the Fire, while those who remain on the Straight Path will reach Paradise. The Straight Path is guided by the Qur'an, which calls people to follow it without deviating. On the path are two walls representing Allah’s set limits, with open doors symbolizing prohibited actions. A caller warns against entering these doors, urging people not to stray from the path. The Qur'an, as the guide, and Allah’s admonition in the hearts of believers, direct them to avoid the temptations of the other paths.
