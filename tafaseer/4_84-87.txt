Allah commands His Messenger, Muhammad, to fight in Jihad and not to be concerned about those who do not join. The verse emphasizes that Muhammad is only responsible for himself in this regard. The Prophet is encouraged to incite the believers to fight, strengthening their resolve. At the Battle of Badr, he rallied the believers by promising them Paradise, emphasizing the great reward for those who participate in Jihad. The rewards of Jihad are immense, with one hundred grades in Paradise reserved for those who fight in Allah's cause. Allah's might is greater than the disbelievers, and He can punish them, but He allows fighting to test the believers. Those who intercede for good causes will share in the reward, while those who intercede for evil causes will bear the burden of it. When greeted with a greeting, Muslims are urged to respond with a better greeting or at least equally. The verse concludes by affirming Allah’s absolute authority and truth, stating that He will gather everyone on the Day of Resurrection, and no one is truer in speech than Allah.
