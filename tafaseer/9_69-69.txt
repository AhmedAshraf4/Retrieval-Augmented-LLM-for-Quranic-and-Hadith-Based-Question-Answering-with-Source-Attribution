The verse refers to those who mocked their religion, as stated by Al-Hasan Al-Basri. The phrase "and you indulged in play and pastime as they indulged in play and pastime" refers to those who engage in falsehood and lies. Their deeds are rendered void and will earn no reward in this world or the Hereafter, making them the ultimate losers. Ibn Abbas draws a parallel with the Children of Israel, and the Prophet Muhammad warned that his followers would imitate them so closely that if a man from among them entered a lizard’s den, they would follow him. This hadith, emphasizing how closely the followers of the Prophet would imitate those before them, was affirmed by Abu Hurayrah, with the Prophet confirming that this imitation would be exact, step by step, even in the smallest of actions.
