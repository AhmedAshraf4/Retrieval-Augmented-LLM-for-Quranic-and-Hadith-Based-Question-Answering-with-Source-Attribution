“All praise is due to Allah, Lord of the worlds” affirms that every form of praise and gratitude belongs exclusively to Allah and not to any created being, because all favors originate from Him alone. He created the means of worship, the bodies by which commands are carried out, the provision of worldly sustenance, and a life of ease without compulsion from anyone, and He informed creation of the path leading to everlasting reward. Praise includes acknowledgment of Allah’s actions and His perfect names and attributes, while gratitude is for specific blessings, and this statement gathers both meanings together. The definite form of praise indicates that all possible praise, in every sense and at every time, belongs to Allah. Calling Him Lord establishes Him as the sole owner, master, and absolute authority over what He possesses, a title used without qualification only for Him. The worlds refers to everything that exists besides Allah, encompassing all created beings in the heavens and the earth, each category forming a distinct world, and their existence serves as evidence pointing to their Creator and His oneness.
