Allah reminded His Messenger of the mercy that made him gentle and kind-hearted toward his followers, unlike the harshness that would have caused them to scatter. The Prophet’s kindness was a result of Allah's mercy, as He had made him soft-hearted, in contrast to someone with a severe heart. The Prophet sought the advice of his Companions in matters like battles and major decisions, including the battles of Badr, Uhud, and Khandaq, to ensure their active participation in decisions. Allah commanded the Prophet to pardon, seek forgiveness for, and consult with his followers. After making decisions, the Prophet was instructed to place trust in Allah, as success and defeat come from Him. He was also exonerated from any dishonesty in matters like the spoils of war, with a strong warning against Ghulul, or deceit. The Prophet emphasized the severe consequences of such actions on the Day of Judgment. Allah contrasted those who seek His pleasure through obedience with those who earn His anger and face eternal punishment in Hell. Believers are in varying ranks with Allah according to their deeds, and Allah sees all their actions. Finally, Allah granted a great favor to the believers by sending them a Messenger from their own kind, so they could converse with him and learn from him, thus purifying their hearts and guiding them away from ignorance.
