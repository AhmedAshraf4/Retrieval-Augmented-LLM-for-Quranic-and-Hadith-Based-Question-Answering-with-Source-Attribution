Allah declares that every soul will taste death, as all creation is perishable except for His face, full of majesty. The jinn, mankind, angels, and even those who bear His Throne will eventually die. Only Allah, the eternal, remains. This serves as a comfort, as all living beings face death, marking the end of this world and the commencement of the Day of Resurrection, when everyone will be rewarded for their deeds, without injustice. Those who are spared from the Fire and admitted to Paradise are the true victors. This world’s life is fleeting, deceptive, and insignificant in comparison to the eternal Hereafter. Believers are tested in their wealth and lives, with trials tailored to their faith's strength. They are commanded to be patient and forgiving, following the Prophet's example, especially in the face of harm from others, whether from disbelievers or the People of the Scripture. The Prophet showed mercy even to those who opposed him, and believers must endure hardship with trust in Allah, knowing that ultimate victory lies in the Hereafter.
