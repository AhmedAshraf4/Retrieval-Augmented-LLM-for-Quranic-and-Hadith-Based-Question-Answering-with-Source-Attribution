The verse condemns the People of the Book for commanding others to practice righteousness while neglecting it themselves, despite reciting and knowing the Scripture that warns of punishment for violating God’s commands. They instructed people to obey God, pray, fast, and uphold righteousness, yet contradicted these commands through their own actions. They also warned others against rejecting the messenger and the covenant recorded in the Scripture, while they themselves broke that covenant and rejected what they knew to be true. The rebuke targets their self-contradiction, not the act of commanding righteousness itself, since enjoining good is obligatory, but it must be joined with personal compliance. The failure to act upon what one commands is presented as harm done to oneself and as a state of heedlessness and lack of reason.
