Allah appointed heirs (Mawali) for every individual, referring to relatives such as children who inherit from their parents and family. This includes the property left by parents and close kin. The verse also refers to individuals with whom a pledge of brotherhood was made, instructing that they be given their due share of inheritance, thus honoring the pledges made. Initially, emigrants in Madinah would inherit from the Ansar, while the Ansar's relatives would not, due to the brotherhood bond. However, this practice was later abrogated, and the verse canceled the brotherhood pledge concerning inheritance, allowing the inheritance rights to revert to the individuals' blood relatives. The verse about fulfilling pledges remained valid for mutual cooperation and advice but not for inheritance, which could now be included in a will for someone previously entitled to inherit.
