The Jews claimed that the Fire would touch them only for a limited number of days and that they would then be saved from it. God challenged this claim by asking whether they possessed a binding covenant from Him guaranteeing such an outcome, and stated that no such covenant existed, so their claim was a lie spoken without knowledge. Their statement rested on an assumed fixed, short punishment rather than a promise from God. This claim appeared again after the conquest of Khaybar, when the Prophet Muhammad questioned the assembled Jews and asked who would be the people of the Fire. They replied that they would remain in it only briefly and that others would replace them afterward. He rejected this claim and declared that they would not be replaced in it. In the same gathering, they admitted to poisoning a sheep presented to him, explaining that they sought to test whether he was truthful, which further exposed their false assertions and deliberate actions.
