God taught Adam the names of all created things, including their specific identities, qualities, and functions, while the angels did not possess this knowledge. God then presented the created objects to the angels and challenged them to name them, exposing their inability and confirming their earlier lack of knowledge when they questioned the creation of a successor on earth. The angels admitted they knew only what God had taught them and declared His complete knowledge and wisdom. God commanded Adam to inform the angels of the names, and Adam accurately named all things, including living creatures and angels, demonstrating his superiority in knowledge. God then reminded the angels that He knows the unseen of the heavens and the earth and knows what they reveal and conceal. This included what was openly spoken about corruption and bloodshed on earth and what was hidden, such as Iblis’s pride and the angels’ assumption of their own superiority. The event established Adam’s elevated status through knowledge granted by God and confirmed that God’s prior statement about knowing what they did not know was fulfilled.
