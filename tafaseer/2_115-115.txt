“To Allah belong the east and the west, so wherever you turn, there is the Face of Allah” was revealed in connection with the change of prayer direction. The Messenger prayed toward Jerusalem in Makkah with the Ka‘bah between him and that direction, then continued facing Jerusalem after migrating to Madinah for about sixteen or seventeen months, before being commanded to face the Ka‘bah. When the prayer direction changed, the Jews objected, and this statement affirmed that all directions belong to Allah and that He directs worship as He wills. It also applied to specific situations where a fixed direction was not required, such as voluntary prayer while traveling, prayer during fear or combat, or when the correct direction could not be determined due to darkness or clouds, in which case prayer was valid in whichever direction one faced. For the people of Madinah and nearby regions, the valid prayer direction lay between east and west. The conclusion affirms that Allah fully encompasses His creation with provision and generosity and that He has complete knowledge of all actions and circumstances.
