Tafsir Explanation: The idols worshipped by disbelievers are inanimate objects that neither create, nor have the power to create anything. These idols cannot hear, see, or help their worshippers. Even if all the false gods gathered together, they would be unable to create a fly, and if the fly took something from them, they would be powerless to retrieve it. These idols are not only weak but also created by humans, as Prophet Ibrahim said, "Do you worship what you carve?" The idols cannot defend themselves, nor can they assist those who worship them. For instance, Prophet Ibrahim broke the idols of his people to show their impotence. Similarly, the idols are unable to answer calls to guidance, as they neither hear nor see. Prophet Ibrahim, in challenging his people, declared his innocence from the worship of idols, saying that only the Creator, who guides him, is worthy of worship. Despite their appearances, idols are incapable of providing aid or guidance, and those who call upon them are deceived by their lifeless forms.
