God took a binding covenant from the Children of Israel to worship Him alone and follow His messengers, and when they resisted, He raised Mount Sinai above them like a canopy so they feared it would fall, forcing their submission to the pledge. They were commanded to hold firmly to the Torah with seriousness and strength and to remember what is in it by reading it and carrying out its commands. After this clear and enforced covenant, they turned away and broke what they had pledged. God’s grace and mercy restrained the consequence of their breach by forgiving them and continuing to send prophets to them. Without that grace and mercy, they would have been complete losers in this world and the Hereafter.
