Allah commanded the performance of Wudu for prayer, making it obligatory in cases of impurity and recommended when pure. Initially, Muslims were required to perform Wudu before every prayer, but this rule was later abrogated. The Prophet performed Wudu before each prayer but made an exception on the Day of Victory, using a single Wudu for all five prayers. Wudu involves washing the face, hands up to the elbows, and feet, and wiping over the head. The Prophet also passed his fingers through his beard during Wudu and encouraged washing between the fingers. The feet must be washed, not just wiped, to avoid neglecting any part of the body. After completing Wudu, it is encouraged to pray two Rak’ahs with full attention, which can lead to forgiveness of sins. If no water is available or when ill, Tayammum with clean earth is allowed as a substitute for Wudu. Wudu purifies the believer, washing away sins associated with the washed body parts. The practice of Wudu is a symbol of faith, and those who perform it properly with sincere intention can expect to enter Paradise through any of its gates.
