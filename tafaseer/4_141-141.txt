The hypocrites observe the believers, hoping for their downfall, and seek to align with whichever side prevails. When the Muslims achieve victory, they attempt to associate themselves with the believers, saying, "Were we not with you?" But when the disbelievers triumph, as in the Battle of Uhud, they try to strengthen their ties with the disbelievers, claiming, "Did we not help you and protect you from the believers?" Their aim is to secure their position by pretending to be loyal to both sides. However, Allah will judge them on the Day of Resurrection, exposing their true intentions. He will not allow the disbelievers to triumph over the believers, either in this life or the Hereafter. Though the disbelievers may sometimes gain a temporary victory, the ultimate victory will belong to the believers, as promised by Allah, who will grant His Messengers and the believers triumph in both worlds.
