Alif Lam Mim are separate letters from the Arabic alphabet placed at the beginning of the chapter. Their exact meaning is known only to God, and no authentic explanation from the Prophet defines them. They are not meaningless, nor were they revealed as play. They consist of ordinary letters used by people in daily speech, yet they introduce a revelation that humans are unable to match. Their placement serves as a direct demonstration of the Qur’an’s inimitability, because the Book is composed of the same letters available to all, while its content and structure remain beyond human ability to reproduce. These letters appear in varying combinations at the openings of multiple chapters to repeatedly establish this challenge and affirm the Qur’an’s miraculous nature.
