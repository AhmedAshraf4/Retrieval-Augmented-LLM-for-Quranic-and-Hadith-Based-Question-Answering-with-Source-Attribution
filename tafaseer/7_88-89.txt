Tafsir Explanation: The disbelievers threatened the Prophet Shu`ayb and his followers with expulsion from their village or forceful return to their disbelief. They rejected the call to revert to their former ways, expressing that although they hated it, they would not accept the Prophet's teachings, as doing so would be a great falsehood against Allah. They acknowledged that returning to disbelief was only possible if Allah willed it, and they placed their trust solely in Him for all their affairs. They called on Allah to judge between them and their people with truth, seeking victory over those who opposed them, affirming that Allah is the best of those who judge.
