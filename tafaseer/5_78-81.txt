Tafsir Explanation: Allah cursed the disbelievers among the Children of Israel for their disobedience and transgression, revealing this curse through His Prophets Dawud and Jesus, son of Mary. They were cursed in the Tawrah, Injil, Zabur, and Qur'an because they did not forbid each other from evil and sins, and their behavior was condemned. Allah warned that if the believers did not enjoin good and forbid evil, they would face punishment. Many Hadiths emphasize the duty of forbidding evil and promoting righteousness, including the duty to oppose sin with one's hand, tongue, or heart. Allah also censured the hypocrites, who took disbelievers as their allies instead of the believers, resulting in the anger of Allah and eternal punishment. If they had truly believed in Allah and His Prophet, they would not have betrayed the believers. The verse further mentions that the greatest enmity towards the believers comes from the Jews and polytheists, while the closest in love to the believers are those who call themselves Christians, due to their humility and reverence for their scholars.
