“And among the people are those who say, ‘We believe in God and the Last Day,’ but they do not believe. They seek to deceive God and those who believe, but they deceive only themselves, while they do not perceive.” This refers to hypocrites in Medina, especially among Aws and Khazraj, who publicly declared belief after Islam became dominant but concealed disbelief. Hypocrisy is outward conformity with inward denial. It began after the migration to Medina and the victory at Badr, when Islam gained strength and figures like Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul pretended to accept Islam to preserve status and safety. Their claim of belief was only verbal; their hearts rejected it. By showing faith while hiding disbelief, they thought teaches would protect their lives and wealth and mislead believers, but this behavior only harmed them. Their deception did not affect God; it exposed their ignorance and resulted in self-deception, since their inner rejection contradicted their public testimony.
