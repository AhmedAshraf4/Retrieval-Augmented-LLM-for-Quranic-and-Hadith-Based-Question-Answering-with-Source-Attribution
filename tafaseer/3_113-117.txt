Tafsir Explanation: The People of the Book who embraced Islam are distinct from those who rejected it. Among the former are figures like Abdullah bin Salam and others who believed in Allah, followed His laws, and embraced Prophet Muhammad. They were devoted in worship, reciting Allah's verses at night and performing prostration in prayer. They believed in the Last Day, encouraged good deeds, and forbade wrong actions, excelling in good works. For these actions, Allah ensures their good deeds will not go unrewarded. In contrast, the disbelievers, including polytheists, will find their wealth and offspring useless in averting Allah's punishment, leading them to eternal torment in the Fire. Allah compares their worldly expenditures to a destructive, cold wind that destroys harvests when they are most needed, symbolizing the destruction of their deeds due to their rejection of faith, as they wrong themselves rather than being wronged by Allah.
