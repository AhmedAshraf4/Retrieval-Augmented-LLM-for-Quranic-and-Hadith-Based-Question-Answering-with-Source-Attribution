Tafsir Explanation: The suckling period is prescribed as two years, and suckling beyond this time does not establish Tahrim (prohibition of marriage). The action of suckling, which leads to Tahrim, must occur before the child is weaned, specifically before the two-year mark. If suckling takes place after two years, no prohibition in marriage is established. The father is responsible for providing reasonable sustenance and clothing for the mother during this time. Both parents must avoid causing harm to each other with respect to the child’s care, and the responsibility of maintaining the mother’s rights extends to the father's heirs. If both parents agree to wean the child earlier than two years, this is permissible without sin, as long as the decision is made through mutual consultation. If they choose a wet nurse for the child, the agreed payment for her services must be fair and reasonable. Parents are encouraged to act with fear of Allah and be aware that Allah is ever watchful of their actions.
