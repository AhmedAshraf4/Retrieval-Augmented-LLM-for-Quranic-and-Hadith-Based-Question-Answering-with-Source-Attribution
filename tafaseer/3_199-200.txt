Tafsir Explanation: Some of the People of the Book truly believe in Allah, accepting what was revealed to Muhammad and acknowledging the previous scriptures. These individuals do not hide the knowledge they have about the Prophet Muhammad and his Ummah. Among them are the best people, whether Jews or Christians, who sincerely follow the truth. When the Qur’an is recited to them, they fall into prostration, acknowledging its truth. Such people, like a few Jewish rabbis and many Christians, embraced Islam and are rewarded twice—once for their earlier faith and once for accepting Muhammad’s prophethood. Allah promises them a reward with Him. These individuals do not exchange the verses of Allah for a small price, as others do. Some of the People of the Book wept upon hearing the Qur’an, like when Ja’far bin Abi Talib recited Surah Maryam to King An-Najashi, leading to a shared display of humility. Allah commands the believers to endure patience, be more patient, and engage in Ribat, which involves steadfastness in worship or guarding the Muslim community, like in battles or awaiting prayers. A day in Ribat is better than the whole world and its possessions. Those who die while in Ribat have their deeds continue and are protected from the trials of the grave. Allah also advises to have Taqwa, ensuring success both in this life and in the Hereafter.
