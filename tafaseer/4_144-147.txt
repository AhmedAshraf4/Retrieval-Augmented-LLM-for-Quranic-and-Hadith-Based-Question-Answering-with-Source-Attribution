Tafsir Explanation: Allah forbids believers from taking disbelievers as friends instead of fellow believers, warning against intimate relationships with them, sharing secrets, or advising them. This is emphasized by the reminder that such actions lead to Allah's punishment, except when fear of harm from them justifies it. Allah asks if believers desire to provide proof (manifest Sultan) against themselves, which would justify His torment. Hypocrites, who show disbelief while pretending to believe, will be in the lowest depths of Hell, surrounded by fiery coffins, with no helper to save them. However, if they repent sincerely, perform righteous deeds, and purify their faith for Allah, they will join the believers in the Hereafter, where Allah will grant them great reward. Allah, being self-sufficient, does not punish those who thank Him and have faith, as He appreciates and knows those whose hearts are devoted to Him.
