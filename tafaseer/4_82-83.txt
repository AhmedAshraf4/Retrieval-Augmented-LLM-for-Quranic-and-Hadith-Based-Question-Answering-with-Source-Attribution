Allah commands reflection on the Qur'an, emphasizing its consistency and truth, free from contradictions. If it were from anyone other than Allah, there would be discrepancies. The Qur'an is a truthful revelation, with both clear and ambiguous verses, the former guiding the believer while the latter is understood by those with knowledge. Those who distort its meaning are misled. Disputes over the Qur'an led the Prophet Muhammad to warn that previous nations were destroyed due to such disagreements. He instructed his companions to follow what they understood and refer unclear matters to those with knowledge. Allah also chastises those who spread unverified news, urging that matters of public concern should be investigated properly before disclosure. If people had referred their uncertainties to the Prophet or those in authority, the truth would have been clarified.
