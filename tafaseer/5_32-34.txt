Allah legislated for the Children of Israel that killing an innocent person or causing corruption on earth is as grievous as killing all of humanity, while saving a life is as if saving all of mankind. This highlights the sanctity of human life. If one refrains from shedding innocent blood, it’s as if they have preserved the lives of everyone. The verse criticizes those who continue their misdeeds despite clear guidance, such as the Jews of Medina who fought in wars during the time of Jahiliyyah, only to ransom their captives afterward. Allah condemns those who spread corruption and wage war against Him and His Messenger, decreeing severe punishments such as death, crucifixion, cutting off limbs, or exile. These punishments serve as disgrace in this world and a severe torment in the Hereafter. However, if someone repents before being apprehended, Allah’s mercy applies, and they are forgiven, as seen in examples like the repentant individuals who were granted mercy despite their past actions.
