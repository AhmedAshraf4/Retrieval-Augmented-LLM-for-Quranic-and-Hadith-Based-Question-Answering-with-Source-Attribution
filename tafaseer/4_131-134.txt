Tafsir Explanation: Allah asserts His ownership over the heavens and the earth, establishing His supreme authority. He instructed the People of the Scripture and the believers to have Taqwa, worshiping Him alone. He warns that disbelief does not diminish His sovereignty, as everything belongs to Him. Allah is not in need of His creation and is worthy of all praise. He has control over all affairs, and if He wills, He can replace people for their disobedience. Allah owns both the rewards of this world and the Hereafter, and He grants them to whom He pleases. Those who seek only worldly rewards will receive no portion in the Hereafter, while those who ask for goodness in both this life and the next will be granted it. Allah increases the reward of those who seek the Hereafter and provides the worldly enjoyment to those who desire it. Allah sees and hears everything, preferring some over others as He wills.
