In his old age, after losing hope of having children, Ibrahim and his wife Sarah were given the good news of a son, Ishaq, by the angels, who also informed them that Ishaq would be a Prophet and have his own offspring, Yaqub. This news comforted Ibrahim, as it meant his lineage would continue, especially since elderly parents are often concerned about their children’s future. Allah’s promise extended to Ibrahim’s descendants, ensuring the continuation of prophethood through Ishaq and Yaqub. This was a reward for Ibrahim’s faith and sacrifice in leaving his people to worship Allah alone. The angels further emphasized that if Ibrahim or his descendants had associated others with Allah, their deeds would have been nullified, highlighting the grave danger of shirk. Allah made prophethood a part of Ibrahim’s lineage, as He did for Nuh, ensuring that righteous successors would continue to follow His guidance. Allah entrusted the message to the Muhajirun and Ansar, who would accept it and follow the path of the Prophets. The Prophet Muhammad was specifically commanded to follow the guidance of these predecessors, who had been blessed by Allah.
