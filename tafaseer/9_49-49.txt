Tafsir Explanation: Al-Jadd bin Qays, a leader of Bani Salimah, requested permission from the Prophet Muhammad to stay behind from the battle, fearing the temptation of seeing the Roman women. He expressed his inability to resist their allure, claiming he would be unable to maintain patience. The Prophet granted his request, and the verse was revealed in response, showing that Al-Jadd's preference for staying behind and avoiding the battle was a greater trial (fitnah) than the one he falsely feared. Despite his claim of concern, his refusal to join the Prophet in jihad, prioritizing his safety over the mission, was the true trial. This incident reflects that the fitnah of avoiding jihad is more severe than the temptations he anticipated. The verse emphasizes the consequences of such choices, warning that those who reject faith will be surrounded by Hell with no escape.
