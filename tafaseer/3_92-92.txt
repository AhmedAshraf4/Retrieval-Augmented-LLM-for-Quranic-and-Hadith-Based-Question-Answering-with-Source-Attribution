Al-Birr refers to attaining Paradise, which can only be achieved by spending from what one loves. When this verse was revealed, Abu Talhah, who owned the Bayruha' garden, the most beloved of his possessions, offered it in charity, asking the Prophet to distribute it where Allah saw fit. The Prophet praised him, calling it a profitable property, and advised him to give it to his relatives. Abu Talhah followed the Prophet's guidance and distributed the garden among his family. Similarly, when `Umar was asked about his prized possession in Khaybar, the Prophet instructed him to retain the land but dedicate its fruits to charity.
