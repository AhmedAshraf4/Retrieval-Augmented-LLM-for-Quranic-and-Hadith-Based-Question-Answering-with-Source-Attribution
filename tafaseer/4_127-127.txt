Tafsir Explanation: The verse addresses the situation of a man who acts as the guardian and caretaker of a female orphan. If he desires to marry her, it is because he controls her wealth, and he may prevent her from marrying someone else to avoid sharing her wealth with another man. This practice, which occurred in pre-Islamic times, was prohibited by Allah. If the orphan girl is beautiful and wealthy, the guardian may wish to marry her, but if she is not attractive, he would refuse to let her marry at all, ultimately inheriting her wealth. Allah commands guardians to act justly with orphan girls, giving them a dowry equal to that of other women if they wish to marry them. If they do not desire to marry them, they should marry others. Guardians are also forbidden from denying orphan girls their rightful share of inheritance, and Allah sets clear instructions for ensuring justice for orphans and children, establishing their fixed shares of inheritance. The verse emphasizes the importance of fairness and justice in these matters, encouraging good deeds and reminding that Allah is aware of all actions.
