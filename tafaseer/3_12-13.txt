Tafsir Explanation: Allah commanded the Prophet Muhammad to inform the disbelievers that they would be defeated in this life and gathered to Hell on the Day of Resurrection. This was demonstrated when, after the victory at Badr, the Prophet gathered the Jews in the marketplace of Bani Qaynuqa, pointing to the clear sign of victory from Allah, which occurred during the battle of Badr. The two armies, the Muslims and the disbelieving Quraysh, faced each other in combat. The idolators were made to perceive the Muslims as being twice as numerous, while the Muslims thought the idolators were twice their number. This illusion was part of Allah’s plan to ensure victory for the Muslims. When the two sides met in battle, Allah made each side appear smaller to the other, creating a sense of encouragement for both to fight. The victory at Badr was a clear sign of Allah’s support for His believing servants and a lesson for those with understanding, highlighting Allah’s wisdom in granting victory to the faithful both in this life and in the Hereafter.
