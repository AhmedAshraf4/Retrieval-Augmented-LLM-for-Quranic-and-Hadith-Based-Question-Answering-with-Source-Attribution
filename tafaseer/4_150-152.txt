Allah condemns those who disbelieve in Him and His Messengers, specifically the Jews and Christians who accept some Prophets while rejecting others based on personal desires and the practices of their forefathers. The Jews reject Jesus and Muhammad, while the Christians reject Muhammad, the final Prophet. The Samaritans believe in no Prophet after Joshua, and the Zoroastrians follow Zoroaster but disregard his teachings. Rejecting even one Prophet equates to rejecting all, as true belief in one necessitates belief in all Prophets sent by Allah. Those who claim belief in some Prophets but reject others show their faith is false, as it is based on whim, not truth. Allah declares these people as true disbelievers, for their faith is not genuine if it is not consistent. Their punishment for this disbelief is humiliation and torment, both in this world and the Hereafter. In contrast, those who believe in all Prophets and do not make distinctions between them are promised great rewards, for their belief is sincere, and Allah is merciful and forgiving.
