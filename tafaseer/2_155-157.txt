Allah tests His servants with both blessings and hardships, including fear, hunger, loss of wealth, lives, and crops. These trials are meant to distinguish the patient (As-Sabirin). Those who are patient when afflicted with calamity recite "Truly, to Allah we belong and to Him we shall return," acknowledging their submission to Allah's will. They are rewarded with Allah's blessings, mercy, and guidance. This statement demonstrates their recognition that everything belongs to Allah and that nothing is lost with Him on the Day of Resurrection. As a result, they are honored with Allah’s praise and are among the guided ones. In a narration, it is explained that anyone who expresses this statement during affliction and asks for compensation will be rewarded by Allah, as Umm Salamah experienced when she said this after her husband's death and was later blessed with marriage to the Prophet Muhammad, whom she considered a better reward.
