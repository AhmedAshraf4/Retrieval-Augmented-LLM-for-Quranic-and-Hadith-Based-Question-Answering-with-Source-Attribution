Allah's Names are numerous, with ninety-nine being most commonly referenced, but they are not limited to just these. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) stated that whoever memorizes and preserves these Names will enter Paradise, emphasizing that Allah is Witr (the One) and loves odd-numbered things. Allah's Names encompass all His attributes, and in times of distress, the Prophet taught a supplication invoking every Name of Allah, which promises relief from sadness and grief. Allah also warns against those who deviate in His Names, such as attributing idols like Al-Lat and Al-‘Uzza to His Names, with deviation (Ilhad) meaning wickedness or straying. The grave hole, called Lahd, symbolizes deviation as it is a hole within a hole facing the Qiblah.
