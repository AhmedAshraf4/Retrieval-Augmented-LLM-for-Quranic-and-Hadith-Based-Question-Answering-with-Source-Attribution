The people of the Fire will argue and curse each other. Those who associate others with Allah and reject His signs will be told to join the nations before them, similar to them in actions, who have already been cast into the Fire. Every time a new nation enters, it will curse the one before it. On the Day of Judgment, the followers will deny those who led them astray, saying they misled them, and ask Allah to increase their punishment. The first of the nations to enter the Fire will tell the last, "You were no better than us," as they all share in the consequences of their deeds. Those who were arrogant will blame those they led astray for failing to obey guidance, while the weak will accuse them of plotting their misguidance. Each party will regret their actions and conceal their sorrow when they face the torment, which is a recompense for their deeds.
