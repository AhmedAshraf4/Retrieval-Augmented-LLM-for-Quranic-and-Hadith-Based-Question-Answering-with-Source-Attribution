Allah did not allow the hypocrites to join the believers in jihad. If they had intended to fight, they would have made preparations, but Allah made them stay behind, disapproving of their participation. Had they marched with the believers, they would have only caused disorder and sowed discord among them. They would have quickly spread falsehoods and division, and some among the believers would have listened to their harmful words, unaware of their true intentions. Some of the hypocrites, like Abdullah bin Ubayy and Al-Jadd bin Qays, were respected leaders of their people, and there were believers who might have obeyed them, further spreading mischief. Allah knew this outcome and the harm that would have resulted, as He knows the actions of all wrongdoers. He reminded that had there been any good in them, they would have listened to the truth, but they would have rejected it if given the chance.
