The chiefs of Pharaoh’s people agreed with him, calling Musa a skilled sorcerer after Pharaoh returned to his throne. They held a council to decide how to deal with him, fearing his influence might lead people to follow him and overthrow their rule. They conspired to discredit Musa by portraying him as a liar and fraud, trying to extinguish the light of truth he brought. Their fear was realized when, as Allah decreed, Pharaoh and his supporters, including Haman, faced the very downfall they had feared.
