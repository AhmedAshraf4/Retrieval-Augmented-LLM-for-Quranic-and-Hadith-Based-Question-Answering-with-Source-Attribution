The idolators boasted that maintaining Al-Masjid Al-Haram and providing water for the pilgrims were superior to embracing faith and performing Jihad. They claimed to be the guardians of the Sacred Mosque, but Allah rejected this notion, revealing that these acts, while outwardly noble, were meaningless as they were performed while committing shirk. Their arrogance in boasting about these deeds, which included providing water and serving the Ka'bah, was not enough to earn Allah's favor. Allah declared that these deeds are not equal to the faith and Jihad for His cause, as they were performed by those who denied the truth. The actions of the idolators, like their boastful talk by night, could not benefit them due to their idolatry. Allah clarified that those who commit injustice by associating others with Him cannot be guided, and their service to the Masjid would not avail them. This verse also responded to Al-‘Abbas, who, when captured in the Battle of Badr, claimed that maintaining the Masjid and providing water were acts of merit, but Allah affirmed that such deeds are not accepted while in a state of shirk.
