The verse highlights the humiliation placed upon the Jews as a consequence of their disobedience and defiance of Allah's commands. This humiliation would continue until the Day of Resurrection, with various nations, including the Greek Kushdanin, Chaldeans, and later Christians, subjugating them and requiring the payment of Jizyah. The Jews were also under the rule of Islam after Prophet Muhammad's time, where they had to pay Jizyah as well. The verse points to a future where the Jews will support the Dajjal, and alongside `Isa, son of Mary, the Muslims will defeat them. Allah's punishment is swift for those who defy Him, but His mercy is extended to those who repent. The verse balances both the warning of punishment and the hope of forgiveness.
