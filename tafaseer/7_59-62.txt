Tafsir Explanation: Nuh, the first Messenger sent by Allah after Adam, was tasked with calling his people to worship Allah alone. His people, who had begun idol worship after the deaths of righteous men, resisted his message. They had built statues of these righteous figures to remember them, which later became objects of worship. Nuh warned them of the torment on the Day of Resurrection if they continued their idolatry. His people accused him of being in error for opposing the practices of their forefathers. In response, Nuh declared that he was not misguided but a Messenger from Allah, conveying His message and offering sincere advice. He reminded them that he possessed knowledge from Allah that they did not. His stance reflected the true role of a Messenger: to deliver the message clearly, offer guidance, and advise with knowledge.
