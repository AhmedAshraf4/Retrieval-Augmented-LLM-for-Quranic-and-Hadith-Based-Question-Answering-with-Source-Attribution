Allah highlights the pleasures of this world, such as women, children, wealth, horses, cattle, and fertile land, which serve as tests and temptations. Women are mentioned first, as their allure is a greater trial for men, as the Prophet said, "I did not leave behind me a test more tempting to men than women." Enjoying women for the sake of marriage, children, and chastity is encouraged, and the Prophet praised those who marry righteous women. The desire for wealth, horses, and cattle can be either for pride and arrogance or for righteous use, such as spending on acts of worship and supporting the family. The desire for children is praised when it leads to the growth of the Ummah of Muhammad. The true reward, however, is not in these earthly pleasures but in the eternal reward of paradise, where those with taqwa are promised gardens beneath which rivers flow, purified mates, and the pleasure of Allah. This reward is far superior to the fleeting joys of this world.
