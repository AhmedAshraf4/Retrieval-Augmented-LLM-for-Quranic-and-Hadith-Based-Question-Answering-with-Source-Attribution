Tafsir Explanation: This verse addresses the idolators who associated the Jinn with Allah in worship. The idolators obeyed the Jinn's commands, worshiping idols as a result. Allah reveals that Shaytan misled them by promising false desires and guiding them to practices like altering the creation of Allah, such as cutting the ears of cattle. Allah condemns those who worshipped the Jinn and took Shaytan as their protector instead of Him, leading them to ruin. The verse refutes the false attribution of partners to Allah, emphasizing that He alone is the Creator and deserves worship. Allah is exalted beyond the lies of those who claim He has offspring, like the false claims made by Jews about Uzayr, Christians about Isa, and the Arabs about angels being daughters of Allah.
