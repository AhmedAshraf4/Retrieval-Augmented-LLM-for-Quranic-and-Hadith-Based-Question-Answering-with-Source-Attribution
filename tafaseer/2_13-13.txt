When the hypocrites are told to believe as the people have believed, they are being commanded to believe exactly as the true believers did, by affirming God, the angels, the revealed books, the messengers, resurrection after death, Paradise, and Hellfire, and by obeying God and His Messenger through commands and prohibitions. The hypocrites respond by mocking this command and saying they will not believe as the fools believe, intending by this insult the Companions of the Messenger. They view equality with the believers in faith and obedience as humiliation and consider the believers ignorant and lacking judgment. God answers this claim by declaring that the hypocrites themselves are the fools, because foolishness is ignorance of what truly benefits or harms. Their condition is worse because they do not recognize their own ignorance, remain unaware of their deviation, and persist in blindness while thinking themselves correct.
