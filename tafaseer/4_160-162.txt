Because of the Jews' injustice and transgression, Allah prohibited certain foods that were previously lawful for them. This change occurred as a consequence of their distortion of the Scriptures and their extreme actions in the religion. Before the Tawrah was revealed, all foods were allowed for them, except for the camel's meat and milk, which Isra'il (Jacob) had made unlawful for himself. Later, Allah imposed further prohibitions in the Tawrah due to their disobedience, including the forbidding of animals with undivided hooves and the fat of cattle and sheep, except for specific parts. These prohibitions were a punishment for their rebellion, defiance of their Messenger, and disputes with him. Their actions of hindering others from Allah's path continued throughout history, including the rejection of both Muhammad and Jesus. Additionally, despite being forbidden from taking usury (riba), they persisted in it through deceitful practices, unjustly consuming others' wealth. In response to their actions, Allah prepared a painful punishment for the disbelievers among them. However, those among the Jews who were firmly grounded in knowledge and believed in the revelations sent to Muhammad, as well as the preceding scriptures, gave charity (zakah), and believed in Allah and the Last Day, would be rewarded with a great reward, namely Paradise.
