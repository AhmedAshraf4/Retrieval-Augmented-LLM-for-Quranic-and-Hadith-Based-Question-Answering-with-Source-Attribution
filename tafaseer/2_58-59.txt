After leaving Egypt with Moses, the Children of Israel were commanded to fight the Canaanites and enter the holy land, which was Jerusalem, but they refused out of weakness and fear, so God punished them with forty years of wandering. After the wandering ended, God granted them victory under Joshua son of Nun, holding back the sun until the conquest was completed. When they entered the city, they were commanded to enter the gate in submission, bowing in humility, and to say a word seeking forgiveness so their sins would be forgiven and their reward increased. Instead, the wrongdoers deliberately altered both the action and the words: they entered arrogantly with raised heads, some dragging themselves mockingly, and they replaced the commanded word of repentance with a distorted phrase of ridicule. Because they changed the command in word and deed after being shown mercy and victory, God sent down a punishment from heaven upon them as a consequence of their rebellion and defiance.
