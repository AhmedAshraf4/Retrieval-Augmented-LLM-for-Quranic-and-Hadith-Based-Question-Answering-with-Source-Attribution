Tafsir Explanation: Umar once asked for a clear ruling on alcohol (khamr), prompting the revelation of the verse stating its great sin and some benefits. While the benefits include physical pleasures like digestion and a sense of joy, these are outweighed by the harm it causes to one's mind and religion. Despite this, Umar continued to seek a clear prohibition. Later, a verse was revealed forbidding believers from approaching prayer while intoxicated. Even after this, Umar sought further clarification, leading to the final prohibition in Surah Al-Ma’idah, which described intoxicants as an abomination of Satan's handiwork, urging believers to avoid them to achieve success. This culminated in a decisive ban on khamr, with the ultimate harm outweighing the benefits. The verse also highlights the responsibility of those managing orphans’ property, stating that the best approach is to work honestly with their belongings and to treat them as brothers, with Allah knowing those who act with good intentions from those who cause harm.
