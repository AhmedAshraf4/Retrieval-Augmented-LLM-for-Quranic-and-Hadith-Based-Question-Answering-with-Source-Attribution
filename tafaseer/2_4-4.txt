Those who believe in what has been revealed to you and what was revealed before you, and who are certain of the Hereafter, are described as people who affirm everything God sent through Muhammad and through all earlier messengers without rejecting or separating any of them. Their belief includes all earlier scriptures as revelation from the same Lord. Their certainty in the Hereafter is specific and concrete: resurrection after death, standing for judgment, reckoning of deeds, the scale that weighs actions, Paradise, and the Fire. These qualities complete the earlier description of believers who believe in the unseen, establish prayer, and give from what they were provided, because belief in the unseen and worship cannot exist without accepting all divine revelations and the reality of the final return. This description applies to every true believer, regardless of origin, including humans and jinn. Faithful people from earlier scriptures who accept Islam believe both in their original books and in what was revealed to Muhammad, and for this they receive double reward, while other believers may surpass them through firmer and more comprehensive faith.
