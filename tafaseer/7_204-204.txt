Allah commanded that the Qur'an be listened to with respect and honor when it is recited, contrasting the behavior of the Quraysh pagans who mocked its recitation by advising others not to listen and to make noise. This command was revealed after the Quraysh rejected the Qur'an, specifically after they had made efforts to disrupt its recitation. The verse was also a response to the practice of greeting one another during the prayer, which was halted upon the revelation of this command. The ayah emphasizes the importance of listening attentively to the Qur'an when it is recited.
