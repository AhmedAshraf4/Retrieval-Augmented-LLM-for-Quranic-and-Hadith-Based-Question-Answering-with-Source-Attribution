“And who is more unjust than one who prevents Allah’s name from being mentioned in His places of worship and strives for their destruction” refers to the Quraysh idolaters who blocked the Prophet from entering Mecca at Hudaybiyyah, prevented him from praying at the Kaaba, and restricted the Sacred Mosque for idols and polytheism. Their striving for ruin means stopping prayer, remembrance, pilgrimage, and visitation by those who maintained the mosques through worship, not physical demolition. They justified this by refusing entry to those who killed their leaders at Badr, even though no one had previously barred access to the House. This wrongdoing was fulfilled in reversal: after the conquest of Mecca, idolaters were barred from the Sacred Mosque, allowed entry only under fear or treaty, and later forbidden entirely unless they accepted Islam. Just as they expelled believers and blocked worship, they themselves were expelled and prevented from entry, earning disgrace in this life and severe punishment in the Hereafter for violating the sanctity of the House by idols, false invocation, and naked circumambulation.
