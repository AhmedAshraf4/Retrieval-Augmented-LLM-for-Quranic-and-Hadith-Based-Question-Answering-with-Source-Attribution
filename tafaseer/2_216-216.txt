Allah made Jihad obligatory for Muslims to fight against the enemies who transgress against Islam. This includes those who actively participate in combat as well as those who support the cause through aid or by marching forth when commanded. The Prophet emphasized that after the conquest of Makkah, only Jihad and good intentions remained, with the requirement to march forth if called. The difficulty of fighting is acknowledged, as it involves risks like being killed or wounded, but it leads to victory, control over enemy lands, and their resources. Allah also warns that while one may desire to avoid fighting, such a preference can lead to greater harm, such as the enemy's victory. Ultimately, Allah's knowledge surpasses human understanding, and His commands should be followed for true guidance.
