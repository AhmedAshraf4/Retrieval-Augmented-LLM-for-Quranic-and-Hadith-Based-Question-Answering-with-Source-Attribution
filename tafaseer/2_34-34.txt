Allah commanded the angels to prostrate before Adam as an act of obedience to Allah and an honor granted to Adam. The angels carried out the command. Iblis was included in the command because he lived among the angels and imitated their worship, even though he was from the jinn. He refused to prostrate, showed arrogance, and disobeyed the command. His refusal was based on envy and pride, claiming superiority because he was created from fire while Adam was created from clay. This arrogance was the cause of his disbelief and expulsion from divine mercy. The prostration itself was directed to Adam, but the act of obedience belonged solely to Allah. Prostration as a form of honor was permitted for earlier nations, as seen when Joseph’s parents prostrated before him, but it was later prohibited for this community.
