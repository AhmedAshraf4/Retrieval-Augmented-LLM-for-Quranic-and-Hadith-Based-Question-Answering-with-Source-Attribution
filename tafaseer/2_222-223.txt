The verse addresses the prohibition of sexual intercourse with menstruating women, which was a practice different from that of the Jews who avoided contact with them altogether. Allah instructs that men should keep away from their wives during menstruation until they are purified, which is interpreted as the cessation of bleeding and the performance of a purification ritual, typically a bath. The Prophet Muhammad clarified that physical intimacy, other than intercourse, was allowed during menstruation, as long as it did not involve the sexual organ. The verse also emphasizes that sexual relations should only occur in the vagina and forbids anal sex, which was considered by some as permissible before the revelation. Allah encourages repentance for those who err in such matters and stresses the importance of purity, both physical and spiritual, in the relationship between spouses. The act of "going to the tilth" refers to sexual intercourse in the permissible manner, reinforcing the concept of modesty and lawful conduct in marital relations.
