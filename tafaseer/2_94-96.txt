The Jews claimed that the home of the Hereafter belonged to them alone and that only Jews or Christians would enter Paradise, so they were challenged to call for death if their claim was true, meaning to invoke God to destroy the lying party between them and the Muslims. They refused to make this invocation, because they knew the Prophet was true and they had knowingly rejected him, and their refusal exposed their falsehood. It is stated that if they had asked for death, they would have perished immediately and seen their punishment. They never wished for death because of the deeds they had already committed, and because they feared what awaited them after death. They were described as the most eager of people for long life, even more than idolaters, yet long life would not remove punishment or delay the final outcome. Their desire to live for hundreds or thousands of years would not protect them from judgment, just as prolonged life did not benefit Satan. Death would inevitably meet them despite their attempts to flee it, and God fully sees their actions and will repay them accordingly.
