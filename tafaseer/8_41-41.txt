Tafsir Explanation: The spoils of war (Ghanimah) are specifically allocated to the Muslims, with one-fifth designated for Allah and His Messenger. Ghanimah refers to war booty gained through military efforts, while Fai refers to property forfeited by disbelievers, such as when they surrender or leave behind wealth after death. The one-fifth share for Allah and His Messenger must be paid in full, even for minor items like needles. Any deceit in handling the spoils, such as stealing before distribution, will result in severe consequences on the Day of Resurrection. The Prophet Muhammad, when leading his armies, would reserve the share for Allah and the Messenger and distribute the remaining four-fifths among the soldiers. The Prophet clarified that even a soldier's personal item, like an arrow, has no more right to it than his fellow Muslim. Additionally, the Prophet selected certain items, such as weapons or slaves, from the war booty for himself, but the majority was shared with the army. The share for the Prophet's relatives was directed to Banu Hashim and Banu Al-Muttalib, who supported him during his early trials. Allah also mandated support for orphans and travelers in need. Adherence to these rulings reflects true faith in Allah, with surrendering the war spoils being a key act of belief. The Day of Badr is referred to as Yawm Al-Furqan, marking the decisive victory where truth was distinguished from falsehood.
