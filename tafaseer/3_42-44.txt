The angels spoke to Maryam, informing her that Allah had chosen her for her devotion, modesty, honor, and innocence, and that she was favored over all women. The Prophet Muhammad confirmed her virtue, saying she was the best of women of her time, alongside Khadijah. Allah instructed Maryam to increase her worship, humbleness, and submission, which would prepare her for the miraculous birth of her son, Isa, without male intervention. The test of her obedience elevated her status in both this life and the Hereafter. The verse also recalls the incident where the custodians of Maryam were chosen through a lottery; Zakariyya won and took her into his care, as Maryam's mother had vowed to dedicate her to the service of the Masjid. The event occurred when the rabbis, including Zakariyya, cast lots with their pens, and the pen that remained afloat in the river signified Zakariyya’s responsibility over Maryam.
