The idolators forbade certain types of cattle, such as the Wasilah, which they designated as sacred and not to be used for ordinary purposes. They claimed that only certain people could eat from these cattle, while others were prohibited. This practice was a fabrication against Allah, as they falsely attributed these prohibitions to Him. The cattle in question, like the Bahirah, Sa'ibah, Wasilah, and Ham, were treated in ways that prevented them from being used for work or consumption. Additionally, they did not mention Allah’s Name when slaughtering these animals or using them for other purposes. These acts were not divinely sanctioned but were instead inventions of the idolators, who falsely imposed these restrictions. Allah rejected their claims and will recompense them for the lies they fabricated against Him.
