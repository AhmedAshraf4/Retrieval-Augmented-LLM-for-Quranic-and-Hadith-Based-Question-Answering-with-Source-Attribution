The Prophet Muhammad was instructed by Allah to call the idolators to listen to what Allah has forbidden for them, beginning with the prohibition of associating partners with Him, as it is a command for all to understand. Allah emphasizes the importance of worshiping Him alone, a principle which is reiterated in several hadiths, including the promise that those who die without associating partners with Allah will enter Paradise, regardless of their other sins. The Prophet also highlighted the importance of being dutiful and kind to one's parents, even if they were idolaters, as commanded by Allah. Furthermore, Allah forbids killing children out of fear of poverty, assuring that He provides sustenance for both parents and children. Immoral sins, both open and secret, are prohibited, as well as unjustified killing, with specific exceptions such as for those who commit adultery, life for life, or those who abandon their faith. Lastly, Allah commands not to kill anyone whose life is sacred, emphasizing the seriousness of this prohibition.
