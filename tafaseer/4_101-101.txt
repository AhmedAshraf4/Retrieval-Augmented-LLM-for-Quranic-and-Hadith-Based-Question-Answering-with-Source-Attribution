When traveling, Muslims are allowed to shorten the prayer from four Rak'ahs to two. This concession was revealed due to the circumstances at the time, where Muslims often feared attacks from disbelievers during travel. In the early days of Islam, after the Hijrah, Muslims faced constant threats from enemies in most areas, making fear prevalent. This led to the allowance for shortening prayers during such times of fear or danger. The Prophet Muhammad, when asked about this, confirmed that it was a gift from Allah, showing that it was a divine concession. This practice was demonstrated when the Prophet prayed two Rak'ahs during his travel to Makkah and when he prayed in Mina during a time of safety.
