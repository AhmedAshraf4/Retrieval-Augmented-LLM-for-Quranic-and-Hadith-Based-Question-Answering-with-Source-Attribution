Tafsir Explanation: Allah reminds the believers of His favor by sending them the Messenger and establishing Islam. He took a covenant from them to follow, support, and convey the message of the Prophet, which they accepted with a pledge of obedience. Allah calls on them to have taqwa and reminds them that He knows the secrets of their hearts. Believers are commanded to stand for justice and be truthful, even if they have enmity with others. Allah warns them not to let hatred prevent justice, as fairness leads to greater taqwa. He promises forgiveness and great reward for those who believe and act righteously, while those who disbelieve will face Hell. Allah protected the Muslims from enemies who plotted to harm them, such as the incident with the Bedouin man who threatened the Prophet but was disarmed by Allah's protection. This is followed by the mention of the plot by Banu An-Nadir to kill the Prophet, which Allah thwarted, allowing the Prophet to return safely and later expel them from Medina.
