Tafsir Explanation: Allah forbids the People of the Book from going to extremes in religion, a tendency particularly seen among the Christians, who elevated Jesus from being a prophet to being a god, worshipped alongside Allah. They exaggerated further by claiming that his followers were divinely inspired and followed every word they said, regardless of truth. This led to the idolization of their rabbis and monks, whom they took as lords besides Allah. Allah clarifies that Jesus is only a servant and messenger, created by His word "Be," and a spirit from Him. Jesus was not divine, as he was created by Allah’s command, just as Adam was created from dust. Mary, his mother, was a righteous woman, and both of them were signs for humanity. Jesus, like any other messenger, was a servant of Allah and had no divine nature. Allah commands belief in His oneness and the messengership of all His prophets, including Jesus, and forbids attributing partners to Him. The Christian belief in a trinity and the divinity of Jesus is rejected, as Allah is above such claims, and all creation belongs to Him alone.
