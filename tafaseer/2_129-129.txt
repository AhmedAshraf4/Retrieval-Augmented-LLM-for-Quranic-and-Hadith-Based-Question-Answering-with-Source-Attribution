Ibrahim prayed for security and provision for the people of the Sacred Area and invoked Allah to send a Messenger from his offspring, which was fulfilled when Muhammad was sent among the Ummiyyin, non-Arabs, Jinn, and mankind. Ibrahim was the first to mention this Prophet, and later Jesus confirmed it to the Children of Israel, giving glad tidings of a Messenger named Ahmad. The Prophet said that this was the supplication of Ibrahim and the glad tidings of Jesus. His mother saw a light emerge from her that illuminated the palaces of Ash-Sham while pregnant, and this light later appeared in Ash-Sham, signaling the establishment of Islam there. Ash-Sham will remain a refuge for Islam, and Jesus will descend near the eastern white minaret in Damascus. Allah instructs in the Book, meaning the Qur’an, and in wisdom, meaning the Sunnah or comprehension of religion, and He purifies through obedience to Him, demonstrating His might and wisdom in all affairs.
