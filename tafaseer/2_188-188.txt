Bribery is prohibited, and consuming what is not rightfully yours is a sin. The verse addresses the situation of an indebted person who denies a loan without evidence, even though they know the money is not theirs. The case may go to authorities, but the individual still knows they are guilty. The ruling of a judge does not change the truth of a matter, and if the judgment is incorrect, the wrongdoer will still face the consequences on the Day of Judgment. The ruling is based on the judge’s best judgment, and if they err, the one receiving the unjust ruling will bear the burden of their wrong. This is why the verse warns against consuming others' property unjustly and bribing rulers to manipulate decisions. Ultimately, false claims will not stand before Allah, who will judge fairly.
