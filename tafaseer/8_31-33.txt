Tafsir Explanation: The Quraysh boasted they could produce something like the Qur'an, with some claiming, "We have heard it, and we can say the like of this." This claim was a false boast to deceive others, as they failed to meet the challenge to produce even a single chapter like it. An-Nadr bin Al-Harith, who had learned stories from Persia, would compare his tales to the Qur'an, asking, "Who has better tales, I or Muhammad?" When captured at Badr, he was executed by the Prophet. The Quraysh also dismissed the Qur'an as mere "tales of the ancients," falsely claiming Muhammad had copied them. They demanded Allah's punishment, saying, "If this is the truth, rain down stones on us." This was a sign of their ignorance and stubbornness, in contrast to seeking guidance. Allah responded that He would not punish them while the Prophet was among them or while they sought forgiveness. The Prophet's presence and their repentance acted as shields against immediate torment. After the Prophet's death, the act of seeking forgiveness would remain as a means of protection until the Day of Judgment.
