Allah commanded the Prophet Muhammad to forgive those who wronged him and to overlook their bad behavior, showing leniency for their mistakes. This command was fulfilled during a period of ten years, after which Allah instructed the Prophet to be stern with the idolaters. The verse also highlights the importance of pardoning those who harm, giving to those who deprive, and maintaining relations with those who sever ties. The Prophet was advised to repel evil with kindness, as this could turn enemies into close friends. This approach of forgiveness and patience is exemplified in the actions of figures like Umar, who, despite being provoked, adhered to Allah's command to show forgiveness and avoid reacting to ignorance. The verse also advises seeking refuge with Allah from the whispers of Shaytan, especially when angered, as Shaytan seeks to turn one away from patience and forgiveness.
