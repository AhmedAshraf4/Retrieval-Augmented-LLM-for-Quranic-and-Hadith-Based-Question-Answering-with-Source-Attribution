As-Safa and Al-Marwah are symbols of Allah, and it is not a sin for those performing Hajj or Umrah to walk between them. This was revealed in response to the Ansar, who hesitated to perform Tawaf between the two mountains due to their previous idol worship practices. In the pre-Islamic period, they had associated the mountains with the idol Manat, which led to their reluctance in performing Tawaf. After the revelation, the Prophet Muhammad established it as a Sunnah to perform Tawaf between As-Safa and Al-Marwah, which is now an integral part of Hajj and Umrah. The origin of this practice traces back to Hajar’s search for water for her son, Isma'il, which led to the Zamzam well. Allah rewarded her humility, and those performing the Sa'i (the act of walking between As-Safa and Al-Marwah) should reflect on her example of humbleness and trust in Allah. This act is a reminder of Allah’s guidance and forgiveness. Allah recognizes and rewards even small deeds, promising immense rewards for those who do good.
