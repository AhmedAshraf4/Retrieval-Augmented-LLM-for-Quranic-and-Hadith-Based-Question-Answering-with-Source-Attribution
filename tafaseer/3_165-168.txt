Tafsir Explanation: The defeat at Uhud occurred after the Muslims suffered seventy fatalities, while at Badr, they had killed seventy Mushriks and captured seventy. When the Muslims faced the disaster at Uhud, they questioned its cause, and Allah clarified that it was due to their own actions, particularly their decision to take ransom at Badr and the disobedience of the archers who abandoned their positions during the battle. Allah decreed the events of Uhud by His will to test the believers' patience and to expose the hypocrisy of those who refused to fight, such as the followers of Abdullah bin Ubayy. These hypocrites claimed they would have fought if they had known a battle would take place, although it was clear that the enemy had come with a larger force. They also blamed the death of their brethren on the decision to fight, but Allah challenged their claims by stating that if they truly believed in their ability to avoid death, they should avert it themselves. The hypocrisy of Abdullah bin Ubayy and his followers was evident as they pretended to support the believers while concealing their true intentions.
