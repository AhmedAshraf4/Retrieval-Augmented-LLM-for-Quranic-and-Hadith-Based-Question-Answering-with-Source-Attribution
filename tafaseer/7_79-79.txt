The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) addressed prominent disbelievers such as Abu Jahl, Utbah, and Shaybah, asking if they had found the punishment promised by their Lord to be true, while affirming that he had found the victory promised by his Lord to be true. When Umar questioned why the Prophet was speaking to those who were already dead in their hearts, the Prophet replied that they heard him as well as anyone, but they could not respond. This reflects the disbelievers' inability to benefit from the message, similar to how Prophet Salih (peace be upon him) told his people that he had conveyed the message of his Lord and offered sincere advice, but they rejected it. Likewise, Prophet Lot (peace be upon him) confronted his people with their immoral actions, highlighting that no one before them had committed such transgressions.
