Tafsir Explanation: Allah forbids believers from taking Jews and Christians as allies because they are enemies of Islam. Those who befriend them are considered to be like them. A report from Umar reveals that he expelled a Christian scribe after learning of his religion, citing the prohibition in the verse. The verse warns against the hypocrisy of those with doubts in their hearts, who hasten to form alliances with Jews and Christians out of fear of potential harm, such as a defeat by the disbelievers. However, Allah promises that He may grant victory or a decision that would lead to the disbelievers' subjugation, leaving the hypocrites regretful. The believers will then recognize these hypocrites, who swore their allegiance but ultimately found their actions to be in vain, with their efforts leading to loss and failure.
