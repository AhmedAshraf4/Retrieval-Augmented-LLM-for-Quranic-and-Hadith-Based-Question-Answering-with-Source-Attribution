Tafsir Explanation: Allah permits indirectly mentioning marriage during the Iddah period for a widow, such as expressing a desire for marriage without making a direct proposal. This was exemplified when the Prophet instructed Fatimah bint Qays to wait for her Iddah after her husband’s third divorce, and upon completion, Usamah bin Zayd was allowed to marry her. However, no marriage proposals, direct or indirect, are allowed for a divorced woman until her Iddah is completed. Allah commands not to make secret promises of marriage, like asking a woman to promise not to marry another after her Iddah, but encourages speaking honorably, such as expressing desire for someone like her. Marriage contracts during the Iddah are invalid, and no marriage bond should be finalized until the Iddah ends. Allah warns that He knows the thoughts in people's hearts and urges them to be mindful of Him, as He is Forgiving and Forbearing.
