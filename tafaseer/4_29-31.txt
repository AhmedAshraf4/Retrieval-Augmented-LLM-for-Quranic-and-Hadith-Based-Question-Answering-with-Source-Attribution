Tafsir Explanation: Allah prohibits His believing servants from acquiring each other's property through dishonest methods, such as Riba, gambling, and other deceptive practices that may appear lawful but are, in reality, meant to deal in interest. This includes trading where there is no mutual consent, as exemplified by the scenario where a man buys a garment and either keeps it or returns it with an additional amount. Allah allows commercial transactions with mutual consent, such as buying, selling, or gifting, and emphasizes that both parties retain the right to change their minds as long as they have not parted ways. He also forbids self-harm, highlighting that taking one's own life or engaging in sin through aggression or injustice will lead to severe punishment in the Fire. If believers avoid major sins, Allah promises to forgive minor ones and grant them entry into Paradise. The Seven Destructive Sins include associating partners with Allah, murder, magic, consuming Riba, taking an orphan's wealth, fleeing the battlefield, and accusing chaste women. Among the greatest sins are false testimony and being undutiful to one's parents, with actions such as cursing one's own parents being especially severe. These actions are grave, and their consequences are sternly warned against in the hadiths of the Prophet Muhammad.
