Allah declares the dissolution of all obligations to the idolators and grants them a four-month period of safety, after which they are to be met with the sword wherever they are found. The idolators are denied refuge while persisting in disbelief and shirk, except for those with whom a treaty was made near Al-Masjid Al-Haram on the day of Hudaybiyyah. These included those who had opposed the Muslims and hindered them from reaching the Sacred Mosque. As long as such people adhere to the treaty, the Muslims are to honor their commitment, as Allah loves those with Taqwa. The treaty between the Muslims and the Quraysh held until the Quraysh broke it by aiding their allies, Banu Bakr, in killing members of the Khuzaah tribe in the Sacred Area. This breach led to the Muslims' military action, and in the eighth year of Hijrah, the Sacred Area was conquered by the Muslims. Following this, the Quraysh who embraced Islam were freed, while those who remained in disbelief were given four months of safety to move freely. Among those granted this period were Safwan bin Umayyah and Ikrimah bin Abi Jahl, who later accepted Islam and became strong believers.
