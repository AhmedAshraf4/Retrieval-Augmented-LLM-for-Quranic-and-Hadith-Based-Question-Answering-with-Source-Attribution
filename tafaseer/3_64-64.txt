This verse addresses the People of the Book—Jews and Christians—and invites them to a common agreement, emphasizing the worship of Allah alone without associating any partners with Him. It calls for rejecting all false deities and for no one to take others as lords besides Allah. This message was the core of all previous Prophets' teachings. If they refuse the call, the Muslims are instructed to declare their submission to Islam. The verse is connected to the Prophet’s letter to Heraclius, urging him to embrace Islam for safety and reward. The delegation of Najran, which came before the conquest of Makkah, responded to a similar message. This event preceded the revelation of the Jizyah law, which was later confirmed by the Qur’an, harmonizing with the earlier call.
