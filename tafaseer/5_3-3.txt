Tafsir Explanation: Allah forbids the consumption of certain animals, such as those that die before being slaughtered (Maytah), due to the harm caused by the blood that clogs in their veins. Fish, even when dead, and locusts are exceptions. Blood, specifically flowing blood, is also prohibited, but liver and spleen are allowed. The flesh of swine is forbidden, including its fat. Animals slaughtered with the name of other than Allah, or those that die from strangulation, a violent blow, a headlong fall, or being gored by another animal, are unlawful, unless they are slaughtered while still alive. If an animal is partially eaten by a wild animal, it is also prohibited. If the animal is still alive when slaughtered after being injured, it is lawful. Allah also forbids the consumption of animals sacrificed at altars dedicated to idols. Additionally, using arrows (Al-Azlam) for decision-making, a practice common in pre-Islamic Arabia, is forbidden. Allah declares that the religion has been perfected for the believers, and they should not fear the disbelievers but rather fear Allah, who has completed His favor upon them by choosing Islam as their religion. In cases of extreme necessity, such as severe hunger, eating forbidden animals is allowed, but only when there is no intention to sin, as Allah is forgiving and merciful.
