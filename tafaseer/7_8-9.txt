Tafsir Explanation: On the Day of Resurrection, the deeds of every individual will be weighed with absolute justice, as Allah will not wrong anyone. The deeds, though intangible, will be given physical weight. Those whose deeds are heavy will be rewarded with a pleasant life in Paradise, while those whose deeds are light will be cast into the fiercely blazing Fire of Hell. The balance of deeds is a true measure, and on that day, not even the smallest action will be overlooked. For example, if a person has a single card inscribed with "La ilaha illallah" that outweighs an innumerable number of scrolls filled with sins, the card will prevail. This event will be accompanied by tangible displays, such as the Qur'an appearing as a young man to its reciter, or good deeds appearing as a pleasant young man to the believer, while the opposite will occur for the disbeliever. The deeds, the books of records, or even the individual performing them can all be weighed, with Allah ensuring perfect justice in the process.
