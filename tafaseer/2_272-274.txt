The verse addresses the allowance of giving charity to polytheists, particularly to relatives. Initially, Muslims disliked giving charity to their polytheist relatives, but were later permitted to do so. The verse emphasizes that charity given in good intention, seeking Allah’s pleasure, will be repaid fully without injustice. It teaches that the reward for charity is tied to one’s intention, not the recipient’s actions. A story is shared about a man who gave charity unknowingly to an adulteress, a rich person, and a thief, and each time he praised Allah. He was later informed that his charity was accepted, as it could potentially benefit the recipients in different ways. The verse also clarifies the deserving recipients of charity, especially the poor, including migrants who cannot support themselves and those who do not ask for help despite their need. Allah knows all acts of charity, and He promises reward for those who spend their wealth sincerely in His cause, both in secret and openly. Those who do so will receive a reward from Allah, with no fear or grief on the Day of Resurrection.
