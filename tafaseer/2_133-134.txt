When Jacob approached death, he advised his children to worship Allah alone without partners. He asked them what they would worship after him, and they replied that they would worship the God of Jacob’s fathers, Abraham, Ishmael, and Isaac, affirming monotheism and submission to Allah. Mentioning Ishmael, Jacob’s uncle, reflects the Arab practice of calling an uncle a father. Jacob emphasized singling out Allah in divinity and obedience. The ayah shows that worship is personal and inherited lineage provides no benefit; each person is accountable for their own deeds, as previous nations earned their own rewards and others theirs. The unity of the Prophets’ message is affirmed: all were sent to call to the worship of Allah alone, even if their laws differed.
