This ayah outlines the procedure for witnessing a will before death. It requires two just witnesses from among the believers, though non-Muslims from the People of the Book may serve as witnesses when traveling and in the absence of Muslims. The witnesses are to swear by Allah after the prayer, affirming they have not been influenced by worldly gain or personal ties, even if the beneficiary is a close relative. If the witnesses are found guilty of dishonesty or theft regarding the will, they are replaced by two others from the rightful heirs who swear their testimony is more truthful. This process ensures the testimony remains truthful and prevents distortion. The heirs swear to assert their claim and to protect the integrity of the will, with the fear that false testimony could be exposed. The importance of fearing Allah and adhering to His commands is emphasized, and those who rebel against this guidance will not receive Allah’s guidance.
