Allah took a covenant from the Children of Israel and raised Mount Sinai above them to compel their acceptance and obedience. They verbally acknowledged hearing the command but acted in disobedience by breaking the covenant soon after. Their hearts became filled with devotion to the calf, meaning its worship took firm hold within them due to their disbelief. This exposed the falsehood of their claim to faith, since their actions consisted of rejecting Allah’s signs, opposing the prophets, violating the covenant, worshipping the calf instead of Allah, and later disbelieving in Muhammad, the final messenger sent to all people. These deeds showed that what they called faith only commanded them to persist in rebellion and disbelief.
