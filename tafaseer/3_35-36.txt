Tafsir Explanation: The mother of Maryam, named Hannah, was barren and prayed to Allah for a child. After her prayer was accepted, she became pregnant and vowed to dedicate her child to the service of Bayt Al-Maqdis. When she gave birth, she was surprised to find it was a girl, and she named her Maryam, acknowledging the differences between males and females in their roles. She sought Allah's protection for Maryam and her offspring, particularly from Shaytan. Allah accepted her prayer, and as a result, Maryam and her son, Isa, were protected from Shaytan's touch at birth, unlike other children.
