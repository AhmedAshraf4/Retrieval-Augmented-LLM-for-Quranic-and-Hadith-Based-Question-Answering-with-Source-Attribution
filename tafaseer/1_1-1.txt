In the Name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. All praise is due to Allah, Lord of all creation. This opening chapter was revealed in Makkah and is the opener of the Book and the chapter by which the prayer begins. It is called the Mother of the Book and the seven repeatedly recited verses, and it is described as the greatest chapter of the Qur’an and as a light given to this Prophet that was not given to earlier prophets. It is called the Prayer because it is required for the validity of prayer, and whoever does not recite it in a prayer has an incomplete prayer. Allah divided this recitation between Himself and His servant: when the servant praises Allah and describes Him as the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful and Owner of the Day of Recompense, Allah affirms the servant’s praise and glorification; when the servant says, “You alone we worship and You alone we ask for help,” Allah says this is shared between Him and His servant; and when the servant asks for guidance to the straight path—the path of those favored, not those who earned anger or went astray—Allah says this request is for the servant and will be granted. It is also used as a remedy, as a Companion recited it as a healing for a poisoned tribal leader and the Prophet affirmed it as a valid remedy. The phrase “In the Name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful” begins the recitation, and “Allah” is the Lord’s proper name, while “the Most Gracious” indicates mercy encompassing all creation and “the Most Merciful” indicates mercy especially for the believers.
