Ibrahim questioned his people, challenging them about their belief in false gods, asserting that he feared no one but Allah, whose will alone determines harm and benefit. He emphasized that these idols could not affect him and that Allah’s knowledge encompasses all things. Ibrahim’s reasoning mirrored that of Prophet Hud, who similarly confronted his people with the futility of idol worship. Ibrahim highlighted the inconsistency of fearing powerless idols while ignoring the severe consequences of associating partners with Allah, a practice for which no divine authority had been given. He posed a clear challenge: which party—those who worship the true God or those who worship powerless idols—deserved security? Allah confirmed Ibrahim’s argument, affirming that those who believe in Him and avoid the injustice of shirk will find security and guidance in both this life and the Hereafter. Allah granted Ibrahim this proof, raising him in status, and declared His wisdom and knowledge in guiding or misguiding people according to His will.
