God addresses the Children of Israel, calling them to submit and follow Muhammad, and reminds them of their father Jacob, a prophet who obeyed God, as a model to follow. They are commanded to remember God’s concrete favors: rescue from Pharaoh, deliverance from slavery, water brought from stone, manna and quail, and the sending of prophets and revealed books among them. They are ordered to fulfill the covenant taken from them, which was to believe in and follow Muhammad when he was sent; in return, God would fulfill His promise by forgiving sins, admitting them to Paradise, and removing restrictions imposed because of earlier disobedience. They are warned to fear God alone and the punishment that afflicted their forefathers if they turn away. They are commanded to believe in what was sent down confirming what they already possess, because the Qur’an confirms the Torah and contains the description of Muhammad known to them. They are warned not to be the first among the Children of Israel to disbelieve in the Qur’an and the Messenger, despite their prior knowledge, and not to trade God’s revelations for worldly gain by hiding or distorting the truth. They are commanded to fear God alone by obeying Him and not opposing the Messenger.
