When Allah revealed the prohibition against approaching orphans' property except to improve it, guardians of orphans began separating their food and drink from that of the orphans. Any leftover food or drink was kept for the orphan until they could eat it or it spoiled. This practice became difficult for the companions, and they sought guidance from the Prophet Muhammad, who then conveyed Allah’s command that mixing the orphan’s affairs with one’s own is permissible as long as it benefits them. This instruction eased their difficulty, and they started mixing their food and drink with that of the orphans. Allah further commanded justice in dealings, emphasizing the need for full measure and weight and warning against cheating. He also stressed that people must speak justly, even when dealing with close relatives. Lastly, Allah instructed believers to follow His straight path and avoid other paths that would lead them astray, warning that these paths are led by devils. The Prophet Muhammad demonstrated this by drawing a straight line and marking diverging paths, symbolizing the dangers of deviating from Allah's path.
