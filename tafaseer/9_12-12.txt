Tafsir Explanation: If the idolators, with whom peace treaties were made for a set time, break their oaths and attack Islam with criticism and disapproval, they are to be fought. This includes those who curse the Messenger or attack Islam. The term "fight the leaders of disbelief" refers to fighting those whose oaths hold no value, with the goal of stopping their rebellion and disbelief. The verse applies not just to the Quraysh but to all idolators. Specific figures like Abu Jahl and Umayyah bin Khalaf were initially targeted, but the principle remains general. The verse was fulfilled when Abu Bakr, upon sending an army to Ash-Sham, instructed them to strike against those resembling the disbelievers, emphasizing the importance of dealing with the leaders of disbelief as the verse commanded.
