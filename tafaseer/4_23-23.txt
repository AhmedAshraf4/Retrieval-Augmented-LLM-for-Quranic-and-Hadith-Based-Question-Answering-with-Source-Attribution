This verse establishes the categories of women who are prohibited for marriage due to blood relations, suckling, or marriage. It includes seven categories of blood relations and seven of marriage relations, such as mothers, daughters, sisters, aunts, and nieces. Foster relations also establish marriage prohibitions, similar to blood relations, with suckling before the age of two being the determining factor. The verse further prohibits marriage to a wife’s mother, stepdaughters under guardianship, and the wives of one’s biological sons. It also forbids taking two sisters as wives simultaneously, with the exception of what occurred during the time of Jahiliyyah, which was forgiven. This prohibition extends even to adopted sons, as illustrated by the Prophet’s marriage to the former wife of his adopted son, Zayd.
