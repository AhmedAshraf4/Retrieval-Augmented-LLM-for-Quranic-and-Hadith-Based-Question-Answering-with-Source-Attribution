Tafsir Explanation: Allah forbids His believing servants from engaging in Riba (interest), as was common during the time of Jahiliyyah, where creditors would demand interest when a debtor sought deferment. Allah commands His followers to have Taqwa (piety) to attain success in this life and the Hereafter and warns them of the Fire prepared for the disbelievers. He encourages hastening towards forgiveness and Paradise, which is vast as the heavens and the earth. This Paradise is described as spacious and is understood to be above the heavens, under the Throne, while the Fire is positioned in the lowest realms. The believers are those who spend in Allah's cause, repress their anger, and forgive others. Allah loves those who do good, and those who control their rage and forgive will be rewarded with special blessings. When a believer wrongs themselves, they remember Allah and seek His forgiveness, with Allah promising to pardon those who sincerely repent.
