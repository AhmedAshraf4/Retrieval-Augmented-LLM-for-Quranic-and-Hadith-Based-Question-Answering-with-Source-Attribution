Allah presents the hypocrites as people who exchanged guidance for misguidance and were left in utter loss, likening them to a person who kindled a fire that briefly illuminated the surroundings and brought safety, then was suddenly extinguished, leaving him trapped in complete darkness with no ability to see a path. The removal of the light represents the removal of what benefited them, while the remaining darkness represents doubt, disbelief, and hypocrisy that harm them. Their state is described as deaf, dumb, and blind: deaf because they cannot hear guidance, dumb because they cannot speak words that would benefit them, and blind because they cannot recognize the correct way even if it were shown. This condition results from their initial belief followed by disbelief, since they abandoned guidance after possessing it. Because they deliberately traded guidance for deviation, they are unable to return to the state of clarity and direction they once had.
