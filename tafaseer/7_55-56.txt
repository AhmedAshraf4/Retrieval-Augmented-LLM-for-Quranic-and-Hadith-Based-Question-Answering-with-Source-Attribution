Allah commands His servants to supplicate to Him with humility and sincerity, seeking His help in both this life and the Hereafter. Supplication should be made quietly, with a humble heart, and with certainty of Allah's Oneness. Allah prohibits raising one's voice in supplication to show off, as He is close and always hears. Aggression in supplication, such as asking for something beyond one's due, like the rank of Prophets, is forbidden. Allah also forbids causing corruption on Earth after it has been set in order, as mischief harms people when affairs are otherwise in order. He instructs His servants to invoke Him with both fear of His punishment and hope for His reward. Allah’s mercy is near to those who do good, and His mercy encompasses all things, especially for those who fear Him and obey His commands.
