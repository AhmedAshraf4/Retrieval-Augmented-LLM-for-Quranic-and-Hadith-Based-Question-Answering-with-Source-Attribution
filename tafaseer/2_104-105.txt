Believers were forbidden to use a phrase meaning “listen to us” because Jews deliberately twisted it to convey insult while pretending respect, using ambiguous speech to mock the Prophet. Some Jews also greeted Muslims with words that outwardly sounded like peace but actually meant death, showing deliberate verbal deception. Because of this practice, believers were commanded to use a clear phrase meaning “make us understand” and to listen attentively, avoiding any wording that could be distorted or imitated from hostile speech. This prohibition prevented adopting the language and behavior of disbelievers whose intent was mockery and opposition. The next statement clarifies the cause: disbelievers from the People of the Scripture and the idolaters did not wish any good or revelation to be given to the believers. Despite their hostility, God selects whom He wills for mercy and grants favor by giving guidance and revelation to the believers.
