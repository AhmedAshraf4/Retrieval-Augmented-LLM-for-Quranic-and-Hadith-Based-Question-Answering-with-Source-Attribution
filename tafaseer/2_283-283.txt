Tafsir Explanation: If someone is traveling and cannot find a scribe to record a debt, a pledge (mortgage) should be taken from the borrower in place of writing the transaction. The Prophet Muhammad’s shield was mortgaged to a Jew in exchange for barley to provide for his household. If one person entrusts another with a loan, the entrusted person must discharge the trust faithfully. If there is mutual trust, it is not necessary to have a written document or witnesses. The debtor should be conscious of Allah and fulfill their obligation. Concealing evidence or giving false testimony is a serious sin, and those who hide testimony will face a sinful heart. This is reinforced by the Qur'an’s command to stand for justice and truth, even against oneself or close relatives, and not to follow personal desires in testimony.
