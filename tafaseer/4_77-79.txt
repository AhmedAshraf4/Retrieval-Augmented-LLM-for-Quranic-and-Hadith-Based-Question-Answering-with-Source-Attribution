Tafsir Explanation: In the early years of Islam, Muslims in Makkah were instructed to pray, give charity, forgive the idolators, and practice patience. They eagerly awaited the time when they could fight, but the circumstances, including their small numbers and the sanctity of Makkah, did not allow for warfare. When the Muslims established themselves in Madinah and gained strength, the command for Jihad was revealed. However, when the time came to fight, some Muslims hesitated, wishing the battle had been delayed to avoid bloodshed and loss. Allah responded, reminding them that death will inevitably reach everyone, regardless of their efforts to avoid it. The verse also addresses the hypocrisy of those who attribute good fortune to Allah and misfortune to the Prophet, revealing that all events, good or bad, are decreed by Allah. The verse emphasizes that any harm that befalls individuals is a consequence of their own actions, while the good is from Allah’s mercy. The Prophet was sent as a messenger to guide people, and Allah alone is the witness to his mission and the responses of the people.
