The event occurred when the Prophet sent Abdullah bin Jahsh and eight companions to observe a Quraysh caravan near Nakhlah. Abdullah was instructed not to force anyone to join, but two men turned back while the rest continued. They encountered the caravan, and Waqid bin Abdullah killed Amr bin Hadrami, causing the Quraysh to accuse the Muslims of violating the sanctity of the Sacred Month. Allah revealed that fighting in the Sacred Months is a great transgression, but greater is preventing people from the path of Allah, disbelief, and expelling people from the Sacred Mosque. The Muslims, criticized by some and defended by others, returned to the Prophet with the caravan and prisoners. Despite the criticism, the Prophet accepted the ransom of the prisoners only after confirming the safety of two of his companions, Sa’d bin Abu Waqqas and Utbah bin Ghazwan. Later, Allah reassured the Muslims through the revelation that those who believe and strive for Allah’s cause will receive His mercy.
