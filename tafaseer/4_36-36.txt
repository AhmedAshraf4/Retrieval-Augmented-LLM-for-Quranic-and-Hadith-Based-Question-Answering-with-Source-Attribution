Allah commands the worship of Him alone, as He is the Creator and Sustainer of all. The Prophet Muhammad emphasized that the right of Allah on His servants is to worship Him without associating any partners, and in return, Allah will not punish those who fulfill this duty. Allah also commands dutifulness to parents, as they are the reason for one's existence. He connects the worship of Allah with being good to parents in many places, also instructing kindness toward relatives, orphans, the poor, and neighbors. Allah orders the treatment of orphans with compassion and the poor with sufficient support to meet their needs. Neighbors, whether relatives or strangers, must be treated with kindness, as emphasized by the Prophet’s numerous sayings about their rights. Additionally, Allah calls for the kind treatment of slaves, who should be fed, clothed, and only required to do what they can bear. Arrogance and boasting are condemned, as Allah does not love those who display such traits, particularly those who are ungrateful for His blessings and who look down upon others.
