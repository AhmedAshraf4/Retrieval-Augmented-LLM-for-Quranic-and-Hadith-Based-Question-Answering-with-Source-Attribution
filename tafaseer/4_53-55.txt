Allah rebukes the Jews for their greed and envy, stating that if they had any share in dominion, they would withhold even the smallest thing, like a speck on the back of a date-stone. This highlights their miserly nature. Allah points out their envy towards the Prophet Muhammad for the prophethood given to him, despite it being a divine favor, as they believed themselves more deserving. Allah reminds them that the family of Ibrahim received the Book, wisdom, and a great kingdom, with some among them believing and others rejecting. Despite these blessings, many of them disputed with their own Prophets, and thus their rejection of Muhammad, who was not from the Children of Israel, is even more grievous. Allah warns them of Hell as a just punishment for their disbelief and defiance.
