Allah prohibits Khamr (intoxicants) and Maysir (gambling), considering them as abominations of Shaytan’s handiwork. These are practices that lead to enmity and hatred between people and hinder them from the remembrance of Allah and from prayer. The prohibition was revealed in stages, starting with a recognition of the harm in intoxicants and gambling, followed by a clearer restriction, and ultimately a firm command to avoid them entirely. The prohibition also extends to related practices like the use of altar stones (Ansab) for sacrifices and arrows (Azlam) for decision-making. The believers are urged to stay away from these evils for success. When intoxicants were fully prohibited, the companions, including Abu Talhah and Anas, responded by spilling out their alcohol, which had been made from dates, and stopped consuming it. The prohibition was a clear command, and those who died while indulging in these acts before the prohibition are not held accountable for their past actions if they had sincere faith and righteousness in their lives thereafter. This was clarified by the verse stating that those who believe and do righteous deeds are not blamed for what they ate, as long as they maintain faith and perform good deeds, and they are loved by Allah.
