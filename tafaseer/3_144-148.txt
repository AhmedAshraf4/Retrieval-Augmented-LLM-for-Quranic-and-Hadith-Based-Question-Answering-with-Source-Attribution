Tafsir Explanation: After the defeat at Uhud, rumors spread that the Prophet Muhammad had been killed, weakening the resolve of some Muslims. Shaytan fueled this by claiming Muhammad's death, and some Muslims believed it, thinking it was inevitable, as other Prophets had faced similar fates. To counter this, Allah revealed that Muhammad was merely a Messenger, and like other Messengers before him, could be killed in fulfilling his mission. When the Prophet died, Abu Bakr reminded the believers of this, urging them to remain steadfast, as worship of Allah continues regardless of Muhammad's death. Allah affirmed that no one dies except by His decree at an appointed time, urging believers to continue their struggle in His cause without fear. Those who seek worldly gains will only receive what Allah has decreed, while those who seek the Hereafter will be rewarded accordingly. Allah also reassured the believers, reminding them that many Prophets and their followers endured hardships but persevered, and they, too, would be rewarded for their patience.
