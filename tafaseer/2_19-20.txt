The verse presents a parable of hesitant hypocrites whose faith alternates between clarity and confusion, likened to people caught in a rainstorm with darkness, thunder, and lightning. The rain represents revelation descending amid disbelief and hypocrisy, the darkness represents doubt and confusion, the thunder represents fear that shakes their hearts, and the lightning represents brief flashes of faith that almost overwhelm their weak perception. Out of fear of destruction, they block their ears against the thunder, yet this caution does not protect them because divine control fully surrounds them. When the lightning flashes, they move forward, meaning they align with faith and speak truth when Islam brings benefit or victory, but when darkness returns, they stop, meaning they withdraw into doubt or disbelief when trials or losses occur. The lightning nearly snatching their sight indicates that the clarity of revelation exposes their inner reality while exceeding their capacity to remain firm. If God willed, He could remove their hearing and sight completely because they abandoned the truth after recognizing it. This parable describes a category of hypocrites whose belief appears and disappears, distinct from pure hypocrites, and it corresponds to their final state when light is given according to faith and withdrawn from hypocrisy, leaving them unable to proceed when the light is extinguished.
