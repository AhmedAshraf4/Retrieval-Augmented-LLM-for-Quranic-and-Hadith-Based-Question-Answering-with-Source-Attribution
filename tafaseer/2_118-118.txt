The disbelievers said, "Why does not Allah speak to us or show us a sign?" This statement mirrors what previous peoples, including the Jews and Christians, said. The Arab idolaters demanded extraordinary proofs, such as seeing a spring gush from the earth or receiving angels, just as past nations asked Moses to show Allah openly. Their hearts displayed the same disbelief, stubbornness, and injustice as those before them, accusing Messengers of being sorcerers or madmen. Allah made His signs clear for those who believe with certainty, but for those whose hearts, hearing, and sight are sealed, no sign will lead them to faith; they will only believe when they face the painful torment.
