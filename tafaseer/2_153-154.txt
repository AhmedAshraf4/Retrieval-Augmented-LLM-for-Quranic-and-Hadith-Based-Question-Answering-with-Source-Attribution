Allah commands patience and prayer to help the servant in both bounty and calamity. If a believer receives a blessing, gratitude benefits him; if he faces hardship, patience benefits him. Patience includes avoiding sins, performing acts of worship, and enduring afflictions, with obedience carrying greater reward. Those who practice these forms of patience are among the truly patient. Martyrs who are killed in the way of Allah are alive, not dead, and their souls inhabit green birds that move freely in Paradise before taking refuge in lamps under the Throne. Allah asks them what they desire, and they request to return to earthly life to fight and be killed again, but Allah decrees they will not return. The soul of every believer, in general, is like a bird feeding on Paradise’s trees until Allah reunites it with the body on resurrection.
