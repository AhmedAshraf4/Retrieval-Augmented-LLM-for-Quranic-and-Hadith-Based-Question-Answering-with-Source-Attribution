Allah stated that the Prophet will not be questioned about the dwellers of the blazing Fire, meaning he is only responsible for conveying the message, not for the rejection or disbelief of others. His duty is to remind people, not to force them into belief. The Qur'an warns those who fear Allah’s threat, while Allah knows best what people say. The Prophet is described in the Torah with the same characteristics as in the Qur'an: he is a witness, bringer of good news, warner, and safe refuge for the unlettered. He relies on Allah, is gentle in dealings, does not repay evil with evil, forgives, and will continue his mission until the wicked’s religion is straightened and people acknowledge Allah’s oneness. Through him, Allah opens blind eyes, deaf ears, and sealed hearts.
