Allah forbids making oaths to abandon good deeds or sever family ties, as fulfilling such vows is more sinful than breaking them with expiation. The Messenger of Allah emphasized that breaking such vows by paying the required Kaffarah is preferable to continuing in the sin of cutting off relations with family. The majority view holds that if a better action appears, one should break the vow, pay Kaffarah, and choose the better act. Unintentional vows, like habitual oaths or those made in anger, are not punishable, but deliberate lies or vows to break Allah’s commands require accountability. Allah will hold people accountable for deliberate oaths and actions that come from the heart, while He remains Most Forgiving and Forbearing.
