Allah saved Lot and his family, as only his household believed in him. His wife, however, did not believe and conspired with the people of her community, informing them of the visitors to Lot’s house. When Lot was commanded to leave at night with his family, he was instructed not to tell his wife or take her with him. Some say she followed them, but when the torment struck, she looked back and was punished like the others. However, it is more apparent that she did not leave the city and remained with her people. Allah's statement "except his wife; she was of the Ghabirin" refers to those who remained behind or were destroyed. The people of Lot were destroyed when stones of baked clay rained down on them in a well-arranged manner, marked from Allah. The end of the criminals was a clear lesson for others who disobeyed and rejected Allah’s messengers.
