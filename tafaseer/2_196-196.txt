Tafsir Explanation: Allah commands the completion of Hajj and Umrah, meaning to finish the rituals once started. If a person is prevented from completing them, they must sacrifice an animal, such as a sheep, cow, or camel, depending on what they can afford. This occurred during the Treaty of Al-Hudaybiyyah when the Prophet and his companions were blocked from entering Makkah. They slaughtered camels, shaved their heads, and ended their Ihram outside the Sacred Area. The requirement for the sacrifice applies to those who cannot complete their pilgrimage, with the sacrifice symbolizing the act of compensating for the incomplete rituals. Those who are unable to sacrifice may fast ten days—three during Hajj and seven upon returning home. The residents of Makkah are excluded from performing Tamattu, a type of pilgrimage that involves performing `Umrah before Hajj. This ruling emphasizes the need for adherence to the prescribed rituals, with a clear warning of Allah’s severe punishment for disobedience.
