Tafsir Explanation: The verse addresses the Jews and Christians who claimed purity for themselves, asserting that they were Allah's chosen people and would be the only ones to enter Paradise. Allah refutes this, stating that He alone sanctifies whom He wills and will not wrong anyone, even by the weight of a thread. The Jews falsely invented lies about themselves, claiming Allah's special favor, while relying on their forefathers' deeds to secure their salvation. Allah clarifies that each person is responsible for their own actions. The verse also condemns their belief in Jibt (sorcery or idols) and Taghut (Satan or false worship), further mocking their misguided faith. It recounts how the Jews claimed the disbelievers were better guided than the believers, favoring them due to ignorance and disbelief in the Prophet Muhammad. The verse culminates with a curse on the Jews, highlighting their alliance with idolaters and their defeat during the Battle of the Confederates (Al-Ahzab), where they gained no advantage, and Allah protected the believers.
