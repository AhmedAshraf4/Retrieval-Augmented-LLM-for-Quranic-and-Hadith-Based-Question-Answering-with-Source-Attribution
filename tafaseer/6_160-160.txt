This verse emphasizes the multiplication of rewards for good deeds and the equal recompense for sins. A good deed, when performed, is rewarded with at least ten times its value, and can be multiplied further up to seven hundred times or more. If someone intends a good deed but does not carry it out, they are still rewarded for the intention. In contrast, if a person intends to commit a sin but refrains, they are credited with a good deed. If they commit the sin, it is counted as one sin, unless Allah forgives it. The verse illustrates that those who intend to commit sin but do not act on it due to fear of Allah will be rewarded with a good deed. However, those who are prevented by forgetfulness or other distractions earn neither a reward nor a sin. Those who fail due to laziness or inability to act, despite intent, are seen as equivalent to those who commit the sin. The verse also highlights that performing acts like fasting three days each month or engaging in prayers like Friday prayers brings immense rewards, such as having the sins between two Fridays or months forgiven.
