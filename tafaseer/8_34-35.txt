The idolators were deserving of Allah's punishment due to their disbelieving actions, but Allah withheld torment during the Prophet's presence among them. When the Prophet migrated, Allah sent His punishment upon them, resulting in the death or capture of their leaders during the Battle of Badr. This delay in punishment was due to the weak Muslims among them who invoked Allah’s forgiveness. If it had not been for these oppressed believers, the torment would have been unavoidable. The idolators hindered believers from reaching the Sacred Mosque, and their actions were a greater transgression than merely disbelief, as they were not the rightful guardians of the mosque. The true guardians were those who had Taqwa, such as the Prophet and his Companions. The idolators’ acts of worship, including whistling and clapping around the Ka'bah, were invalid and disrespectful. Allah’s punishment for their disbelief and hindrance came in the form of death and capture during Badr.
