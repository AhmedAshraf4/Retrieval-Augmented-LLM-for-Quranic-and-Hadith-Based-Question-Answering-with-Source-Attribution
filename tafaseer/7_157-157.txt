Tafsir Explanation: The Prophet Muhammad is described in previous scriptures, such as the Torah and the Gospel, as an unlettered Messenger who would guide people to worship Allah alone and forbid evil. His advent was foretold and his qualities were known to the rabbis and priests, who were aware of his arrival. A Jewish man, whose son was dying, recognized the Prophet's description in the Torah, confirmed his prophethood, and declared his faith. The Prophet personally led the funeral prayer for the boy. The description of the Prophet in the Torah aligns with what is found in the Qur'an, where he is portrayed as a merciful, forgiving Messenger, tasked with commanding good and forbidding evil. He came to ease the burdens on his followers, making their religion simple and forgiving, as seen in the Prophet's teaching that Allah forgives unintentional errors, forgetfulness, and coerced actions. Those who believe in him, honor him, help him, and follow the guidance revealed to him, will be successful in this life and the Hereafter.
