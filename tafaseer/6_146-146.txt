Allah prohibited for the Jews certain foods as a consequence of their transgression, including every bird and animal with undivided hooves, such as camels, ostriches, ducks, and geese. He also forbade them the fat of oxen and sheep, except for the fat on their backs and that which clings to the entrails or is mixed with bones. This restriction was imposed as a punishment for their defiance of Allah's commandments. The Jews had previously claimed that the prohibition was based on what Israel (Jacob) had forbidden for himself, but Allah clarified that the restriction was His decree, not merely an imitation of Israel's actions. Additionally, when the Jews melted the forbidden fats to sell them, they were further cursed for this deception. This violation was condemned in a hadith, which describes how they sold the fats they had melted, disregarding the prohibition.
