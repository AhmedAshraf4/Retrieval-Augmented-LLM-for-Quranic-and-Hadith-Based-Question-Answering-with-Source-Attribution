The verse affirms that Allah alone possesses absolute sovereignty on the Day of Recompense, a day singled out because no created being will claim ownership or authority over anything, and no soul will speak except by His permission. On that day, all voices are subdued, angels stand in ranks, and every person is judged only by Allah’s leave. The Day of Recompense is the Day of Judgment, when Allah reckons the creation for their deeds, repaying evil with evil and good with good, except for those He pardons. Allah is the true King and Owner of all things, and His exclusive kingship becomes fully manifest when He seizes the earth, folds the heavens, and declares His rule while all worldly kings, tyrants, and the arrogant are rendered powerless. Any use of the term “king” for created beings in worldly life is figurative and temporary, unlike Allah’s real ownership. The term “Recompense” denotes the final reckoning, reward, or punishment, when every deed is fully repaid and nothing is hidden.
