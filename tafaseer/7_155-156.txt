Tafsir Explanation: Musa chose seventy men from the Children of Israel to go with him to an appointed meeting place to repent for the worship of the calf. At the designated site, they asked to see Allah directly, despite hearing His commands to Musa through a cloud. When Allah spoke to Musa, the people demanded to witness the divine presence, but this request led to their destruction by a lightning strike or tremor. Musa cried out to Allah, asking why they were destroyed, as they were foolish, and why the rest of the Children of Israel should suffer. He acknowledged that the decision was in Allah's hands, who is the sole ruler and source of mercy. Musa then prayed for forgiveness, mercy, and blessings for his people, asking Allah to ordain good for them in this life and the next. Allah responded by affirming that His punishment affects whom He wills, while His mercy encompasses all things, extending to those who have Taqwa, give Zakah, and believe in His signs.
