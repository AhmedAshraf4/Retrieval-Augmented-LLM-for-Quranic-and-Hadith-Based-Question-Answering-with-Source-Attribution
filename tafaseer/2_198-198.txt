


Tafsir Explanation: During the time of Jahiliyyah, trading was avoided during the Hajj season, as it was seen as incompatible with the sacredness of the pilgrimage. However, the Qur'an clarified that there is no sin in seeking the bounty of Allah through trade during Hajj. This ruling allowed for business transactions without compromising the rituals of pilgrimage. The Prophet confirmed this by referencing the verse when asked about trade during Hajj, noting that those who performed the core rituals of the pilgrimage—like Tawaf, standing at Arafat, and throwing pebbles—would have a valid Hajj, regardless of engaging in trade. Additionally, Arafat is emphasized as a key place for standing during Hajj, marking the time to remember Allah, and the surrounding area of Al-Muzdalifah is designated as Al-Mash`ar Al-Haram, where Muslims are to glorify Allah. The verse reminds Muslims of the guidance they received, having previously been astray before the revelation of the Qur'an and the teachings of Prophet Muhammad.
