Allah knows the good deeds of His creation, including charity and vows, and rewards them generously if done seeking His Face. Those who disobey Him, reject His revelation, and worship others will have no helpers to save them from His anger on the Day of Resurrection. Disclosing charity is permissible, but concealing it is better as it avoids showing off, though if there is a clear benefit in making it known, such as encouraging others to give, it becomes better to disclose. The Prophet Muhammad said that disclosing charity is like openly reciting the Qur'an, while concealing it is like silent recitation. Concealed charity is more virtuous, as seen in the Hadith about seven people whom Allah will give His shade on the Day of Judgment, including one who gives charity secretly so that their left hand does not know what the right hand has given. Giving charity in secret expiates sins, raises one’s rank, and earns Allah’s reward, as He is fully aware of all deeds.
