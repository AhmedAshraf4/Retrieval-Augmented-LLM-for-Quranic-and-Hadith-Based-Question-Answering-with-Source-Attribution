Allah distinguishes the Muhajirin, who left their homes and wealth to support Allah and His Messenger, from the Ansar, the Muslims of Madinah, who provided shelter and aid to the Muhajirin. The Muhajirin and Ansar are allies to one another, with the Muhajirin having more right to the Ansar than anyone else. The Prophet Muhammad established a bond of brotherhood between them, even allowing them to inherit from each other until it was later abrogated. Allah praised both groups, highlighting their selflessness and support for the cause of Islam. In contrast, believers who did not emigrate are not entitled to protection or share in the war booty unless they participate in battle. When non-emigrants seek assistance in religious matters, it is the duty of the Muslims to aid them, except when this involves betraying existing peace treaties.
