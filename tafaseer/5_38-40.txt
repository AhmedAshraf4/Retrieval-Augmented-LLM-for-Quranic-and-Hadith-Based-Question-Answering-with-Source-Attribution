Tafsir Explanation: Allah decreed that the hand of a thief, male or female, should be cut off as punishment, a practice upheld from the time of Jahiliyyah. This punishment is only applied under certain conditions, including the theft of a minimum value, set as a quarter of a Dinar or three Dirhams. This measure aims to deter theft, and the hand is severed as a consequence for violating the rights of others. The punishment applies even if the thief steals something as insignificant as an egg or rope, as stated in hadiths. The punishment is part of Allah’s wisdom and power. However, if the thief repents and performs righteous deeds, Allah will forgive him. An example of this is a woman from the Makhzum tribe who had her hand cut off after stealing during the time of the Prophet Muhammad. When she repented, the Prophet declared her sin absolved, emphasizing that sincere repentance can lead to forgiveness.
